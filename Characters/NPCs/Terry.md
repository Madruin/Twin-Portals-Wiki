# Terry

An intellect devourer who became one of [[SWOJ]]'s most loyal and beloved companions. Originally posing as the party's Zuber (carriage taxi) driver in Waterdeep, Terry's true nature was revealed when his host body was murdered -- and rather than treating him as a monster, SWOJ accepted him as a friend. Over the course of both seasons, Terry inhabited a series of increasingly memorable bodies, evolved from a dependent creature into an independent hero, and ultimately became the first official member of the SWOJ guild beyond the four founders.

**First appearance:** [[S01E04 - Episode Four]]
**Total appearances:** 17 episodes

## Description

Terry is an **intellect devourer** -- a brain-shaped creature with legs, typically created by mind flayers to serve as parasitic infiltrators. Terry was originally created by [[Nihiloor]], the mind flayer working for the [[Xanathar Guild]], but defected to SWOJ's side after forming a genuine bond with the party.

Terry communicates exclusively through **telepathy**, projecting his thoughts directly into the minds of those nearby. His personality is earnest, loyal, and often unintentionally comedic -- a brain creature navigating the social complexities of the Sword Coast with more heart than most humanoids.

### Bodies Inhabited

Terry's arc is partly defined by the succession of bodies he has occupied:

1. **The Zuber Driver** (S01E04-S01E06) -- Terry's first known host, a human carriage driver who served as SWOJ's regular transportation around Waterdeep. Found murdered at the base of the God Catcher statue, run through with a blade.
2. **A Dead Body** (S01E07) -- After being revealed as an intellect devourer when his host died, Terry was temporarily placed into a dead body. SWOJ gave him a SWOJ t-shirt as a gesture of acceptance.
3. **A One-Eyed Golden Retriever** (S01E09) -- Terry inhabited the corpse of a golden retriever with one eye. In this form, he played with the Cassalanter children outside the villa and tried to get help during the attic fight.
4. **A Dead Cultist's Body** (S01E10-S01E11) -- Terry obtained a new human body from a dead cultist found after the Cassalanter villa incident. He struggled without adequate brain sustenance and desperately ate an alley cat's brain to survive.
5. **A Horse** (S01E13 onward) -- Terry's most iconic and enduring form. As a horse, he pulled the SWOJ-mobile, burst into courtrooms to stomp enemy intellect devourers, carried the party across the city, and eventually became a legend in his own right. By the Season 2 finale, he had been **awakened** and upgraded with daggers, armor, a SWOJ hauberk, and faintly glowing yellow eyes. He could throw daggers from his hooves and arrived dramatically via a summoned lightning bolt.

## Role in the Story

### Season 1: From Zuber Driver to War Horse

- **S01E04:** Terry is introduced as SWOJ's regular Zuber (carriage taxi) driver in Waterdeep
- **S01E05:** Continues as the party's regular Zuber driver
- **S01E06:** Terry is found murdered at the base of the God Catcher statue, run through with a blade
- **S01E07:** Terry's true nature is revealed -- he is an intellect devourer who had been inhabiting the Zuber driver's body; placed into a dead body temporarily; given a SWOJ t-shirt
- **S01E09:** Terry now inhabits a one-eyed golden retriever corpse; left outside the Cassalanter villa with guards; plays with the Cassalanter children; tries to get help during the attic fight
- **S01E10:** Terry receives a new human body from a dead cultist found after the Cassalanter villa incident; still struggles without adequate brain sustenance
- **S01E11:** Terry desperately eats an alley cat's brain for sustenance; [[Janks]] suggests [[Hrabbaz]] (Gralhund bodyguard) as a potential body for Terry
- **S01E13:** Now inhabiting a horse body, Terry arrives with the SWOJ-mobile to assist in the pursuit through Trollskull Alley
- **S01E15:** Terry the horse bursts into the courtroom during the trial chaos and stomps enemy intellect devourers; follows SWOJ's carriage through the city in a comically un-stealthy manner
- **S01E19:** Terry is mentioned in passing
- **S01E22:** The party reunites with Taga, Leaf, and Terry at the Short Rest tavern upon returning from the Feywild
- **S01E24:** SWOJ contacts Terry via sending stone; he reports being at the "Kooky Cobble"
- **S01E25:** Terry the telepathic horse communicates with the party
- **S01E26:** Terry arrives at [[Remalia Haventree|Remalia]]'s house with *pass without trace* ability, helping the party move stealthily
- **S01E27:** Terry telepathically announces incoming reinforcements; brings [[Mellafarne]] and [[Aurinax]] (dragons) to the Cassalanter Villa battle, communicating telepathically with [[Mr. E]] and SWOJ
- **S01E29:** In the aftermath of Season 1, Terry has become a **celebrity and independent vigilante figure** in Waterdeep, spending time at [[Vincent Trench]]'s detective office and developing his own legend

### Season 2: The Return of the Horse

- **S02E38:** Terry returns for the Season 2 finale, now dramatically upgraded -- an **awakened horse** equipped with daggers, armor, a SWOJ hauberk, and faintly glowing yellow eyes; can throw daggers from his hooves; arrives via a summoned lightning bolt triggered by waving his old business card; leaps across rooftops to transport the party to the [[Masked Lords]]; invited as the **first official member** of the SWOJ guild beyond the four founders

## Relationships

- **[[SWOJ]]** -- Adopted companion and first official guild member; the party accepted him despite his monstrous nature
- **[[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]** -- Fellow frontline fighter; Terry's horse-form courtroom entrance to stomp intellect devourers mirrored Scoot's brute-force approach
- **[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]** -- Connected via sending stone; Lark could contact Terry for coordination
- **[[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]** -- Communicates telepathically with the party
- **[[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]** -- Fellow party member
- **[[Nihiloor]]** -- Terry's creator, a mind flayer working for the Xanathar Guild; Terry defected from his control
- **[[Mellafarne]]** -- Bronze dragon brought by Terry as reinforcements to the Cassalanter Villa battle
- **[[Aurinax]]** -- Gold dragon brought by Terry as reinforcements
- **[[Vincent Trench]]** -- Terry spent time at his detective office during his vigilante phase
- **[[Janks]]** -- Suggested potential bodies for Terry; one of the Trollskull Alley Irregulars who took the sending stone from Terry's body at one point
- **[[Cassalanter Children]]** -- Played with Terry in his golden retriever form outside the villa

## Notable Items

- **[[SWOJ T-Shirt]]** -- Given to Terry as a symbol of acceptance when his true nature was first revealed
- **[[Terry's Business Card]]** -- An old card from Terry the horse; when waved in the air, a lightning bolt strikes nearby and Terry appears
- **[[Sending Stones]]** -- One held by Lark, the other by Terry/Janks; used for short-range communication

## Trivia

- Terry is the only intellect devourer in known D&D lore to become a heroic figure and guild member
- His horse form became so iconic that he kept it for the majority of the campaign
- The name "Terry" was originally just the Zuber driver's name, but it stuck through every body he inhabited
- His vigilante career in Waterdeep during the time-skip between seasons suggests he became a folk hero in his own right
- Terry's arrival at the Season 2 finale -- via lightning bolt summon, armored and armed -- is one of the most dramatic character entrances in the series
