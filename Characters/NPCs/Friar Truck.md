# Friar Truck

> *"Manshoon was always the better leader. I'll prove it to you."*
> -- Friar Truck to [[SWOJ]], in the phylactery laboratory beneath [[Zhentil Keep]] ([[S02E23 - Episode Fifty Three (S02E23)|S02E23]])

**First appearance:** [[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]
**Death:** [[S02E23 - Episode Fifty Three (S02E23)|S02E23]] (killed by [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]'s moonbeam)
**Total appearances:** 7 episodes
**Faction affiliations:** [[The Jolly Gents]]

---

## Description

Friar Truck was a demonic cleric and the hidden architect behind the corruption of [[The Jolly Gents]]. While he ostensibly served under [[Rowan Crooks]], Truck was in fact the original manipulator who orchestrated the real Rowan Crooks' downfall and replacement by the 14th [[Manshoon]] clone. He served a patron he called the "Lord of Nexus" and operated the phylactery laboratory beneath [[Zhentil Keep]], where he maintained over a hundred phylacteries containing the bound souls of Jolly Gents members.

Truck wielded a mace he called "the Truck" and carried a dark spellbook covered in blood magic and pentagrams. In combat he proved a formidable opponent: immune to fear effects, capable of casting spirit guardians (which manifested as ghostly Jolly Gents), banishment, flame strike, wall of fire, and an invisibility spell that could veil multiple allies at once. His spirit guardians dealt necrotic damage within a 15-foot radius, and the arcane altar in his laboratory provided him with a protective shielding aura.

Despite his zealous loyalty to Manshoon -- whom he openly declared "the better leader" over the original Crooks -- Truck presented himself as a helpful healer when the Jolly Gents first allied with [[SWOJ]]. He healed the wound on [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s head after [[Rowan Crooks]] extracted the [[Gem of Seeing]] that the Necromancer had implanted, and he used smelling salts and healing magic to revive Lark afterward. This early act of apparent kindness concealed his true nature as the conspiracy's mastermind.

When finally killed by [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]'s third-level moonbeam dealing 28 radiant damage, Truck did not revert to any alternate form -- he was simply a "demonic human dude" whose fiery aura was replaced by ghostly flames of searing pain before he collapsed.

## Relationships

- **[[Rowan Crooks]]** -- Truck served the Manshoon clone who had replaced the real Rowan Crooks, but he was not merely a subordinate. Truck's private notes revealed that he was the one who manipulated the original Crooks into staging a doomed assault on [[Zhentil Keep]], leading to his downfall and replacement. Truck was the conspiracy's true architect.
- **[[Manshoon]]** -- Truck served Manshoon with zealous devotion, declaring him "the better leader" over the original Crooks. He invoked the "Lord of Nexus" in battle, suggesting a connection to Manshoon's network of power. His laboratory work maintaining phylacteries and clone tanks directly served Manshoon's ambitions.
- **[[Little Joan]]** -- Joan fought alongside Truck during the betrayal at the [[The Breathless Elder|Breathless Elder]], standing over him during the combat. She later revealed that Truck had been "influencing some of the souls of the dead Gents to return more docile, more subservient" -- describing his role in suppressing the free will of resurrected Jolly Gents members.
- **[[SWOJ]]** -- Truck initially posed as a healer and ally when the Jolly Gents arrived at [[Trollskull Manor]], but revealed himself as an enemy during the betrayal at the Breathless Elder. He was ultimately killed by the party in his own laboratory.

## The Phylactery Laboratory

Friar Truck's laboratory was hidden beneath [[Zhentil Keep]], accessible through the underground tunnels below the throne room. The laboratory contained:

- **Over a hundred phylacteries** lining the walls -- small metal objects including spheres, tubes, and boxes, all cold to the touch and faintly humming. Each contained the bound soul of a [[The Jolly Gents|Jolly Gent]].
- **A central arcane altar** flanked by four small pedestals, each holding a piece of glowing metal that powered protective magic. The altar shielded Truck and dampened offensive spells cast within its field (such as [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]'s faerie fire). When [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] shattered the altar, the protective current dissipated and Truck's banishment spell broke.
- **Clone tanks** containing [[Rowan Crooks]] clones in various stages of development. The clones grew from a central organ or brain, with simple weaponry capable of severing the body while the organism survived and regrew.
- **Truck's private workstation** with notes and logs detailing his manipulation of the original Crooks, the soul-harvesting operation, and Manshoon's plans for the [[Radiant Citadel]] and the [[Kelemvor's Wall Of The Faithless|Wall of the Faithless]].
- **An infernal spellbook** filled with blood magic rituals and pentagrams.

The laboratory served as the engine of the Jolly Gents' corruption. Truck used it to harvest and bind the souls of Gents members, then influenced those souls to return "more docile, more subservient" -- effectively stripping free will from the resurrected members and ensuring their obedience to Crooks.
