# Lif Lindgren

The ghost of a middle-aged elven bartender who haunts [[Trollskull Manor]]. Once a warning spirit trapped in a building full of dark history, Lif became one of the [[Silver Waves of Justice]]'s most enduring allies -- the silent, steadfast caretaker of the [[Short Rest Tavern (Trollskull Manor)|Short Rest]] who kept the tavern running through years of the party's absence.

**Race:** Elf (ghost)
**First appearance:** [[S01E05 - Episode Five|S01E05]]
**Total appearances:** 7+ episodes
**Location:** [[Trollskull Manor]] (bound to the building)

## Description

Lif is a spectral figure -- the ghost of a middle-aged elf who worked as a bartender at [[Trollskull Manor]] approximately 95 years before the events of the campaign. He communicates non-verbally: writing messages on surfaces, opening and closing doors with ghostly telekinesis, moving objects, preparing food, and using expressive gestures such as sign language, smiles, and eyebrow waggles. The party affectionately nicknamed him "Leafy."

Lif is bound to [[Trollskull Manor]] and cannot leave the building. Despite this limitation, he became indispensable to the household -- cooking meals, opening doors, fetching cooking implements from shelves, serving as a ghostly greeter and attendant, and helping manage the tavern alongside [[Tagga]] and the [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars]].

## History

### Life and Death

Lif Lindgren worked as a bartender at [[Trollskull Manor]] roughly 95 years before SWOJ's arrival. His diary, discovered in a hidden workshop in the manor's cellar, revealed that he had uncovered the terrible truth about [[Esther]], a hag who had run an orphanage in the building and preyed on children. [[Mordecai Gralhund]] helped Lif find work at the manor. Lif died under unspecified circumstances and remained as a ghost, trapped in the building alongside the hag's lingering evil.

### First Encounter (S01E05)

When the [[Silver Waves of Justice]] first visited [[Trollskull Manor]] after receiving the deed from [[Volothamp Geddarm|Volo]], Lif manifested by writing urgent warnings on surfaces: "closing time," "she is coming," and "please save the children." The party discovered three street urchins -- [[Nat]], [[Jenks]], and [[Squidly]] -- who had been living in the abandoned building, and found Lif's hidden workshop in the cellar containing his diary, personal effects, gold, a bartender's smock, and a magical short sword inscribed with the initials "M.G." (for [[Mordecai Gralhund]]).

When the children accidentally unearthed a glowing talisman -- a shriveled green hand clutching a heart -- the manor was pulled into a demi-plane and the hag [[Esther]] manifested with seven spectral children servants. SWOJ defeated Esther, freed the spectral children, and Lif appeared one final time with a smile before fading -- his warnings heeded, the hag destroyed, and the children saved.

### Becoming Part of the Household

Rather than passing on, Lif remained at [[Trollskull Manor]] as a friendly presence. By the next morning ([[S01E06 - Episode Six|S01E06]]), he had transformed from a tormented warning spirit into a domestic ally, cooking eggs for the party. When [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]'s mother [[Tagga]] arrived to help run the tavern ([[S01E07 - Episode Seven|S01E07]]), Lif immediately began collaborating with her -- opening cupboards, bringing down cooking implements from shelves, and assisting in the kitchen. Scoot introduced Taga to "Leafy," describing him as "employee of the month."

Lif settled into the role of ghostly caretaker with a personality that ranged from helpful to testy. When [[Pog Waggletop]] got overexcited about building a rookery on the roof ([[S01E08 - Episode Eight|S01E08]]), Lif told him to shut up -- one of the few times he communicated so bluntly and a sign of his familiar, if sometimes grumpy, cohabitation with the growing household.

### The Short Rest Era

As [[Trollskull Manor]] was renovated into the Short Rest tavern with a 1,000 gold loan from [[Renaer Neverember]], Lif became a fixture of the establishment. He served food when asked ([[S01E16 - Episode Sixteen|S01E16]]), kept watch over the building, and helped look after the [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars|Trollskull Alley Irregulars]] -- [[Nat]], [[Jenks]], and [[Squidly]] -- who grew up under the combined care of Lif, [[Tagga]], and SWOJ.

When SWOJ returned from the Feywild with [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s soul in a red hat ([[S01E22 - Episode Twenty Two|S01E22]]), Lif was among the allies waiting at the Short Rest to greet them, described simply as an "ally of SWOJ."

### The Three-Year Absence (S02E05)

When SWOJ returned to [[Waterdeep]] after three years lost in the [[The Shadowfell|Shadowfell]] ([[S02E05 - Episode Thirty Five (S02E05)|S02E05]]), Lif had been managing the Short Rest throughout their entire absence. He appeared before [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] as Scoot carried the unconscious [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] upstairs. Lif examined the four of them closely and guardedly at first -- then his jaw dropped, he held his arms wide, and he gave them an "I love you" in sign language, overjoyed to see them again. When Scoot said "I would hug you, but you don't have a body," Lif opened doors with his ghostly powers and helped get the exhausted party settled.

Scoot immediately asked Lif where [[Tagga]] and [[Pog Waggletop|Pog]] were, and Lif pointed upstairs toward Taga's bedroom. Later, when [[Grinda Garloth]] of the [[Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors]] was given permission to set up a teleportation circle in the basement, Lif nodded, magically opened the basement trapdoor, and phased through the floor to begin cleaning downstairs.

### Season Two Finale (S02E38)

In the Season 2 finale ([[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]]), Lif welcomed SWOJ home from their battle at the [[Radiant Citadel]]. As the party approached [[Trollskull Manor]] down the road, the front door opened and Lif's spectral figure appeared, welcoming them with a waggle of his eyebrows. He also delivered a letter from the [[Masked Lords' Palace|Masked Lords]] to [[Squidley]]. By this point, Lif was described as a "ghostly spectral attendant" -- a permanent, beloved part of the Trollskull household.

## Relationships

- **[[Silver Waves of Justice]]** -- SWOJ freed Lif from the hag's shadow and gave him purpose as the caretaker of the Short Rest. He remained their loyal ghostly attendant throughout the campaign.
- **[[Tagga]]** -- [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]'s mother and Lif's primary partner in running [[Trollskull Manor]]. They collaborated seamlessly from their first meeting, with Lif opening cupboards and fetching implements while Taga cooked.
- **[[Nat]], [[Jenks]], [[Squidly]]** -- The [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars]], whom Lif helped watch over as they grew up at the Short Rest. Both Nat's and Jenks's pages describe Lif as "the ghost of the Short Rest, who helped look after the tavern and the children."
- **[[Pog Waggletop]]** -- Fellow permanent resident of [[Trollskull Manor]]. Their relationship was familiar if sometimes testy -- Lif once told Pog to shut up when the goblin got overexcited about the rookery.
- **[[Mordecai Gralhund]]** -- Mordecai helped Lif find work at [[Trollskull Manor]] during his lifetime. Lif's hidden chest contained the [[Song Sword]] bearing Mordecai's initials "M.G."
- **[[Ylka Gralhund]]** -- Retrieved the [[Song Sword]] from Lif's hidden chest, forging a connection between Lif's past and Ylka's [[Gralhund Family|Gralhund]] heritage.
- **[[Esther]]** -- The spectral hag who haunted [[Trollskull Manor]]. Lif's diary revealed her history, and his warnings helped SWOJ defeat her.

## Key Items

- **[[Song Sword]]** -- A magical blue steel short sword with a gemstone pommel and brass plaque inscribed "M.G." for [[Mordecai Gralhund]]. Cold to the touch, deals cold damage, and plays a melody when struck. Found in Lif's hidden chest in the [[Trollskull Manor Cellar|cellar]]. Became [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]'s signature weapon.
- **Lif's Diary** -- A journal kept during his time as bartender at [[Trollskull Manor]], revealing the history of [[Esther]] the hag and the danger lurking in the building.
