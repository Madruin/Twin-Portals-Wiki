# Yagra

Half-orc woman and [[Zhentarim]] operative affiliated with the [[Doom Raiders]]. A business associate of [[Lark Wavesilver|Lark]], Yagra is an independent and capable fist-fighter who first appears brawling with tattooed thugs at the [[Yawning Portal]]. She also plays cello in the [[Doom Raiders]] band and later helps [[SWOJ]] infiltrate the Cassalanters' [[Founders Day]] feast.

**First appearance:** [[S01E01 - Episode One|S01E01]]
**Total appearances:** 4 episodes
**Faction affiliations:** [[Doom Raiders]], [[Zhentarim]]

## Description

Yagra is a half-orc woman with small tusks who fights with her bare fists. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] compares her appearance to a "granny weaver, but bigger." She is sober and serious compared to her [[Doom Raiders]] bandmates and is described as a cello virtuoso. Through [[Fel'Rekt Lafeen]], [[SWOJ]] learns that Yagra was the mysterious sender of the "Y" note that warned the party, linking her to [[Bregan D'Aerthe]] intelligence networks as well as the [[Zhentarim]].

## Role in the Story

- **[[S01E01 - Episode One|S01E01]]:** A bar fight erupts between Yagra and five tattooed thugs at the [[Yawning Portal]]; [[Lark Wavesilver|Lark]] identifies her as his business associate and [[SWOJ]] rushes to her defense, with Yagra fighting alongside them until [[Zhentarim]] crossbow agents and [[Durnan]] help end the brawl.
- **[[S01E07 - Episode Seven|S01E07]]:** [[Fel'Rekt Lafeen]] visits [[Trollskull Manor]] and reveals that Yagra was the one who sent the mysterious "Y" note to [[SWOJ]], connecting her to [[Bregan D'Aerthe]] intelligence.
- **[[S01E11 - Episode Eleven|S01E11]]:** [[Davil Starsong]] introduces Yagra at the [[Yawning Portal]] as a fellow [[Doom Raiders]] band member and cello player; [[SWOJ]] discusses whether she and Davil are jointly searching for the [[The Stone of Golorr]], and she slips away before the situation escalates.
- **[[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]]:** Yagra joins [[Davil Starsong]] and the [[Doom Raiders]] in helping [[SWOJ]] infiltrate the Cassalanters' [[Founders Day]] feast disguised as band members, remaining sober and serious while others carouse.
