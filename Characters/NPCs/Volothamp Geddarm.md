# Volothamp Geddarm

Full name **Volothamp Ignatius Geddarm**, better known as **Volo**. The famous chronicler, wizard, adventurer, and celebrity of the Sword Coast whose fateful hiring of four strangers to find his missing friend set the entire Twin Portals saga in motion. Foppish, mustachioed, and perpetually overdressed, Volo is equal parts brilliant author and shameless self-promoter -- a man whose generosity with property deeds far exceeds his generosity with actual gold.

**First appearance:** [[S01E01 - Episode One]]
**Total appearances:** 8 episodes (across 3 registry entries: "Volothamp Geddarm," "Volo," and "Volothamp 'Volo' Geddarm")

## Description

Volo is a foppish, mustachioed man who wears a fancy hat and scarf. He is a famous chronicler and wizard, known across the Sword Coast as the author of *[[Volo's Guide to Monsters]]* and other popular tomes. He carries himself with the confidence of a celebrity, prone to grand entrances, dramatic apologies, and self-aggrandizing storytelling. Despite his vanity, Volo is genuinely well-meaning and maintains real affection for his friends -- particularly [[Floon Blagmaar|Floon]], whose kidnapping drove him to hire the adventurers who would become [[SWOJ]].

Volo is notably better at offering creative payment than actual currency. When he could not afford to pay SWOJ the promised gold for rescuing Floon, he instead gave them the deed to [[Trollskull Manor]] -- a rundown estate in the North Ward that would become the party's headquarters, tavern, and spiritual home for the entire campaign.

## Role in the Story

### Season 1: The Quest Begins

- **S01E01:** Volo hires the party at the Yawning Portal to find his missing friend [[Floon Blagmaar]], described as having "more beauty than brains" with red hair; this quest launches the entire campaign
- **S01E04:** After SWOJ rescues Floon and [[Renaer Neverember|Renaer]], Volo gives the party the deed to [[Trollskull Manor]] as payment, unable to pay the promised gold
- **S01E05:** Volo arrives at the very end of the episode after the hag encounter is already resolved, apologizing for being late; he had sent a [[Volo's Paper Crane|paper crane message]] earlier saying he was delayed
- **S01E29:** Volo arrives at Trollskull seeking **5,000 gold** to fund his next book, *Volo's Book of Portals and Portents*; he leaves with the promise of [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s stories instead, beginning a friendly rivalry between the two storytellers

### Season 2: War Stories and Tall Tales

- **S02E01:** Volo is encountered at The Way Inn during the Feywild journey; he is later **captured and replaced by a podling** in the Shadowfell
- **S02E03:** The real Volo is found captive in a pod inside the [[Beast of Erua]]; amazed to learn he was inside the legendary creature, he immediately insisted on recording everything
- **S02E05:** Volo returns to the [[Short Rest]] (the renovated Trollskull Manor) and explains what happened in Waterdeep during the three-year gap, including the war between Waterdeep and Neverwinter over the Cassalanter diamond mines
- **S02E38:** Volo attends the massive God's Night celebration at Trollskull Manor during Marpenoth, sauntering in to tell his own tall tales and competing with Lark in a friendly storytelling rivalry

## Relationships

- **[[Floon Blagmaar|Floon]]** -- Volo's missing friend whose kidnapping launched the entire adventure; described by Volo as having "more beauty than brains"
- **[[SWOJ]]** -- The adventuring party Volo hired and inadvertently launched into legend; gave them Trollskull Manor
- **[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]** -- Fellow storyteller; Lark owns an autographed copy of *Volo's Guide to Monsters*; the two share a friendly rivalry over who tells the better tale
- **[[Renaer Neverember|Renaer]]** -- Rescued alongside Floon by SWOJ
- **[[Trollskull Manor]]** -- The property Volo gave to SWOJ as payment, which became their legendary headquarters

## Notable Items and Works

- **[[Volo's Guide to Monsters]]** -- His most famous published work; a tell-all about monsters in the Sword Coast region; Lark owns an autographed copy
- **[[Volo's Paper Crane]]** -- An origami paper crane message Volo sent to the party informing them he would be delayed
- ***Volo's Book of Portals and Portents*** -- An unfinished work Volo sought 5,000 gold to fund; he settled for Lark's stories as source material instead
- **[[Trollskull Manor]] Deed** -- The property deed Volo gave to SWOJ as payment for rescuing Floon and Renaer

## Trivia

- Volo's full name -- Volothamp Ignatius Geddarm -- is one of the most elaborate names in the campaign
- He is a canonical Forgotten Realms character who appears in the official D&D 5E module *Waterdeep: Dragon Heist*, which forms the basis of Season 1
- His gift of Trollskull Manor, intended as a consolation prize for not having enough gold, turned out to be the most consequential payment in the entire campaign -- giving SWOJ their home base, their tavern business, and their identity
- Volo was captured and replaced by a podling in the Shadowfell during the Feywild arc, making him one of several NPCs who experienced the dangers of the planes firsthand
- His friendly storytelling rivalry with Lark persists through the end of Season 2
