# Tyr

The god of justice and heroism in the Forgotten Realms. Tyr's influence in the Twin Portals campaign appears primarily through his association with [[Lord Halueth Never]], the first king of [[Neverwinter]], and through iconography in the crypts beneath [[Castle Never]].

## Role in the Story

In the [[Castle Never Crypts]], [[SWOJ]] found two sealed doors at the end of a long hallway lined with ancient burial chambers. The gold-scaled door bore the symbol of Tyr, while the silver-scaled door bore the symbol of [[Kelemvor]]. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] opened the gold door leading to [[Lord Halueth Never's Tomb]], the ornate crypt of Neverwinter's first king and a disciple of Tyr.

The gold scales of Tyr's door represent his association with golden justice, contrasting with [[Kelemvor]]'s silver scales of neutral judgment. [[Lord Halueth Never]]'s tomb contained motifs of oaths sworn with knights, reflecting Tyr's domain of justice, honor, and righteous combat.

## Key Connections

- [[Lord Halueth Never]] -- First king of Neverwinter and disciple of Tyr
- [[Kelemvor]] -- Fellow deity; their symbols mark the twin doors in the Castle Never crypts
- [[Castle Never Crypts]] -- Location of the gold-scaled door bearing Tyr's symbol
- [[Lord Halueth Never's Tomb]] -- The crypt behind Tyr's door
