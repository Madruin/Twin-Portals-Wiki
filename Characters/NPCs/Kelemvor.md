# Kelemvor

Kelemvor is the god of the dead and divine judgment in the Forgotten Realms. His presence weaves through the Twin Portals campaign as a distant but consequential force -- from the afterlife politics surrounding [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s stolen soul, to the construction and eventual destruction of his [[Wall Of The Faithless]], to his climactic manifestation as a fifty-foot robed figure bearing silver scales during the Season Two finale at the [[Radiant Citadel]].

**First appearance:** [[S01E21 - Episode Twenty One|S01E21]]
**Total appearances:** 7 episodes

## Description

Kelemvor's symbols appear throughout the campaign as silver scales -- the instrument by which he weighs and judges the dead. In the crypts beneath [[Castle Never]], a door bearing his silver scales marks the resting place of [[Lady Lissandra Never]]. In the grand cathedral deep beneath [[Castle Naerytar (Surface)|Castle Naerytar]], his presence manifests as a set of floating silver scales that actively judge and divert trapped souls away from the dracolich ritual.

At the [[Wall Of The Faithless]], Kelemvor's physical remains take the form of an enormous skeleton at the base of the Wall -- a relic of his mortal past. When necrotic energy from [[Manshoon]]'s forces animates a portion of this skeleton into a bone colossus, it becomes a terrifying obstacle for [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]].

In his most dramatic appearance, Kelemvor manifests fully as a fifty-foot robed figure with silver scales, appearing at the climactic moment of the Season Two finale to present [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]] with the choice to break or preserve the Wall of the Faithless.

## Role in the Story

- **[[S01E21 - Episode Twenty One|S01E21]]:** [[The Hag Of Trollskull Manor]] reveals that she plucked [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s soul directly from Kelemvor's grasp after Lark's death and delivered it to [[Malar]] as bait to lure the unicorn [[Lyrian]] into a trap. This establishes Kelemvor as the rightful custodian of Lark's soul and frames his death as cosmically significant -- important enough that a hag would dare steal from the god of the dead himself.

- **[[S02E18 - Episode Forty Eight LIVE (S02E18)|S02E18]]:** In the haunted crypts beneath [[Castle Never]], [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]] finds two doors: gold scales representing [[Tyr]] (god of justice) and silver scales representing Kelemvor (god of the dead and judgment). Behind Kelemvor's door lies the simple stone coffin of [[Lady Lissandra Never]] and the mithril [[Crown of Neverwinter]]. Kelemvor's association with humble death -- a plain coffin rather than an ornate tomb -- quietly underscores his domain.

- **[[S02E24 - Episode Fifty Four (S02E24)|S02E24]]:** Among [[Friar Truck]]'s notes, [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]] discovers [[Rowan Crooks]]' plan: to use the [[Hungering Shard]] at the [[Radiant Citadel]] to break Kelemvor's [[Wall Of The Faithless]] and free the remaining fragments of [[Manshoon]]'s soul trapped on the other side. This revelation reframes the Wall as both a cosmic injustice and a strategic target, setting up the endgame of Season Two.

- **[[S02E26 - Episode Fifty Six (S02E26)|S02E26]]:** [[The Raven Queen]]'s emissary addresses [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]] directly about the Wall of the Faithless -- the massive barrier in the afterlife built by Kelemvor to imprison the souls of those who worshipped no deity. The Raven Queen opposes Kelemvor on this matter and seeks allies in her cause. She gifts [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] enchanted raven feathers and takes [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] away for a private audience, deepening the theological stakes of the campaign.

- **[[S02E29 - Episode Fifty Nine LIVE (S02E29)|S02E29]]:** Deep in the Shrouded Citadel beneath [[Castle Naerytar (Surface)|Castle Naerytar]], [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]] emerges from the divine trials of [[Jergal]] and [[Orcus]] into a grand cathedral containing Kelemvor's floating silver scales and a cracked phylactery. This is the party's first direct interaction with Kelemvor's power: they help his scales divert trapped dragon souls away from the phylactery and the [[Cult of the Dragon]]'s dracolich ritual, redirecting them toward the [[Preserve Of The Ancients]].

- **[[S02E35 - Episode Sixty Five (S02E35)|S02E35]]:** The [[Radiant Citadel]] exits the [[Keening Gloam]] and arrives at the [[Wall Of The Faithless]] -- Kelemvor's domain. [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]] is teleported to the Wall's surface, where they encounter [[Arcturia]], [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]'s sister, who has come on behalf of [[Halaster Blackcloak]] to break the Wall. After tense negotiations and combat, Arcturia leads the party to the base of Kelemvor's enormous skeleton, where she begins a ritual to summon the souls trapped within the Wall.

- **[[S02E36 - Episode Sixty Six (S02E36)|S02E36]]:** The climax of Season Two. As [[Arcturia]]'s ritual progresses at the foot of Kelemvor's skeleton, [[Manshoon]]'s necrotic forces attack and the skeleton itself animates into a bone colossus. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] is hurled at the construct and uses thunderwave to destroy it. The ritual completes, raising an army of ancient souls from the Wall -- blue and white spectres in forgotten armor, led by an orc woman warrior ghost. In the final confrontation with Manshoon, Kelemvor manifests in his full divine form: a fifty-foot robed figure with silver scales. He presents [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]] with the ultimate choice -- break the Wall of the Faithless or leave it standing. SWOJ votes unanimously to break it. Silver waves of energy cascade into the Wall, shattering it and freeing countless trapped souls. Manshoon's soul is sent to [[The Raven Queen]].

## Relationships

- **[[The Raven Queen]]** -- A rival deity of death who opposes Kelemvor's Wall of the Faithless. She actively seeks allies against him, recruiting [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]] to her cause. Despite their opposition on the Wall, both deities ultimately benefit from its destruction: Kelemvor releases the souls, and the Raven Queen receives [[Manshoon]]'s soul in judgment.
- **[[Malar]]** -- The Beast Lord, whose servant [[The Hag Of Trollskull Manor]] stole [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s soul from Kelemvor's grasp. This theft represents a direct affront to Kelemvor's authority over the dead.
- **[[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]]** -- The adventuring party who ultimately decides the fate of Kelemvor's Wall. They first interact with his power by helping his scales divert souls in the cathedral beneath Castle Naerytar, and later vote unanimously to shatter his Wall of the Faithless, freeing all trapped souls.
- **[[Arcturia]]** -- [[Halaster Blackcloak]]'s apprentice who performs the ritual at Kelemvor's skeleton on the Wall of the Faithless. Her magic raises an army of ancient souls and triggers the chain of events that leads to Kelemvor's manifestation.
- **[[Jergal]]** -- An ancient deity known as the Lord of the End of Everything, one of the original gods of death before Kelemvor. His trial in the Shrouded Citadel precedes [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]]'s encounter with Kelemvor's scales.
- **[[Manshoon]]** -- The primary antagonist of Season Two, whose fragmented soul was partly trapped in the Wall of the Faithless. His defeat is sealed when Kelemvor manifests and SWOJ votes to break the Wall, sending Manshoon's soul to the Raven Queen.
