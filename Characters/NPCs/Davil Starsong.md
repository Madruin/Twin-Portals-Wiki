# Davil Starsong

Half-elf bandleader, adventurer, and leader of the [[Doom Raiders]], a moderate subfaction within the [[Zhentarim|Zhentarim (Black Network)]]. Davil is a charismatic showman and "grandstander" who refuses to hide behind masks or black leather, instead operating openly as both a musician and a political operator in [[Waterdeep]]. He came to Waterdeep from [[Neverwinter]] searching for the [[Stone of Golorr]], and over the course of the campaign became one of [[Scoot Sparkles|SWOJ]]'s most enduring allies -- ultimately rising to lead the Zhentarim in Waterdeep after [[Manshoon]]'s defeat.

**Race:** Half-elf
**Occupation:** Bandleader of the [[Doom Raiders]]; Zhentarim faction leader
**First appearance:** [[S01E11 - Episode Eleven|S01E11]]
**Total appearances:** 8+ episodes across Seasons 1 and 2
**Faction affiliations:** [[Doom Raiders]], [[Zhentarim]]

---

## Description

Davil Starsong is described as a dashing half-elf figure with a force of personality far exceeding his musical talent. At the [[Yawning Portal]], [[Bonnie Hilltopple|Bonnie]] points him out to SWOJ as a half-elf who is "really dashing, kind of like grandstanding, like telling stories to a big crowd" and who "seems like he knows more than he lets on." He plays the lute, and while his technique is "kind of out of tune, kind of off pitch," he compensates through sheer charisma -- working the crowd, making connections with the audience, and welcoming everyone into the chorus.

His breath "kind of stinks," as [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] discovers upon their first handshake.

Davil's aesthetic embodies the Doom Raiders' philosophy: where [[Manshoon]]'s Zhentarim agents wear black leather armor and masks, Davil dresses as an adventurer -- weapons, potions, and trophies on display. When SWOJ suggested the Doom Raiders partially cover their faces before infiltrating the [[Cassalanter Villa|Cassalanter]] feast, Davil flatly refused, saying he would not hide who he was. This openness -- performing in public, maintaining a visible presence in the city, refusing to operate from the shadows -- defines both the man and the faction he leads.

---

## Personality

Davil is above all a salesman. He grandstands, he boasts, he works a room. He introduced himself to [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] with "It is my utmost pleasure to meet you" while standing on a table in the middle of the [[Yawning Portal]], two drinks in hand, lute freshly put down after leading the crowd in a rendition of "The Wizard War." He describes himself and his crew as people who "fight evil for money -- not anyone for money," drawing a deliberate contrast with the Necromancer's assassins and soldiers.

He is direct about his faction's needs and does not disguise his Zhentarim affiliation. When requesting the 5,000 gold investment from SWOJ, he opened with flattery ("Amazing what you did at the Cassalanter Villa") before plainly admitting: "We are all some members of the Black Network, if I hadn't made that clear by now." He acknowledged that this was "a big ask" but framed it as "also a big sell," offering the services of his crew in return.

## Role in the Story

### First Contact at the Yawning Portal (S01E11)

SWOJ first encountered Davil at the [[Yawning Portal]] after [[Bonnie Hilltopple|Bonnie]] identified him as someone with connections to the Zhentarim who "knows more than he lets on." Bonnie telepathically read Davil's mind -- rolling a natural 20 -- and pulled [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] aside on the balcony to share critical intelligence: Davil had come to Waterdeep from [[Neverwinter]] specifically searching for the [[Stone of Golorr]], and he had a connection to [[Yagra]].

Meanwhile, Davil was standing on a table leading the crowd in song. When Ylka leapt down from the balcony using featherfall and floated toward him to shake his hand, the crowd erupted. Davil gave her a strong handshake, set his drinks down, and introduced himself: "You must be Ilka. I'm Davil. Davil Starsong."

Davil later attended the grand opening of [[Trollskull Alley  The Short Rest|The Short Rest]] tavern, but when a [[Griffin Cavalry Of Waterdeep|griffin cavalry]] rider appeared overhead during the catastrophic [[Trollskull Alley]] fireball explosion, he fled -- a sign of his faction's precarious position within the Zhentarim power structure and his wariness of the city's military authorities.

### The Cassalanter Feast Infiltration (S01E27)

When SWOJ needed to infiltrate the [[Cassalanter Family  Cult Of Asmodeus|Cassalanters']] Founders Day feast -- a front for a devil-soul harvest -- the Doom Raiders provided their cover. The band had been booked as the lead musical act at the feast. SWOJ disguised themselves as additional members of the Doom Raiders and entered [[Cassalanter Villa]] alongside Davil, [[Yagra]], and the rest of the band.

The Doom Raiders' aesthetic -- adventurer garb rather than uniforms -- made it possible for SWOJ to blend in. When the party suggested that the band partially cover their faces for the infiltration, Davil refused: he was not going to hide. His stubbornness forced SWOJ to find other means of disguise (Vincent Trench's office provided some options), but it also reflected his principled refusal to operate like Manshoon's shadow agents.

While SWOJ moved through the feast, the Doom Raiders performed. After SWOJ discovered the poisoned food and confronted [[Victoro Cassalanter]] in a battle against spined devils and imps, the Doom Raiders were heard chiming in musically from afar as the chaos escalated -- "Suddenly it's Queen at Wembley."

### The 5,000 Gold Investment (S01E29)

In the Season 1 finale, during a grand celebration in [[Trollskull Alley]] with food carts, a Xanathar pinata, and the Doom Raiders performing, Davil approached [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] directly and made his pitch. He explained the Zhentarim's internal power struggle: the Doom Raiders were "sort of our own pocket division of the Zentarim" who "do things our own way," while the Necromancer's main division -- "They're murderers" -- was expanding its influence in the city now that the [[Xanathar]] had been defeated.

Davil requested **5,000 gold pieces** to keep the Doom Raiders operational, framing it as a mutual investment: "My crew have a very diverse set of skills as well. You have access to Ziraj and his bowmanship, Skeemo and his alchemy, myself, and my many prolific talents." He argued that Waterdeep would benefit from having "a maybe more neutral party with influence in the underworld."

SWOJ debated the investment -- [[Lark Wavesilver|Lark]] "really does not like Davil Starsong" -- but ultimately agreed to fund the Doom Raiders. This investment bore fruit across Season 2 as the Doom Raiders became reliable allies and operatives.

### The Doom Network (S02E05)

By Season 2, SWOJ's investment had helped the Doom Raiders formalize their operations through the **[[Doom Network Headquarters|Doom Network]]**, a Waterdeep-based organization that contracted adventurers for jobs. At the Doom Network headquarters, [[Skeemo]] assigned SWOJ the mission to find the missing noblewoman [[Anya Irmbrusk]] (the Staglass) in [[Amphail]]. [[Ziraj The Hunter|Ziraj]] appeared as well, a hulking figure with a giant bow strapped to his back, demanding they find "Thela." City guards at the headquarters recognized SWOJ with excitement and compared them favorably to the Doom Raiders as heroes of Waterdeep.

### The Ethereal War (S02E31-S02E33)

When SWOJ assembled their allies for the final assault against [[Manshoon]]'s forces at the [[Spiral Cavern]] and beyond, the Doom Raiders answered the call. They joined the crew aboard the Ethereal Galleon alongside other allies -- [[Squidly]], [[Fel'Rekt Lafeen]], [[Shaleheart]], and [[Fioleth]]. Their role was characteristically musical: the Doom Raiders served as the second musical act aboard the ship, their performances helping to power the spelljamming helm that propelled the fleet. [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] led them with her concertina in what was described as "very death metal polka."

When the Ethereal Galleon took on extra weight from a towed vessel, the Doom Raiders joined Ylka in making music to help power the ship through the added strain. Throughout the fleet's journey, they kept playing to keep the ships afloat while other allied forces -- the [[Jolly Gents]], Neverwinter troops -- handled scouting and combat.

### Captured and Freed (S02E35-S02E36)

During the battle at the [[Radiant Citadel]] in the ethereal plane, Davil and the Doom Raiders were among those captured by Manshoon's forces. They were found bound in magical tentacle restraints at the bottom of the Citadel, guarded by a cultist. [[Lark Wavesilver|Lark]] freed them alongside [[Squidly]] and [[Urstul Floxxin]], and the Doom Raiders immediately rejoined the fight against the last [[Manshoon]] clone. Their liberation was part of the turning point in the final battle that ended with Manshoon's soul being sent to the [[Raven Queen]].

### The New Zhentarim (S02E38)

With Manshoon permanently destroyed, the Zhentarim entered a power vacuum in Waterdeep. Davil and the Doom Raiders filled the void. By the Season 2 finale, [[Simon]], [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]'s father, returned from years lost at sea and told of being helped home by "Zhentarim-affiliated pirates" who "checked in with the new folks -- they call themselves the Doom Raiders." Simon noted they had "been a local establishment for years" and were "just sort of starting" to take on formal leadership.

The Doom Raiders had established a **public-facing office** in Waterdeep, a far cry from the Kolat Towers and shadow dens of Manshoon's regime. [[Tagga|Tega]], Scoot's mother, **winked knowingly** when asked about the Zhentarim -- suggesting the organization under Doom Raiders leadership had become something less threatening, or at least something Tega had quiet connections to.

The Doom Raiders performed at the massive God's Night celebration at [[Trollskull Manor]] during Marpenoth, playing covers and original music alongside the many guests and musicians who had gathered.

## Relationships

- **[[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]** -- Davil's primary contact within SWOJ. He approached Ylka first at the [[Yawning Portal]], introduced himself with a strong handshake and flattery, and later directed his 5,000 gold pitch to her. Their musical duet at the Yawning Portal -- Davil on lute, Ylka on concertina -- was the foundation of their working relationship.
- **[[Yagra]]** -- Half-orc fighter and cello player in the Doom Raiders. Sober and serious where Davil is boisterous. She was introduced alongside Davil at the Yawning Portal in [[S01E11 - Episode Eleven|S01E11]] and remained a core member of the band throughout.
- **[[Ziraj The Hunter|Ziraj]]** -- Tracker and bowman in Davil's crew. Offered by Davil as part of the 5,000 gold deal ("You have access to Ziraj and his bowmanship"). Later guided SWOJ north from Waterdeep to Amphail.
- **[[Skeemo]]** -- Alchemist and operative who runs the [[Doom Network Headquarters|Doom Network]]. Offered by Davil alongside Ziraj. Assigned SWOJ the Staglass mission in [[S02E05 - Episode Thirty Five (S02E05)|S02E05]].
- **[[Lark Wavesilver|Lark]]** -- Lark "really does not like Davil Starsong," though he participated in the investment decision and later freed Davil from captivity at the [[Radiant Citadel]].
- **[[Bonnie Hilltopple|Bonnie]]** -- The doppelganger bartender at the [[Yawning Portal]] who telepathically read Davil's mind, confirming his search for the [[Stone of Golorr]] and his origins in [[Neverwinter]].
- **[[Manshoon]]** -- Davil's direct rival within the Zhentarim. The Doom Raiders and Manshoon's main division represent opposing visions for the Black Network. Davil's faction outlasted Manshoon's and inherited the organization.

## The Doom Raiders as a Band

The Doom Raiders are as much a musical act as a political faction. Their known lineup includes:

| Member | Instrument / Role |
|--------|-------------------|
| [[Davil Starsong]] | Lute; frontman and vocalist |
| [[Yagra]] | Cello |
| [[Bonnie Hilltopple\|Bonnie]] | Double bass (occasional guest) |
| Other members | Half-orcs, halflings, and others of uncertain heritage |

Their repertoire includes "The Wizard War," a famous Sword Coast anthem. They performed at the Cassalanter Founders Day feast ([[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]]), the Trollskull Alley celebration ([[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]), aboard the Ethereal Galleon during the war against Manshoon ([[S02E31 - Episode Sixty One (S02E31)|S02E31]]-[[S02E33 - Episode Sixty Three (S02E33)|S02E33]]), and at the God's Night celebration at [[Trollskull Manor]] ([[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]]).

Their style aboard the Ethereal Galleon was described as "very death metal polka" when led by [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]'s concertina.
