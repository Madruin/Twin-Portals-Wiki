# Laeral Silverhand

**Open Lord of [[Waterdeep]]** -- the city's sole public ruler and one of its most powerful defenders. A former [[Harpers|Harper]] and the daughter of [[Mystra]], goddess of magic, Laeral replaced the corrupt [[Dagult Neverember]] and proved immensely popular with the citizens. She is a key ally and patron of [[Silver Waves Of Justice (SWOJ)|SWOJ]], commissioning them as her personal agents and providing critical intelligence that shapes the party's Season 1 mission.

**First appearance:** [[S01E01 - Episode One|S01E01]] (mentioned)
**First on-screen appearance:** [[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]
**Total appearances:** 7 episodes

---

## Description

Laeral wears white robes and carries herself with a quiet authority that belies enormous arcane power. During her dramatic courtroom entrance in [[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]], the players described her as "somehow a splice between Galadriel and Ruth Bader Ginsburg." She is known as a "lord of the people" -- generally well liked, she walks the streets of [[Waterdeep]] on her own and conducts unannounced tours of different ward courthouses at least once a month to oversee proceedings. Citizens of the [[Dock Ward]] note she does not visit their neighbourhood as often as they would like, though [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] concedes "you could have a beer with her."

When she returns at the end of Season 2, she is road-worn and armed, her robes tied back and several knives visible -- a far cry from the composed magistrate of Season 1, suggesting an extended and dangerous journey during her long absence.

---

## Background

Laeral is a Chosen of [[Mystra]] -- literally the daughter of the goddess of magic. In her younger days she was a [[Harpers|Harper]], and together with her mother she defeated the evil wizard [[Manshoon]] by splicing into the Weave and channeling great conduits of magic to stop him. As [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] recounts from a favourite [[Rowan Crooks]] comic: "she summoned her mother, who is Mystra goddess of magic, and the two of them used their familial bond to defeat Manshoon." Laeral herself confirms the story in the carriage ride after the trial, adding cryptically that "the legends are unclear -- some say that he created clones of himself and those clones are still alive to this day."

She deposed [[Dagult Neverember]], whose conservative spending policies, disruptive tariffs, and embezzlement of the city's gold hoard had driven [[Waterdeep]] into decline. Under Laeral's leadership the city recovered and prospered.

## Role in the Story

### Season 1

- **[[S01E01 - Episode One|S01E01]]:** Introduced in background exposition as the current Open Lord of Waterdeep, "very popular with the citizens," who replaced the deposed [[Dagult Neverember]].
- **[[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]:** Makes her dramatic on-screen debut. Replaces the corrupt magistrate Umberto Stonemero to preside over SWOJ's trial in the [[Docks Ward Guardhouse|Docks Ward courtroom]]. When the doppelganger [[Willifort Crowell (Doppelganger)|Willaford Crowell]] is unmasked and a [[Grell|grell]] attacks through the window, Laeral demonstrates immense telekinetic power by slamming the grell into the room and crushing it. She frees SWOJ from their manacles and declares the investigation now under the joint control of [[Force Grey]] and the [[Masked Lords Of Waterdeep|Masked Lords]].
- **[[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]:** Takes SWOJ by carriage to the [[Haventree Estate (Harper Safehouse)|Haventree Estate]] near the [[The God Catcher|God Catcher]] in the [[Castle Ward]]. In a long private briefing she:
  - Reveals the [[Stone of Golorr]]'s three eye sockets are keys to [[Dagult Neverember|Neverember]]'s [[Vault of Dragons|dragon hoard vault]]
  - Commissions SWOJ as her personal agents -- "separate from the Masked Lords of Waterdeep, separate from the Harpers, separate from any other agency. This is between me and you."
  - Takes custody of the Stone of Golorr for safekeeping (carefully wrapped in a handkerchief, refusing to touch it directly lest the aboleth gain access to her memories)
  - Provides extensive intelligence on the [[Xanathar Guild]]: the [[The Xanathar|Xanathar]] is a paranoid beholder who trusts only its pet goldfish [[Sylgar]]; a [[Mind Flayer|mind flayer]] breeds intellect devourers via a spawning pool; [[Thorvin Twinbeard]] is the Harpers' paid informant inside the lair; the drow [[Xibrinidas]] has risen to become the Xanathar's personal advisor
  - Reveals a secret stair in the [[Castle Ward]] sewers leading into the Xanathar's Lair and offers to send an agent to guide SWOJ there
  - Examines the sleeping [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] and determines she is safe but dreaming under an outside force; leaves Xae under Harper protection at the estate
  - Offers to put out feelers for the [[Esvelle Wavesilver The Black Viper|Black Viper]] to recruit her for the lair mission, describing her as "a vigilante bringing justice to the streets"
  - Tells SWOJ they can drop her name at [[Blackstaff Tower]] for assistance, though she warns "I wouldn't trust them with anything particularly valuable"
  - Vouches for [[Remallia Haventree]]: "I have one agent in particular named Ramalia Haventree who I know I would trust with my own life"
  - Signs [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s copy of [[Volothamp Geddarm|Volo]]'s Guide to Monsters with silver ink ("For Lark, with love -- Laeral S.") and magically fixes the paint colour on his miniature figurine of her
  - Agrees to cover for SWOJ publicly by saying they are "doing time at a men's farm" and promises to work on reducing the monetary damages from their charges
- **[[S01E25 - Episode Twenty Five|S01E25]]:** Mentioned in the context of city politics during SWOJ's quest to assemble the Stone of Golorr.
- **[[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]]:** [[Esvelle Wavesilver The Black Viper|Isvel Wavesilver]] reports that "Larry Silverhands, the open lord, hasn't been seen in a couple of ten days now" -- the first hint of her disappearance. Her absence is described as "a mystery."
- **[[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]:** The [[Masked Lords Of Waterdeep|Masked Lords]] officially confirm to SWOJ that Laeral has been missing since the spring equinox. They had kept her disappearance secret from the public.

### Season 2

- **[[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]]:** In the Season 2 finale's climactic final scene, twin portals appear on the rooftop of [[Trollskull Manor]] during the God's Night celebration. A teal-green portal releases an aboleth tendril. Then a rose-gold portal opens and Laeral Silverhand arrives, riding the unicorn [[Lyrian]], road-worn with several knives and tied-back robes -- suggesting she has been on an extended dangerous journey across the multiverse. Her arrival closes the green portal. She waves at SWOJ, setting up Season 3.

## Key Relationships

- **[[Silver Waves Of Justice (SWOJ)|SWOJ]]** -- Patron and ally. Commissions them as her personal agents in [[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]] and provides the intelligence and political cover that enables SWOJ's Season 1 mission against the [[Xanathar Guild]].
- **[[Remallia Haventree]]** -- Her most trusted [[Harpers|Harper]] agent, described as someone she "would trust with my own life." Laeral routes all communication with SWOJ through Remallia and the [[Haventree Estate (Harper Safehouse)|Haventree Estate]].
- **[[Mystra]]** -- Her mother, the goddess of magic. Their familial bond was used to defeat [[Manshoon]] by channeling great conduits through the Weave.
- **[[Dagult Neverember]]** -- The former Open Lord she deposed. His embezzlement of the dragon hoard drives much of the Season 1 plot that Laeral oversees.
- **[[Masked Lords Of Waterdeep|Masked Lords]]** -- The anonymous ruling council she leads as Open Lord. They share jurisdiction over the Stone of Golorr investigation with [[Force Grey]].
- **[[Force Grey]]** -- The elite adventuring company under the Open Lord's authority, invoked by Laeral to assume control of the investigation.
- **[[Lyrian]]** -- The unicorn she rides through the rose-gold portal in her dramatic return at the end of Season 2.
- **[[Esvelle Wavesilver The Black Viper|The Black Viper]]** -- Laeral describes her as "a vigilante bringing justice to the streets" and offers to recruit her for SWOJ's lair infiltration mission.
- **[[Manshoon]]** -- Historical enemy. Laeral and Mystra defeated him in the past, but Laeral warns that his clones may still be alive -- a warning that proves prophetic as the Necromancer is later revealed to be Manshoon.
