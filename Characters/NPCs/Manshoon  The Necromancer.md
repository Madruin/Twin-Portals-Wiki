# Manshoon / The Necromancer

The ancient founder of the [[Zhentarim]] and the primary antagonist of the Twin Portals campaign across both seasons. A wizard of terrifying power who perpetuated himself through a network of clones scattered across the multiverse, Manshoon's schemes to achieve godhood drove SWOJ from the streets of Waterdeep to the furthest reaches of the planes.

**First mentioned:** [[S01E21 - Episode Twenty One|S01E21]] (as "The Necromancer")
**Final defeat:** [[S02E36 - Episode Sixty Six (S02E36)|S02E36]]
**Total appearances:** 9+ episodes (across multiple identities)

## Description

Manshoon is a legendary wizard and the original founder of the [[Zhentarim]], a vast criminal network. He discovered a method of perpetuating himself through clone magic, creating multiple copies of his consciousness that operated independently across the multiverse. In his original form as "The Necromancer," he appeared as a young-looking wizard with a **mechanical hand**, operating from an extra-dimensional sanctum accessed through the [[Kolat Towers]] in Waterdeep. He maintained a **simulacrum** that wore an iron mask, which fought on his behalf.

By his final incarnation at the [[Radiant Citadel]], Manshoon had become undead -- wearing a **half-mask** over his face and possessing a **metal arm**. He was a master of devastating arcane magic, wielding spells including *finger of death*, *cone of cold*, *hold person*, *misty step*, *Bigby's hand*, *synaptic static*, and *disintegrate*. He possessed legendary actions in combat.

Manshoon's connection to [[Ylka Gralhund]] runs deep: he was connected to her grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund]], and it was Manshoon (as The Necromancer) who revealed that Ylka is in fact a clone of Zilka, created by Mordecai.

## Known Clones

Manshoon's clones were a defining feature of his villainy -- each operated with partial autonomy but served fragments of his greater ambition. The known clones encountered during the campaign include:

### The Necromancer (Kolat Towers Clone)
The identity SWOJ first encountered. A young-looking wizard with a mechanical hand, he operated from the [[Kolat Towers]] and an extra-dimensional sanctum in Waterdeep. He performed the clone spell to resurrect [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] after his death, and revealed the truth about Ylka's origins. He planted a **Gem of Seeing** in Lark's temple to spy on SWOJ. His simulacrum (iron mask) battled the city watch. He appeared on the theater balcony during the Stone of Golorr battle, casting *Bigby's Hand* against [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]], but retreated after [[Remalia Haventree|Remalia]]'s fireball.

### Rowan Crooks (The 14th Clone) -- see also: [[Rowan Crooks]]
The most dangerous and deceptive of all clones. Manshoon murdered the real Rowan Crooks long ago and assumed his identity, becoming the beloved leader of the [[Jolly Gents]] adventuring group. He traveled to the [[Radiant Citadel]] as a folk hero, built trust with the speakers, and introduced the **soul pylons** -- an infrastructure he later weaponized for his ascension plot. He was revealed as the 14th clone in [[S02E11 - Episode Forty One (S02E11)|S02E11]], where he killed SWOJ, looted their corpses, captured [[Shuni]]'s spirit in a soul stone, and teleported away with all of SWOJ's possessions. He established his floating fortress, [[Zentil Keep]], in the Shadowfell and used the Jolly Gents as his personal army, binding many of their souls to phylacteries via [[Friar Truck]]. Ultimately defeated by [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]'s lightning-split axe attack during the sky-falling battle as Zentil Keep collapsed, then trapped inside the [[Soul Stone]] after his phylactery was destroyed.

### Shuni (The Breathless Elder Clone)
A Manshoon clone living inside the corpse of the [[Breathless Elder]], a colossal dead viper fish deity in the Far Realm. Uniquely among the clones, Shuni was **surprisingly friendly and cooperative** with SWOJ, explaining the gem/lure mechanism controlling the dragon tethers. He was murdered by Rowan Crooks and his allies, then his spirit was recaptured in a cracked soul stone. As a ghost, he briefly advised SWOJ about soul mechanics before being recaptured again.

### The Nancy Clone (Amphail Clone)
A Manshoon clone operating from [[Amphail]], commanding forces mustered in the [[Spiral Cavern]] beneath the mountain. Seen conferring with an [[Ancient Black Dragon (Elf Form)|ancient black dragon in elf form]] at the crystal overlook. Planned to consolidate forces inside the cavern and open a hole at the top of the mountain for aerial access. Walked past [[Mr. E]] without recognizing him.

### Man-Two (Ice Clone)
A clone made of **ice and water** who accompanied SWOJ into the Neverwinter chasm. Used *hypnotic pattern* to neutralize guards at a checkpoint. Destroyed when he stepped on an anti-magic rune trap, causing his magically-sustained body to disintegrate instantly.

### The Body Taker (Plant Clone)
A clone made of **dense, stringy plant material** encountered inside the [[Dynamo of Possibility]]. Controlled vine tentacles and plant-based podlings. More emotionally sensitive than other clones, particularly enraged by mentions of Rowan Crooks. Defeated atop the tower inside the Dynamo.

### Fetch (Soul Fragment)
Not a full clone but a **nine-year-old fragment** of Manshoon's soul, representing his innocence and childhood. Had a crescent moon glow on his heart. Previously worked for the wizard Rantantar. Merged with [[Teriador]]'s soul to gain enough power to transport SWOJ out of the collapsing Dynamo.

### The Final Manshoon (Radiant Citadel)
The last surviving clone of Manshoon, encountered at the [[Radiant Citadel]]. Undead, wearing a half-mask and possessing a metal arm. He had been channeling [[Rowan Crooks]]' soul pylon to ascend to godhood, planning to use the [[Hungering Shard]] to free his remaining soul fragments from [[Kelemvor]]'s [[Wall of the Faithless]]. He teleported around the battlefield, cast *synaptic static* and exhaustion effects, and desperately searched for the soul stone of Rowan Crooks. Ultimately defeated when [[Kelemvor]] appeared and Manshoon's soul was sent to the [[Raven Queen]], ensuring he could never return.

## Role in the Story

### Season 1: The Necromancer Revealed

- **S01E21:** [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] contacts the inscrutable wizard known as "The Necromancer" via a scrying pool in the Short Rest cellar; he offers to create a copy of Lark's body and send Scoot and Ylka into the afterlife to retrieve him
- **S01E22:** The Necromancer is revealed as a young-looking wizard with a mechanical hand, connected to [[Mordecai Gralhund]]; performs the clone spell to resurrect Lark from his extra-dimensional sanctum accessed through the [[Kolat Towers]]
- **S01E23:** The Necromancer confronts SWOJ and reveals that Ylka is a clone of Zilka; tells them the [[Cassalanters]] have the third Eye of the [[The Stone of Golorr]]; his simulacrum battles the city watch
- **S01E26:** Manshoon (or his simulacrum) appears on the theater balcony during the Stone of Golorr battle with Zhentarim lieutenants; casts *Bigby's Hand* against Scoot; retreats after Remalia's fireball
- **S01E29:** [[Rowan Crooks]] reveals the Necromancer's true name as **Manshoon**, founder of the Zhentarim; Crooks extracts a Gem of Seeing from Lark's temple that Manshoon had planted to spy on SWOJ; SWOJ sets out through a Feywild portal to hunt him
- **S01E30:** Manshoon is discussed as the primary antagonist thread that remained unresolved, driving the story into the Feywild for Season 2

### Season 2: The Clone Wars and Final Defeat

- **S02E04:** The Body Taker clone (plant material) battles SWOJ inside the Dynamo of Possibility; Fetch, the child soul fragment, merges with Teriador
- **S02E05:** Rowan Crooks captures Fetch's soul in a crystal shard
- **S02E11:** Rowan Crooks is revealed as the 14th Manshoon clone; kills SWOJ, loots their corpses, captures Shuni's spirit, and teleports away
- **S02E12:** The 14th clone finishes carving a teleportation circle, takes all of SWOJ's gear, recaptures Shuni's ghost, takes Little Joan, and teleports away -- leaving SWOJ as helpless spirits
- **S02E15:** Intelligence surfaces that a Manshoon clone is heavily involved in Neverwinter politics, operating behind a mask
- **S02E16:** Man-Two (ice and water clone) accompanies SWOJ into the Neverwinter chasm; uses *hypnotic pattern* on guards; destroyed by anti-magic rune trap
- **S02E19:** The Nancy Clone is spotted in the Spiral Cavern at Amphail, conferring with an ancient black dragon about consolidating forces
- **S02E22:** SWOJ infiltrates Zentil Keep, Rowan Crooks' floating fortress in the Shadowfell
- **S02E24:** SWOJ confronts Crooks publicly at Zentil Keep, triggering a civil war among the Jolly Gents
- **S02E25:** Crooks is defeated by Scoot's lightning-split axe; trapped in the Soul Stone after his phylactery is destroyed; Zentil Keep collapses
- **S02E35:** Sholeh reveals how Manshoon corrupted the soul pylon infrastructure that the real Rowan Crooks had built at the Radiant Citadel
- **S02E36:** The final Manshoon -- undead, half-masked, metal-armed -- channels the soul pylon to ascend to godhood; desperately seeks Rowan Crooks' soul stone; defeated when Kelemvor appears and his soul is sent to the Raven Queen

## Relationships

- **[[Zhentarim]]** -- Founded the organization; used it as his power base across centuries
- **[[Jolly Gents]]** -- Secretly controlled through his Rowan Crooks identity; bound many members' souls to phylacteries
- **[[Mordecai Gralhund]]** -- Connected to the Necromancer; their relationship predates the campaign
- **[[Ylka Gralhund]]** -- Revealed her true nature as a clone of Zilka
- **[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]** -- Resurrected him via clone spell; planted a Gem of Seeing in his temple to spy on SWOJ
- **[[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]** -- Ultimately killed by Scoot's axe in their final confrontation
- **[[SWOJ]]** -- Primary antagonist; killed the entire party once before their final defeat
- **[[Shuni]]** -- A fellow Manshoon clone, murdered by the Rowan Crooks identity
- **[[Friar Truck]]** -- Ally who maintained clone tanks and soul-binding phylacteries at Zentil Keep
- **[[Kelemvor]]** -- The god whose Wall of the Faithless held Manshoon's remaining soul fragments
- **[[Raven Queen]]** -- Manshoon's soul was ultimately sent to her as final punishment
- **[[Doom Raiders]]** -- Moderate Zhentarim faction that opposed Manshoon's main division
