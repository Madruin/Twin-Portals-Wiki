# The Raven Queen

A powerful deity of death, fate, memory, and the transition between life and death. Known by her [[Shadar-kai]] servants as the **Raven Mother**, the **Madam of Feathers**, and the **Queen of Memory**, she rules from the [[Fortress of Memory]] in the [[Shadowfell]]. She opposes the unnatural hoarding and manipulation of souls and stands against [[Kelemvor]]'s [[Wall of the Faithless]]. In Season 2 of Twin Portals she becomes a pivotal divine patron of [[SWOJ]], testing them, arming them, and ultimately granting [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] divine intervention in the campaign's climactic battle.

**First appearance:** [[S02E20 - Episode Fifty (S02E20)|S02E20]] (shrine and murals)
**First direct appearance:** [[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]] (manifests after the dream trials)
**Total appearances:** 10 episodes

## Description

The Raven Queen does not typically present herself in a conventional physical form. When she does manifest, she appears as a ten-foot-tall woman with massive black wings, floating down from above in silence. Her communication is largely non-verbal -- she conveys meaning through emotional impressions, imagery, and the behavior of her spectral ravens. Her emissary (a statue-like figure) speaks on her behalf in a clipped, raven-like cadence.

According to murals in the [[Raven Queen Shrine]] beneath the [[Shard of Moonlight]], the Raven Queen originated from some kind of magical catastrophe. The murals depict her battling other gods of death -- including [[Orcus]], whom she defeated with a great scythe -- and show souls and memories flowing toward her [[Fortress of Memory]] in the sky. The sacred words "more to valued corvus" appear inscribed on the shrine walls.

## Role in the Story

### The Shrine Beneath the Shard of Moonlight ([[S02E20 - Episode Fifty (S02E20)|S02E20]])

After [[SWOJ]] chose the [[Shard of Moonlight]] as their new base of operations, [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] discovered a star staircase descending beneath the tower to a hidden shrine dedicated to the Raven Queen. The shrine contained crow tapestries and murals depicting the Raven Queen's origin and her battles against other death gods. A giant skeletal raven beast guarded the shrine, challenging the party with the Raven Queen's voice: "Do you serve the balance?" After SWOJ defeated the undead guardians, a portal within the shrine launched them into the [[Shadowfell]].

### The Sepulchre and the Soul Stone ([[S02E21 - Episode Fifty One (S02E21)|S02E21]])

In the deep Shadowfell, the [[Shadar-kai]] trio -- [[Idae]], [[Mari]], and [[Blish]] -- directed SWOJ to the [[Sepulchre of the Raven Queen]] atop a mountain spire. To enter, each party member had to make a sacrificial offering at the altar: [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] gave his durags, [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] sang a personal song, [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] whispered a secret, and [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] revealed her middle name. Inside, shadow guides led the party past traps -- including a tempting magical pendant -- to a [[soul stone]], a gift from the Raven Queen intended to trap the "false crux" (later used to imprison [[Rowan Crooks]]).

### Mentioned in the Shadowfell Advance ([[S02E22 - Episode Fifty Two LIVE (S02E22)|S02E22]])

The previously-on recap references the Raven Queen's role in helping SWOJ travel to the Shadowfell to chase [[Manshoon]]'s clone.

### The Dream Trials ([[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]])

After SWOJ defeated [[Rowan Crooks]] atop [[Zhentil Keep]] and trapped his spirit in the soul stone, the Raven Queen drew the party into an elaborate dream sequence to test their worthiness. The dream unfolded across multiple surreal scenarios -- fishing on the [[The Sea Plum|Sea Plum]], a battle of the bands in [[Waterdeep]], a Jolly Gents assault on [[Xanathar's Lair]], a memory-erasing sepulchre, and a climactic fight against shadow versions of themselves. After SWOJ defeated their dark reflections, the Raven Queen herself descended on black wings, silently regarding the party and acknowledging their worthiness.

### The Emissary, the Gifts, and Lark's Audience ([[S02E26 - Episode Fifty Six (S02E26)|S02E26]])

The Raven Queen's emissary -- a statue-like figure speaking in a clipped, raven-like voice -- addressed SWOJ directly. The emissary spoke about the [[Wall of the Faithless]], a massive barrier in the afterlife built by [[Kelemvor]] to trap the souls of those who worshipped no deity. The Raven Queen opposes this wall and sought allies in her cause.

As a gift, she granted [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] enchanted raven feathers attached to his axe, capable of banishing undead once per week. She then took [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] away for a private audience. The details of this meeting were not fully revealed at the time, but Lark later returned with his soul anchored to his hat as a safeguard -- if he were to die, the hat would preserve his essence.

### Lark's Return and the Quest ([[S02E27 - Episode Fifty Seven (S02E27)|S02E27]])

[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] returned from his audience with the Raven Queen bearing the soul-anchor enchantment on his hat. The party referenced the Raven Queen's guidance throughout their journey south toward [[Castle Naerytar]], invoking her name when explaining their mission to the lizardfolk cleric [[Semu]]: "Our companion was given a quest by the Raven Queen." The party understood that the Raven Queen wanted them to destroy relics of [[Myrkul]] that the [[Cult of the Dragon]] was excavating.

### Power Over the Dead ([[S02E29 - Episode Fifty Nine LIVE (S02E29)|S02E29]])

After SWOJ completed the trials within the [[The Shrouded Citadel|Shrouded Citadel]] -- including the [[Trial of Jergal]] and the [[Trial of Orcus]] -- their spirits rose through the earth as incorporeal forms. The Raven Queen's presence guided them, allowing SWOJ to briefly communicate with [[Little Joan]] and the Ripples as spirits. She gave them a series of emotional impressions -- a "ca, ca, ca, ca" sound -- conveying her support. When the party's spirits returned to their bodies in a hallway filled with raven feathers, the Raven Queen imbued them with power over the dead, allowing their thoughts to influence nearby spirits.

### Divine Intervention at the Radiant Citadel ([[S02E34 - Episode Sixty Four LIVE (S02E34)|S02E34]])

During the battle atop the [[Radiant Citadel]], [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] grasped the raven feather attached to his axe and communed with the Raven Queen, pleading for help against the two-headed dracolich: "Great queen, we are trying to do your will. We have found your enemy, and we are trying to send him to you, but we need your help." The Raven Queen answered with a massive flock of spectral ravens that banished the dracolich and destroyed the top [[Soul Pylon|soul pylon]]. This act of divine intervention was a turning point in the Season 2 finale.

### Warning About Manshoon ([[S02E35 - Episode Sixty Five (S02E35)|S02E35]])

In the aftermath of the Citadel battle, the Raven Queen communicated with [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]], warning him about [[Manshoon]]'s ambitions to achieve godhood. As the [[Radiant Citadel]] passed through the [[Keening Gloam]] -- an interdimensional storm between planes -- the spectral ravens faded, their divine power unable to sustain in the void. The party later encountered [[Arcturia]] at the [[Wall of the Faithless]], where the Raven Queen's cause -- breaking the Wall -- became a central question.

### Manshoon's Soul Claimed ([[S02E36 - Episode Sixty Six (S02E36)|S02E36]])

After [[Manshoon]]'s final defeat, [[Kelemvor]] appeared and presented SWOJ with the choice to break or preserve the [[Wall of the Faithless]]. When the party voted to break the Wall, the trapped souls were released. Manshoon's soul was sent to the Raven Queen as punishment, ensuring the villain could never return -- a fitting end given her long opposition to the unnatural manipulation of souls.

## Relationships

- **[[Scoot Sparkles]]** -- Her primary mortal conduit among SWOJ. Scoot received the enchanted raven feathers, communed with her during battle, and channeled her divine intervention against the dracolich.
- **[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]** -- Received a private audience and a soul-anchor enchantment on his hat. The Raven Queen appeared to take a particular interest in Lark, whose own history with death (having been disintegrated and resurrected) may have drawn her attention.
- **[[SWOJ]]** -- Tested the party through dream trials and judged them worthy. Armed them with a soul stone, enchanted raven feathers, power over the dead, and divine intervention.
- **[[Idae]], [[Mari]], [[Blish]]** -- Three [[Shadar-kai]] who serve as her agents in the Shadowfell, collecting wayward souls and their stories to deliver to the [[Fortress of Memory]]. They refer to her as the Raven Mother.
- **[[Kelemvor]]** -- The god of the dead. The Raven Queen opposes his [[Wall of the Faithless]], which traps the souls of those who worshipped no deity. Their theological conflict was a major subplot in the second half of Season 2.
- **[[Orcus]]** -- A demon lord of undeath. The shrine murals depict the Raven Queen battling and defeating Orcus with her great scythe.
- **[[Manshoon]]** -- Her enemy. Manshoon's manipulation of souls through the soul pylons and his ambitions for godhood placed him in direct opposition to the Raven Queen. His soul was ultimately claimed by her.
- **[[Rowan Crooks]]** -- The Manshoon clone whose spirit SWOJ trapped in the Raven Queen's soul stone.

## Associated Locations

- **[[Fortress of Memory]]** -- Her divine seat in the Shadowfell, where souls and their stories are collected and preserved.
- **[[Raven Queen Shrine]]** -- A hidden shrine beneath the [[Shard of Moonlight]] with murals depicting her origin, a skeletal raven guardian, and a portal to the Shadowfell.
- **[[Sepulchre of the Raven Queen]]** -- A sacred tomb-temple atop a mountain spire in the deep Shadowfell, where sacrificial offerings are required for entry and the soul stone was obtained.
- **[[Wall of the Faithless]]** -- The afterlife barrier built by [[Kelemvor]] that the Raven Queen opposes and seeks to destroy.
