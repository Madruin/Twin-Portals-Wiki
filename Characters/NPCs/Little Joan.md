# Little Joan

> *"Our combat reminded me of why I joined the Gents. I had forgotten. It was to be the strongest warrior and use that strength for good -- and something's changed here."*
> -- Little Joan to [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]], in the soul engine room of [[Zhentil Keep]] ([[S02E23 - Episode Fifty Three (S02E23)|S02E23]])

**First appearance:** [[S01E21 - Episode Twenty One|S01E21]]
**Total appearances:** 18 episodes
**Faction affiliations:** [[The Jolly Gents]], [[Jolly Gents (The Ripples)|The Ripples]]

---

## Description

Little Joan is a physically imposing warrior whose ironic name belies her extraordinary size and strength. She is described as a humanoid with pale skin and not much of a nose, giving her an otherworldly, somewhat alien appearance. She wields a dual-bladed axe with devastating proficiency and is capable of feats of raw power that rival [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]'s barbarian rage -- during the collapse of [[Zhentil Keep]], she lifted a massive piece of wall twenty feet wide and ten feet high over her head and hurled it aside like debris.

Joan's arc across the campaign is one of gradual awakening. She first appeared as a member of [[Rowan Crooks]]' inner circle among the [[The Jolly Gents|Jolly Gents]], participating in their arrival through a Feywild portal and later in the devastating betrayal at the [[The Breathless Elder|Breathless Elder]], where she helped murder [[Shoonie|Shuni]] and attack [[SWOJ]]. She barely survived the catastrophic explosion that killed the rest of the combatants, and Crooks teleported her away while she clung to life.

Yet even while serving Crooks, Joan showed signs of independent spirit. She fought SWOJ again at the subterranean lake beneath the Neverwinter chasm, severing the [[The Hungry Elder|Hungry Elder]]'s power source, but her encounters with SWOJ in combat began to stir memories of her original purpose. [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]] later revealed that she and [[Antediluvian Jake]] had been "working on" Joan, who was "starting to crack and develop some free will" -- suggesting that Crooks' control over the Gents had been suppressing her true nature.

The turning point came at Zhentil Keep, where SWOJ found Joan doing one-armed pull-ups in the soul engine room. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] approached her not with threats but with a show of strength and mutual respect -- flexing, pointing, and challenging her to a contest of chain-smashing. His strength-based persuasion (rolling a 22) broke through her remaining loyalty. Joan confessed that fighting Scoot had reminded her why she originally joined the Gents: to be the strongest warrior and use that strength for good. She took up her axe and began attacking the soul engine chains alongside SWOJ, then went to rally other Gents against Crooks. She also gave [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] the original Rowan Crooks journal to help persuade her fellow Gents of the truth.

When the civil war erupted in the Bailey, approximately 20-30 Gents sided with Joan and [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]] against Crooks. Joan stood guard over the chained dragon [[Zelifarn]] during the confrontation. In the sky-falling battle after [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] destabilized the island, Joan raged and physically hurled [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] toward Crooks to help deliver attacks. After the fortress crashed into a Shadowfell canyon, Joan emerged from the rubble by lifting an enormous wall section over her head, confirming her survival.

From that point forward, Joan became the de facto leader of the allied Jolly Gents contingent, which came to be known as "[[Jolly Gents (The Ripples)|The Ripples]]." She led roughly fifteen Gents alongside SWOJ through the remainder of Season 2 -- setting up a secret camp outside [[Castle Naerytar]], commanding the surface operation that blew open an escape chasm for SWOJ, and receiving a spelljammer helm before taking the Ripples to Neverwinter. By the Season 2 finale, Joan was fiercely protective of her alliance with SWOJ, confronting the party suspiciously when they returned to Waterdeep in unfamiliar bodies: "Who are you and what have you done with the Silver Waves of Justice?"

## Relationships

- **[[Scoot Sparkles]]** -- Joan's most significant relationship and the catalyst for her defection. Their repeated combat encounters created a mutual respect grounded in physical strength. Scoot persuaded her to switch sides through a strength-based appeal rather than words, and the two developed an easy camaraderie afterward -- exchanging high fives (low fives, from her perspective) and fighting side by side. Joan's connection with Scoot mirrors how she originally imagined being a Jolly Gent: strength used for good.
- **[[Rowan Crooks]]** -- Joan served as one of Crooks' core lieutenants, participating in the betrayal at the Breathless Elder and the lake ambush. However, her loyalty was never fully willing -- Crooks' soul engine suppressed the Gents' free will, and Joan's growing doubts about his leadership were visible even to her fellow Gents. She ultimately turned against him in the most decisive way possible, rallying his own soldiers to fight him.
- **[[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]]** -- A fellow Jolly Gent who recognized Joan's potential for defection before SWOJ arrived at Zhentil Keep. Ann Jane told [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] that she and [[Antediluvian Jake]] had been "working on Little Joan" and that fighting SWOJ had "opened her eyes." The two exchanged meaningful glances during the Bailey confrontation, coordinating without words as the civil war began.
- **[[Antediluvian Jake]]** -- Jake worked alongside Ann Jane to encourage Joan's growing independence from Crooks. Both were prisoners of Crooks in their own ways, and both ultimately chose to resist.
- **[[SWOJ]]** -- Joan went from enemy to trusted ally. She traveled with the full party for much of Season 2's second half, leading the Ripples as SWOJ's auxiliary force. Her loyalty was tested and proven genuine through Castle Naerytar and the journey to Waterdeep.
- **[[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]** -- During the sky-falling battle over Zhentil Keep, Joan raged and physically hurled Xae through the air toward Crooks, demonstrating both her trust in the party and her raw power.

- **S02E05:** Arrives with Crooks and the full Jolly Gents contingent; present when Crooks captures [[Fetch]]'s soul
