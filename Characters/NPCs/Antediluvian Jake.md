## Description

Antediluvian Jake was the scholarly lore keeper of [[The Jolly Gents]], an ancient wizard who carried an [[Obsidian Tablet (Jake's)|obsidian tablet]] in which he recorded centuries of notes using a magic chisel and his own arcane shorthand. Bookish and somewhat absent-minded, he was more inclined to jot down observations than participate in combat, often trailing behind the group while scribbling on his tablet. He spoke in a formal, archaic manner -- addressing [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] as "young Wavesilver" and dispensing cryptic wisdom like "take the leap and see what you find." His name itself, "Antediluvian," reflected his extreme age; he claimed to have kept notes for centuries and to have been present when the original Jolly Gents fought [[Manshoon]].

As a wizard, Jake was capable of casting [[Teleportation Circle]] rituals and [[Magic Missile]] in combat, though he was never an enthusiastic fighter. During the ambush at the subterranean lake, [[Sixteen String Jack]] taunted him for his lackluster offense, and Jake fired his magic missiles while "looking scared" on his rock. His true value to the Gents lay in his encyclopedic knowledge: he could explain the history of the aboleth-dragon conflict, the nature of the [[The Breathless Elder|Breathless Elder]], the planar instability of the Neverwinter chasm, and the workings of [[Zhentil Keep|Zentil Keep]]'s infrastructure.

Jake's story is ultimately one of coercion and quiet resistance. Like many Jolly Gents, he was killed by [[Manshoon]] and had his soul harvested by [[Rowan Crooks]], who bound it into a phylactery -- a copper orb kept in Crooks' personal office on the sixth floor of Zentil Keep. This left Jake in an undead state, forced to serve the imposter Crooks against his will. Yet Jake found ways to resist. When the Jolly Gents ambushed [[SWOJ]] at the subterranean lake beneath the Neverwinter chasm, Jake secretly dropped his obsidian tablet to Lark and whispered the words that would ultimately bring down Crooks' entire operation: "We're all prisoners of Crooks." The intelligence encoded in that tablet -- revealing the soul-harvesting conspiracy, the location of Zentil Keep in the [[Shadowfell]], and the true fate of the original Jolly Gents -- proved to be the single most important piece of information SWOJ received in Season 2.

At the Season 2 finale, during the God's Night celebration at [[Trollskull Manor]], Jake eulogized the fallen Jolly Gents and the memory of the original [[Rowan Crooks]]. He then performed [[Mordecai Gralhund]]'s soul-binding ritual alongside [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]], binding his soul to his beloved obsidian tablet and ending his undead existence. [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] recognized the ritual as her grandfather's invention -- the same magic originally designed to bind souls to objects, here repurposed as a final act of peace.

## Relationships

- **[[Rowan Crooks]]** -- Jake served under Crooks as part of the Jolly Gents, but the Crooks he served was actually the 14th clone of [[Manshoon]]. Jake's soul was bound in a phylactery and kept in Crooks' office, making him a prisoner rather than a willing servant. Jake's act of rebellion -- passing his tablet to Lark -- was instrumental in Crooks' downfall.
- **[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]** -- Jake developed a rapport with Lark during their time traveling together in the Feywild. Lark peppered Jake with endless questions about the Jolly Gents, Manshoon, and history, which Jake patiently answered. It was to Lark that Jake entrusted his obsidian tablet during the lake ambush, and Lark who later deciphered its contents to expose the conspiracy.
- **[[Sixteen String Jack]]** -- Fellow core member of the Jolly Gents. Jack and Jake fought alongside each other during the lake ambush and teleported away together with the stolen green power stones. Jack mocked Jake's combat abilities during the fight.
- **[[Little Joan]]** -- Fellow core member of the Jolly Gents. Joan participated in the lake ambush alongside Jake and Jack. She later defected to SWOJ's side and led the reformed Gents as [[Jolly Gents (The Ripples)|The Ripples]].
- **[[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]]** -- Jake and Ann Jane traveled together as SWOJ's Jolly Gents escorts through the Feywild and Shadowfell. They were both captured and replaced by podlings inside the [[The Great Beast of Erua|Great Beast of Erua]], then rescued together. At the Season 2 finale, the two of them performed the soul-binding ritual together to end their undead states.
- **[[Giggling Gus]]** -- A top-ranking Jolly Gents member. Jake and Gus appeared together in the [[Raven Queen]]'s dream sequence, leading the legendary assault on [[Xanathar's Lair|the Xanathar's lair]].
- **[[Friar Truck]]** -- The demonic cleric who maintained the phylactery laboratory where Jolly Gents souls were bound. Jake's copper orb phylactery was among the instruments of control Friar Truck oversaw.
- **[[SWOJ]]** -- Jake initially traveled with SWOJ as an ally in the Feywild, was later forced to fight them at the lake, and ultimately aided their cause by providing the intelligence that exposed Crooks and led to the liberation of the Jolly Gents.
- **[[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]** -- Ylka recognized the soul-binding ritual Jake performed at the finale as the invention of her grandfather, [[Mordecai Gralhund]].

## Role in the Story

### Feywild Companion (S02E01 -- S02E05)

When [[Rowan Crooks]] split off from SWOJ to pursue a separate lead on Manshoon, he left Jake, [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]], and the unicorn [[Lyrian]] to accompany the party through the Feywild. Jake proved a willing if somewhat detached companion -- he answered Lark's relentless questions about the Jolly Gents' history, claimed to have been present when the Gents fought Manshoon, and occasionally offered observations about the dangers of the Feywild. He was more interested in his notes than in helping with camp tasks, and Ann Jane kept her distance from both Lark and Jake. When the party entered the [[Shadow Way Inn]] in the Shadowfell, Jake and Ann Jane were captured outside and replaced by podlings -- plant-based duplicates controlled by the [[The Great Beast of Erua|Great Beast of Erua]]. The podling versions locked the door on SWOJ during the ensuing attack.

### Rescue and Reunion (S02E03 -- S02E05)

Inside the Great Beast of Erua, SWOJ discovered the real Jake captive in a glowing pod, his obsidian tablet nearby with a disembodied crawling hand scribbling on it. Once freed, Jake began an incantation to contact Rowan Crooks via a teleportation circle, believing they were close to finding Manshoon. When Crooks and the full Jolly Gents contingent arrived, Jake -- still groggy from his captivity -- helped prepare the teleportation circle that transported everyone to Waterdeep, where three years had passed in their absence.

### The Deep Dragon Temple (S02E10 -- S02E11)

SWOJ found Jake waiting for them at the bottom of the [[Deep Dragon Temple]] on Devil's Rock, working at a makeshift workstation. Overjoyed to have his obsidian tablet returned, Jake explained the history of the temple: an ancient battlefield where aboleths and dragons had warred, with Manshoon later tethering dragon spirits to serve his purposes. He accompanied SWOJ up the cliff face, advising them to mask up against the toxic green mist. At the top, when Rowan Crooks arrived with [[Little Joan]] and [[Friar Truck]] and revealed his true nature as Manshoon's clone, Jake was notably absent from the betrayal -- he remained below while SWOJ proceeded through the portals, suggesting he either was not trusted by Crooks for the ambush or was deliberately kept away.

### The Lake Ambush and the Act of Rebellion (S02E16)

Jake resurfaced alongside Little Joan and Sixteen String Jack at the subterranean lake beneath the Neverwinter chasm, where the three Jolly Gents ambushed SWOJ. During the battle, Jake fired magic missiles from a floating rock -- halfheartedly, by Jack's assessment. When Lark managed to reach Jake's rock, Jake made his move: he dropped his obsidian tablet and whispered, "We're all prisoners of Crooks. He plans to break the wall." Lark attempted to tie Jake up with rope to help him resist Crooks' control, and Jake allowed it -- but his eyes then turned white and opalescent, and he dissolved into mist, reappearing on a floating force cage with a green power stone. Jake, Jack, and the stolen stones teleported away, while Joan fell into the water.

### The Intelligence (S02E17 -- S02E23)

Jake's obsidian tablet became one of the most consequential items in Season 2. Lark investigated its contents across multiple episodes: it contained information about the chasm's planar instability and intersecting planes ([[S02E17 - Episode Forty Seven (S02E17)|S02E17]]), and its full decryption revealed the central conspiracy -- the original Jolly Gents were killed by Manshoon, their souls were harvested by Rowan Crooks, and the secret base was [[Zhentil Keep|Zentil Keep]], a floating fortress above the Shadowfell ([[S02E20 - Episode Fifty (S02E20)|S02E20]]). The tablet's information guided SWOJ to a secret [[Raven Queen]] shrine and onward to the Shadowfell ([[S02E21 - Episode Fifty One (S02E21)|S02E21]]). Lark used the tablet as a cover story to bluff past the sentries [[Rambling Bill]] and [[Long Mary]] at Zentil Keep's entrance ([[S02E22 - Episode Fifty Two LIVE (S02E22)|S02E22]]). Inside Crooks' office, SWOJ discovered Jake's phylactery -- a copper orb -- confirming that Jake was a prisoner whose soul was literally held captive. During the infiltration, Jake's tablet schematics also helped SWOJ understand Zentil Keep's infrastructure, and Jake advised that concentrated shatter spells could damage the phylactery devices ([[S02E23 - Episode Fifty Three (S02E23)|S02E23]]).

### The Dream and the Finale (S02E25, S02E38)

After Crooks' defeat, the [[Raven Queen]] tested SWOJ with a dream sequence that included a vision of the Jolly Gents' legendary assault on [[Xanathar's Lair|the Xanathar's lair]], with Jake and [[Giggling Gus]] leading the charge ([[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]]). At the God's Night celebration at [[Trollskull Manor]] following the Season 2 finale, Jake appeared alongside Ann Jane Flip-Flap. He parked next to Lark with his obsidian tablet and jotted down SWOJ's story of their adventures. On the rooftop that evening, Jake said a few words eulogizing the Jolly Gents who had died and the memory of the original Rowan Crooks. He and Ann Jane then performed [[Mordecai Gralhund]]'s soul-binding ritual -- Jake binding his soul to his obsidian tablet, Ann Jane to a knife -- ending their undead existence for good ([[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]]).

## Key Items

- **[[Obsidian Tablet (Jake's)|Obsidian Tablet]]** -- Jake's most prized possession, a magic stone tablet he had used to record notes for centuries using a magic chisel. It contained his own arcane shorthand, historical records of the Jolly Gents, intelligence on the aboleth-dragon conflict, schematics of Zentil Keep, and -- crucially -- the encoded truth about Manshoon's takeover of the Jolly Gents. Jake ultimately bound his soul to the tablet during the God's Night ritual.
- **Phylactery (Copper Orb)** -- The vessel in which [[Rowan Crooks]] bound Jake's soul, kept in Crooks' personal office on the sixth floor of [[Zhentil Keep|Zentil Keep]]. Its discovery by SWOJ confirmed Jake's status as a coerced prisoner.
