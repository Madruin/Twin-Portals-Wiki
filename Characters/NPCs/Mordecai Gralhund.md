# Mordecai Gralhund

Mordecai Gralhund was a wizard of [[House Gralhund]] and [[Ylka Gralhund]]'s grandfather -- a figure whose legacy casts a long shadow over the entire story of the [[Silver Waves of Justice]]. Though dead before the events of the campaign, Mordecai's arcane work shaped the fates of those who came after him: he created Ylka through the clone spell to protect her soul from dark forces within the Gralhund family, he helped [[Lif Lindgren]] find work at [[Trollskull Manor]], and he invented a soul-binding ritual powerful enough to give the immortal [[Antediluvian Jake]] and [[Angie Fliplap]] a path out of undeath. His initials "M.G." on a magical sword hidden in Lif's workshop were the first clue to a mystery that would take SWOJ dozens of episodes to unravel.

**First appearance:** [[S01E05 - Episode Five|S01E05]]
**Total appearances:** 7 episodes

## Description

Mordecai was a powerful wizard with deep knowledge of soul magic, cloning, and arcane ritual. He was Ylka's grandfather and a member of the influential Gralhund noble family of [[Waterdeep]]. While much of his life remains shrouded in mystery, what is known paints the picture of a man who turned against the darker elements within his own family -- and who went to extraordinary lengths to protect those he loved.

He maintained a secret laboratory in a walled-off section of the [[Trollskull Manor]] cellar, a space the party later discovered and kept hidden from outsiders. His connections in Waterdeep ran deep: he helped Lif find employment as a bartender at Trollskull Manor roughly 95 years before the campaign, and he was an associate of [[The Necromancer]], the young-looking wizard with a mechanical hand who led the Zentarim from the [[Kolat Towers]]. The Necromancer acknowledged owing Mordecai a debt, saying he owed Ylka's grandfather "for his help in the past."

Mordecai was also a musician. He sang a melody that Ylka remembered from childhood -- a tune so deeply embedded in her memory that when she heard the same song emanating from his enchanted sword in Lif's workshop, she recognized it instantly as something her grandfather used to sing to her.

## Role in the Story

- **[[S01E05 - Episode Five|S01E05]]:** SWOJ discovers [[Lif Lindgren]]'s hidden workshop in the Trollskull Manor cellar. Inside a small chest they find a diary, personal effects, and a short sword -- an ornate blade of blue steel with a gemstone on the hilt and a brass plaque bearing the initials "M.G." The sword is cold to the touch and clearly enchanted. When [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] swings it, the blade produces a melody. Ylka is struck by two realizations: the melody is one her grandfather used to sing to her, and the initials belong to her relative Mordecai. Lif's diary reveals that Mordecai helped him find his job at the tavern and that Lif was deeply grateful to him.
- **[[S01E22 - Episode Twenty Two|S01E22]]:** During Lark's resurrection at the Necromancer's sanctum beneath the Kolat Towers, Ylka channels her grandfather's music through her concertina. The Necromancer begins humming the same melody, demonstrating that he knew Mordecai personally. The Necromancer crushes a diamond over Lark's sarcophagus while his mechanical hand channels arcane patterns, completing the clone spell that restores Lark to life. After the ritual, the Necromancer tells Ylka directly: "I owe your grandfather for his help in the past."
- **[[S01E23 - Episode Twenty Three|S01E23]]:** The Necromancer reveals the truth about Ylka's origin. He explains that Ylka is a clone of [[Zylka]], created by her grandfather Mordecai to protect her soul from her own family. Mordecai hid Ylka's soul in the cloned body, then attempted to kill the original to prevent the Gralhund family's dark influences from reaching her. The Necromancer had assumed that was the end of it -- until Ylka reappeared. Zylka, the original body, is found shackled in the Necromancer's construct workshop, in a state of undeath with gray skin and clumpy hair, forced to work on [[Nim]] -- the Gralhund family golem.
- **[[S01E24 - Episode Twenty Four|S01E24]]:** In the aftermath of the Kolat Towers infiltration, Ylka's player (Beth) mentions the possibility of naming Ylka's new Tiny Hut spell after Mordecai, "or whoever the inspiration was for Ylka," reflecting the character's deepening connection to her grandfather's legacy.
- **[[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]]:** While discussing soul magic and resurrection, Ylka references the diamond that was crushed over her during "the Grandpa Mordecai resurrection" -- shorthand for the clone ritual the Necromancer performed using Mordecai's techniques.
- **[[S02E29 - Episode Fifty Nine LIVE (S02E29)|S02E29]]:** Ylka tells a new acquaintance "the process of Grandpa Mordecai" as shorthand for her own complicated origin story, summarizing roughly 70 episodes worth of backstory in a single phrase.
- **[[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]]:** In the Season 2 finale, Mordecai's legacy reaches its culmination in two ways. First, his old laboratory is confirmed to still exist in a walled-off section of the Trollskull Manor basement -- a space SWOJ keeps secret. Second, and more significantly, [[Antediluvian Jake]] and [[Angie Fliplap]] perform the soul-binding ritual that Mordecai invented. Ylka recognizes the magic as Gralhund family work -- specifically, "the ritual that your grandfather Mordecai invented to bind souls to objects." Jake binds his soul to his tablet and Angie to a knife, effectively ending their existence as undead beings and entering a state of half-life. The ritual stops them from being liches. Finally, Ylka declares her intention to investigate the Gralhund family demons and her grandfather's legacy, setting up a major thread for Season 3.

## Relationships

- **[[Ylka Gralhund]]** — His granddaughter, though in a deeper sense his creation. Mordecai cloned Ylka from [[Zylka]] and transplanted her soul into the new body to protect her from the dark forces within House Gralhund. He sang to her as a child, and his music lives on through her bardic magic. Ylka carries his enchanted sword and devotes herself to understanding his legacy.
- **[[Zylka]]** — The original body from which Ylka was cloned. Mordecai attempted to kill Zylka after creating the clone, to prevent the Gralhund family's influence from reaching Ylka's soul. Zylka survived in a state of undeath and was later held captive by the Necromancer.
- **[[Lif Lindgren]]** — Mordecai helped Lif find employment as a bartender at Trollskull Manor approximately 95 years before the campaign. Lif recorded his gratitude in his diary, and Mordecai's sword was stored in Lif's personal chest in the cellar workshop.
- **[[The Necromancer]]** — A fellow wizard who knew Mordecai personally and owed him a debt. The Necromancer knew the same melodies Mordecai sang and performed clone magic using related techniques. He took custody of Zylka and the Gralhund golem Nim after Mordecai's death.
- **[[Antediluvian Jake]]** — Used Mordecai's soul-binding ritual to end his undead state during the Season 2 finale, binding his soul to his tablet.
- **[[Angie Fliplap]]** — Used Mordecai's soul-binding ritual alongside Jake, binding her soul to a knife to enter a state of half-life rather than continuing as undead.
- **[[Nim]]** — The Gralhund family golem. Connected to Mordecai's legacy as a piece of Gralhund magical craftsmanship that Zylka was later forced to maintain.
- **[[House Gralhund]]** — Mordecai's noble family, from whom he sought to protect Ylka's soul. The nature of the "family demons" he was guarding against remains an unresolved mystery that Ylka intends to investigate.
