# Victoro Cassalanter

Noble patriarch of [[House Cassalanter]] and husband of [[Amalia Cassalanter]]. A wealthy Waterdeep lord who entered into a diabolical pact with [[Asmodeus]], sacrificing his children's souls for wealth and power. Served as a major antagonist throughout Season 1, wielding political influence, financial leverage, and arcane power to pursue the Cassalanter agenda. Ultimately defeated by [[Silver Waves Of Justice (SWOJ)|SWOJ]] and sentenced to life in prison.

**First appearance:** [[S01E09 - Episode Nine|S01E09]]
**Last appearance:** [[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]
**Total appearances:** 6 episodes (S01E09, S01E10, S01E12, S01E24, S01E27, S01E29)
**Status:** Imprisoned -- sentenced to life in prison
**Faction affiliations:** [[Cassalanter Family  Cult Of Asmodeus|Cassalanter Family / Cult of Asmodeus]], [[Cult Of Asmodeus]]

## Description

Lord of the Cassalanter household. Victoro presented the outward image of a refined Waterdeep noble, but beneath the surface he was a ruthless schemer who maintained a network of corruption spanning the [[City Watch]], the courts, and allied noble houses. He wielded a Ruby-Tipped Staff capable of causing the ground to shake and trapping enemies. His locked private office at [[Cassalanter Villa]] contained loan documents, ledgers of financial predation, and a gold [[Asmodeus Cloven Hoof Icon]] hidden in a secret compartment.

At the Founder's Day feast, Victoro used a [[Charming Cane]] to mesmerize the crowd with a ball of light and a [[Rod of Rulership]] to compel obedience. He was also capable of casting mirror image and summoning devils. A large portrait of him and Amalia hung in the villa library.

## Role in the Story

### The Pact with Asmodeus

Victoro and Amalia entered into a contract with [[Asmodeus]], Lord of Nessus, trading their children's souls for wealth and power. Their eldest son [[Osvaldo Cassalanter|Osvaldo]] was transformed into a chain devil as a direct consequence. The pact further demanded the souls of their remaining children, [[Terenzio Cassalanter|Terenzio]] and [[Elzerina Cassalanter|Elzerina]], unless a massive ransom of gold was delivered. This drove the Cassalanters to seek [[Lord Dagult Neverember|Dagult Neverember]]'s hidden gold hoard and to hire SWOJ as unwitting pawns.

### Political and Financial Corruption

Victoro authored a threatening letter (signed "V") to the [[House Gralhund|Gralhunds]] on gilded paper in red ink, confirming the Cassalanter-Gralhund alliance under the Cult of Asmodeus. The letter chastised the Gralhunds for the [[Zylka]] incident and warned that "the Lord of Nessus despises incompetence." Through the Cassalanter ledgers, it was later revealed that Victoro had city watch officers and magistrates on the family payroll, bought off judges assigned to cases involving their enemies, and engaged in financial predation -- including demanding 500% interest on loans to houses like [[House Wavesilver]].

### The Soiree (S01E09-S01E10)

SWOJ first met Victoro at the Cassalanter soiree, where he gambled with [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] at Tymora's Fancy and lost 160gp. When [[Zylka]] and cultists in golden masks crashed through the ballroom windows, Victoro fought the cultists using his Ruby-Tipped Staff, causing the room to shake and trapping them. He then rushed to the attic to help restrain his son Osvaldo after SWOJ discovered the chain devil imprisoned in the pentagram. In [[S01E10 - Episode Ten|S01E10]], Victoro and Amalia discussed their guilt over the Asmodeus deal with SWOJ, revealing Osvaldo's transformation.

### The Threatening Letter (S01E12)

[[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] found a letter from Victoro in [[Orond Gralhund]]'s study at [[Gralhund Villa]], confirming that Zylka was a Gralhund agent deployed at the Cassalanter party and that the Cassalanters arranged her escape from custody.

### Infiltration of the Villa (S01E24)

Though Victoro was not present during SWOJ's daytime infiltration, his presence loomed throughout. [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] picked Victoro's office lock (natural 20, total 30), bypassing a helmed horror trap, and discovered loan documents and the Asmodeus Cloven Hoof Icon in a secret compartment. [[Terenzio Cassalanter|Terenzio]] recognized the icon from his nightmares. A portrait of Victoro and Amalia hung in the beauty-and-the-beast-style library upstairs.

### The City Watch Army (S01E27)

After the Xanathar battle at the theater, SWOJ emerged to find a city watch army controlled by the Cassalanters. Victoro directed guards to arrest SWOJ, leveraging the family's deep corruption of Waterdeep's institutions. [[Bricks]] and [[Bracks]] -- ensorcelled by the Cassalanters -- betrayed [[Remalia Haventree]], and SWOJ was imprisoned in a high-security anti-magic dungeon for approximately one week.

### The Founder's Day Feast (S01E27)

At the Cassalanters' Founder's Day feast -- a front for a devil-soul harvest -- Victoro used his Charming Cane on the crowd, producing a ball of light that charmed most attendees. He summoned spined devils and imps to fight SWOJ. During combat, he cast mirror image. [[Mr. E]] attacked Victoro with flaming fists, dragon breath, and his lock chain, destroying two mirror images. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] confirmed the real Victoro and attacked. Victoro opened a portal to escape, but Scoot, Lark, and Mr. E struck him as he fled. Mr. E kicked the unconscious Victoro through his own portal into the temple of Asmodeus beneath the villa.

### Defeat and Sentencing (S01E29)

In the aftermath, Lark restrained the defeated Victoro and stripped him of his Ring of Protection and Rod of Rulership. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] identified the items. Ylka healed Victoro for interrogation. [[Ulkoria Stonemarrow]] coordinated the cleanup at the villa, discovering many city watch members on the Cassalanter payroll through the family's ledgers. Ylka delivered a legendary performance at the trial, and Victoro and Amalia Cassalanter were sentenced to life in prison. Their children Terenzio and Elzerina were placed under city protection and eventually joined the [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars]].
