# Mortimer Quill

**Mortimer Quill** is a skeletal clerk who serves as [[Jergal]]'s bureaucratic representative in the [[Trial Of Jergal]], deep within [[The Shrouded Citadel]]. Immaculately dressed despite his undead nature, he presides over an afterlife courtroom where the fates of recently deceased souls are adjudicated. Dry, officious, and thoroughly procedural, Mortimer runs his court with the weary patience of an overworked civil servant who has been processing the dead for far too long.

**First appearance:** [[S02E29 - Episode Fifty Nine LIVE (S02E29)]]
**Total appearances:** 1 episode

## Description

Mortimer Quill is an undead figure with deeply sunken eyes -- holes where living eyes once were -- and an immaculately dressed appearance that sets him apart from the lesser skeleton clerks under his supervision. He sits at the head desk of a bureaucratic afterlife office filled with skeletons scribbling at smaller desks, their bony fingers clicking and scratching as they draft proposed afterlife plans for the recently deceased. The room smells of old parchment with a hint of brimstone, lit by pale, flickering ghostly light. His manner is formal and measured; he consults the "Tome of Transcendental Technicalities" and speaks in legalistic language, citing interdimensional accords and metaphysical precedent. Despite the absurdity of his courtroom, he takes his role with complete seriousness.

## Role in the Story

- **[[S02E29 - Episode Fifty Nine LIVE (S02E29)|S02E29]]:** After [[Scoot Sparkles|SWOJ]] falls hundreds of feet into [[The Shrouded Citadel]] following the phylactery explosion, they encounter Mortimer Quill's bureaucratic courtroom as the first of four divine trials. Mortimer greets SWOJ and explains the proceedings: a literal trial in which they must argue for the fate of recently deceased souls against a team of three skeletal lawyers carrying briefcases. In the first case, SWOJ argues for the souls of seven cultists killed by otyughs. Rather than letting the skeletal lawyers consign them to undeath, [[Scoot Sparkles]] and [[Ylka Gralhund]] persuade Mortimer to allow the cultists to be reincarnated as beasts of burden on ethical farmsteads, arguing they deserve a chance to learn the value of honest work. Mortimer rules in SWOJ's favor and bans all unauthorized transmigration until further notice. In the second and more critical case, the court must decide the fate of several dragon souls recently freed from the cracked phylactery. The skeletal lawyers argue the souls are bound to return to the phylactery by ritual contract; SWOJ counters that the dragons never consented. [[Zelifarn]] provides testimony via [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s necklace, arguing that dragons as primordial creatures are exempt from ethereal plane zoning ordinances. Mortimer rules that the plaintiffs' position conflicts with the "International Interdimensional Accord of Afterlife Affairs" and orders the dragon spirits freed to the [[Preserve Of The Ancients]]. He then warns SWOJ that the next challenge, the [[Trial Of Orcus]], will not be as civil.

## Relationships

- **[[Jergal]]** -- Mortimer's master, the Lord of the End of Everything. Mortimer serves as his representative and administers justice in his name.
- **[[Scoot Sparkles]]**, **[[Ylka Gralhund]]**, **[[Malark Wavesilver]]**, **[[Xaerine Rumblestride]]** -- The defendants in his court. SWOJ argues two cases before Mortimer and wins both through persuasion.
- **[[Zelifarn]]** -- Provides key testimony during the dragon souls case, helping SWOJ win the ruling to free the spirits.
- **[[Kelemvor]]** -- The current god of the dead in the Forgotten Realms; Jergal predates him as an older death deity.
- **[[Orcus]]** -- The demonic Lord of Undeath, whose trial follows Mortimer's. Mortimer warns SWOJ that Orcus will not be as polite as Jergal.
