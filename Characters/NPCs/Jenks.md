# Jenks

A half-orc orphan who grew from a self-proclaimed "great wizard" wielding a stick with a continual flame candle into an actual wizard-in-training. One of the three [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars]] alongside [[Nat]] and [[Squidly]], and interpreter of Nat's sign language.

**First appearance:** [[S01E05 - Episode Five|S01E05]]
**Total appearances:** 10 episodes
**Faction affiliations:** [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars]]

## Description

Jenks is a half-orc boy who was around nine years old when the [[Silver Waves of Justice]] first encountered him in [[S01E05 - Episode Five|S01E05]], living as a street urchin in the abandoned [[Trollskull Manor]] alongside [[Nat]] and [[Squidly]]. He introduced himself with characteristic bravado as "the Great Wizard Jenks," carrying a stick topped with a continual flame candle as his "magic wand" and speaking of himself in the third person with theatrical grandiosity. Despite the bluster, Jenks served a vital role in the trio: he was the only one who could interpret [[Nat]]'s sign language, translating her commands and thoughts for outsiders through what he described as "our own secret language that no one else knows."

Jenks proved resourceful beyond his years. After the catastrophic [[Trollskull Alley fireball]], he was found burned and shell-shocked against a wall near [[Vincent Trench]]'s office, but recovered quickly enough to sneak past the Watch barricades and deliver a critical piece of evidence to [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] -- a partially spent [[Necklace of Fireballs]] he had found hidden in a barrel. He also demonstrated unusual perceptiveness, once blurting out Lark's full name ("Malark Wavesilver") before the rogue himself had remembered it, later attributing this to something that "came to me in a dream."

Following SWOJ's three-year disappearance into the [[The Shadowfell|Shadowfell]], Jenks grew into a lanky, disproportionate teenager -- still wearing his oversized wizard hat, but now competently managing the [[Short Rest Tavern (Trollskull Manor)|Short Rest]]. He helped [[Tagga]] run the bar, prepared food for patrons, served coffee, and kept the stables clean. Though he retained his flamboyant persona, he had become genuinely dependable.

By the final arc of the campaign, Jenks had begun formally studying wizardry. He arrived at the [[Radiant Citadel]] aboard a hammerhead ship alongside [[Nat]], now wearing a distinctive blue wizard hat and serving as the primary spellcaster among the allied reinforcements sent from [[Waterdeep]]. The boy who once pretended to cast spells with a stick had become a real wizard.

## Role in the Story

- **[[S01E05 - Episode Five|S01E05]]:** SWOJ discovers three street urchins living in [[Trollskull Manor]]: [[Nat]] (mute leader), Jenks (half-orc "great wizard"), and [[Squidly]] (tiefling with eye patch). Jenks introduces himself to [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] by name and interprets [[Nat]]'s sign language for the party. He explains that Nat found a glowing talisman under the tower floorboards and that they believe it is connected to the hag [[Esther]]. The party ventures into the attic with Jenks and Nat to rescue [[Squidly]] from the hag's cauldron.
- **[[S01E07 - Episode Seven|S01E07]]:** The three orphans are formally hired as the [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars]]. Jenks approaches the party in his "sanctimonious manner" with his wand-stick and negotiates terms on behalf of the group after [[Nat]] signs the instructions to him: 10 gold per tenday for running errands and gathering information.
- **[[S01E11 - Episode Eleven|S01E11]]:** Jenks suggests [[Hrabbaz]], the [[Gralhund Villa|Gralhund]] bodyguard known for hurting children, as a potential body for [[Terry]] the intellect devourer. When the [[Trollskull Alley fireball]] devastates the neighborhood during the grand opening of the Short Rest, Jenks is found burned and shell-shocked against a wall but physically unharmed. After recovering, he sneaks past the Watch barricades and delivers a partially spent [[Necklace of Fireballs]] to [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]], having found it hidden in a barrel -- a critical piece of evidence in the fireball investigation.
- **[[S01E12 - Episode Twelve|S01E12]]:** Jenks contacts [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] via sending stone, calling in as "the Great Wizard Jenks" and reporting that [[Renaer Neverember]] has arrived at the Short Rest with company. He is present with [[Nat]] and [[Squidly]] when SWOJ returns to the tavern.
- **[[S01E16 - Episode Sixteen|S01E16]]:** Jenks and [[Nat]] are roughhousing upstairs at the Short Rest. [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] sends them downstairs to keep an eye on suspicious patrons. Jenks declares "the great wizard Jenks is on the case" before heading down to sit and play with his wand. Later, [[Squidly]] references something "Jenks was saying" about the [[Cassalanters]].
- **[[S01E22 - Episode Twenty Two|S01E22]]:** Jenks and [[Nat]] encounter SWOJ outside [[Renaer Neverember]]'s apartment. Jenks performs the Irregulars' secret gang sign with his typical grandiosity, slamming his stick on the ground and announcing "the great wizard Jenks welcomes you." He and Nat report that [[Squidly]] has gone missing and agree to keep searching for him.
- **[[S01E23 - Episode Twenty Three|S01E23]]:** When SWOJ finds [[Squidly]] with [[Urstul Floxxin]], they tell him that "the great wizard Janks and Nat" miss him, which gives Squidly pause.
- **[[S02E05 - Episode Thirty Five (S02E05)|S02E05]]:** Three years have passed. Jenks has grown into a lanky, disproportionate teenager, still wearing his big wizard hat but now competently managing the [[Short Rest Tavern (Trollskull Manor)|Short Rest]]. He prepares food, serves patrons, and keeps the stables clean. [[Tagga]] credits him, [[Nat]], and [[Squidly]] for helping keep things running during SWOJ's absence. Jenks tells the party that Squidly has become "a real hustler" working odd jobs around the city. He also asks [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] about the [[Zhentarim]] situation. When Lark asks Jenks how he once knew Lark's full name before Lark himself did, Jenks explains it "came to me in a dream" and offers to look into it further.
- **[[S02E35 - Episode Sixty Five (S02E35)|S02E35]]:** Jenks arrives at the [[Radiant Citadel]] aboard a hammerhead ship, riding side-saddle on a griffin alongside [[Nat]], now wearing a distinctive blue wizard hat. He waves and calls out "we're here to help" in a booming voice. He serves as the primary spellcaster among the allied reinforcements: approximately 30 soldiers, 5 casters, and animal scouts sent from [[Waterdeep]]. When [[Nat]] selects a griffin for SWOJ to ride, Jenks good-naturedly gives up his spot.
- **[[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]]:** In the Season Two epilogue, Jenks is at [[Trollskull Manor]] in [[Waterdeep]], now in his mid-teens. He is formally studying to become a wizard -- the dream he carried since childhood finally becoming reality. He and [[Nat]] are described as having grown up under [[Tagga]]'s care at the Short Rest.

## Relationships

- **[[Nat]]** -- Fellow orphan, closest companion, and the leader of their trio. Jenks serves as Nat's interpreter, translating her sign language for outsiders. They grow up together at the Short Rest and later serve side-by-side at the [[Radiant Citadel]], with Nat as a [[Griffin Riders of Waterdeep|Griffin Rider]] and Jenks as a wizard.
- **[[Squidly]]** -- The third member of the orphan trio. When Squidly disappears in Season 1, Jenks and Nat report his absence to SWOJ. Jenks later describes the teenage Squidly as "a real hustler" who is always out and about.
- **[[Tagga]]** -- [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]'s mother, who effectively raised Jenks and the other orphans at the Short Rest during SWOJ's three-year absence. By the Season Two finale, Jenks is described as one of Tagga's children.
- **[[Malark Wavesilver]]** -- Lark was the first member of SWOJ to speak with Jenks, and they developed a particular rapport. Jenks uses the sending stone to report to Lark, and once mysteriously knew Lark's full name before the rogue himself remembered it.
- **[[Terry]]** -- The intellect devourer allied with SWOJ. Jenks suggested [[Hrabbaz]] as a potential new body for Terry, calling him an "enemy of all the children of Waterdeep."
- **[[Lif Lindgren]]** -- The ghost of the Short Rest, who helped watch over the tavern and the children.
- **[[Silver Waves of Justice]]** -- SWOJ hired Jenks and the Irregulars, gave them a home, and watched them grow from street urchins into capable young adults.
