# Rowan Crooks

> *"You have outlived your usefulness."*
> -- Rowan Crooks, unmasking himself to [[SWOJ]] inside the [[The Breathless Elder|Breathless Elder]] ([[S02E11 - Episode Forty One (S02E11)|S02E11]])

**First appearance:** [[S01E21 - Episode Twenty One|S01E21]] (Malar's realm, Feywild)
**Final appearance:** [[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]] (trapped in the [[The Stone of Golorr|Soul Stone]])
**Total appearances:** 23 episodes
**Species/Type:** Human (original) / Undead Manshoon clone (imposter)
**Affiliation:** [[The Jolly Gents]] (leader), [[Zhentarim]] (secretly), [[Manshoon]] (true identity)
**Base of operations:** [[Zhentil Keep]] (floating fortress, [[Shadowfell]])
**Status:** Destroyed -- soul trapped in the [[The Stone of Golorr|Soul Stone]] and delivered to the [[Raven Queen]] ([[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]])

## Overview

Rowan Crooks is the name shared by two distinct figures in the Twin Portals campaign: the **genuine Rowan Crooks**, a legendary mustachioed folk hero who fought [[Manshoon]] across the Sword Coast, and the **14th clone of Manshoon**, who murdered the real Crooks, assumed his identity, and corrupted [[The Jolly Gents]] into a soul-harvesting operation. Under this imposter's leadership, the Jolly Gents were transformed from celebrated adventurers into instruments of Manshoon's will -- their members' souls bound in phylacteries, their heroic reputation weaponized as cover for the clone's machinations.

The imposter Rowan Crooks served as the primary antagonist of Season 2 of the Twin Portals campaign. His betrayal of [[SWOJ]] inside the [[The Breathless Elder|Breathless Elder]] ([[S02E11 - Episode Forty One (S02E11)|S02E11]]) -- where he murdered [[Shoonie|Shuni]], killed SWOJ, looted their corpses, and teleported away -- was the campaign's most devastating turning point. SWOJ's subsequent resurrection, infiltration of [[Zhentil Keep]], and final battle against Crooks across the collapsing fortress constituted the climax of the season's central arc.

### Out-of-Game Origin

Rowan Crooks began as a piece of character flavor invented by Justin for [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s backstory -- a fictional folk hero that Lark idolized, inspired by pulp comics and fantasy novels about "Rowan Crooks and his Jolly Gents." The name itself was drawn from a real Duluth, Minnesota newspaper article about a bar fight from roughly a hundred years ago; many of the Jolly Gents' names (including [[Antediluvian Jake]]) came from the list of people arrested in that incident. DM Matias then fleshed out Crooks' actual achievements in the world to give him context, which naturally tied him into the Manshoon storyline. What started as a minor backstory detail grew into the bridge between Season 1 and Season 2 and the foundation of the campaign's central villain ([[S01E30 - Episode Thirty|S01E30]]).

## The Real Rowan Crooks

The genuine Rowan Crooks was a mustachioed rogue and folk hero celebrated across the Sword Coast. He founded [[The Jolly Gents]] as a heroic adventuring company and was known for his dashing exploits -- stealing from the rich and giving to the poor, defeating monsters, and most famously staging a legendary assault on the [[Xanathar's Lair|Xanathar's lair]]. He had his own trading cards, broadsheet comics, and ballads written about his exploits. His signature items included a feathered hat, a rapier, and two silver knives engraved "Rowan" and "Crooks."

The real Crooks fought [[Manshoon]] on at least two occasions and defeated him, which led to Manshoon splitting into his thirteen clones. Crooks then spent years hunting these clones with the Jolly Gents. His downfall was engineered by [[Friar Truck]], a demonic cleric who secretly served the "Lord of Nexus." Truck manipulated Crooks into staging a doomed assault on [[Zhentil Keep]], which led to his capture and replacement by the 14th Manshoon clone ([[S02E24 - Episode Fifty Four (S02E24)|S02E24]]).

Before his death, the real Crooks traveled to the [[Radiant Citadel]] in the Ethereal Plane, where he arrived as a folk hero and won the trust of the speakers. He introduced the soul pylons -- magical infrastructure that he built to help the Citadel -- before mysteriously disappearing. Manshoon later took over and weaponized the pylon infrastructure Crooks had built ([[S02E35 - Episode Sixty Five (S02E35)|S02E35]]).

The real Crooks' original journal -- detailing his heroic ideals and his fight against Manshoon -- was discovered by [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] in the print shop beneath [[Zhentil Keep]], where ghost archivists were being forced to produce propaganda ([[S02E23 - Episode Fifty Three (S02E23)|S02E23]]). Lark gave this journal to [[Little Joan]], who used it to rally Jolly Gents defectors against the imposter.

### Cultural Legacy

Rowan Crooks was a household name on the Sword Coast:

- **Trading cards** -- Collectible cards depicting Crooks and the Jolly Gents, including rare "super foil alternate art" variants. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] carried an unopened pack throughout the campaign and burned them in tribute at the Season 2 finale ([[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]]).
- **Broadsheet comics** -- Serialized adventure stories published about the Jolly Gents' exploits. Lark was an avid reader.
- **Ballads** -- Songs about Crooks' defeats of Manshoon. [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] weaponized these as Vicious Mockery material, which proved especially effective against the [[The Body Taker|Body Taker]] clone, who was enraged by any mention of Crooks' name ([[S02E04 - Episode Thirty Four (S02E04)|S02E04]]).
- **Posters** -- SWOJ had Rowan Crooks posters covering holes in the walls of [[Trollskull Manor]] ([[S01E02 - Episode Two|S01E02]]).
- Lark idolized Crooks so deeply that he modeled his own vigilante activities -- stealing from the rich to give to the poor -- on Crooks' example ([[S01E30 - Episode Thirty|S01E30]]).

## The Imposter: The 14th Manshoon Clone

### Appearance and Mannerisms

The Manshoon clone replicated the real Crooks' appearance convincingly: mustachioed, dashing, wearing a feathered hat that stayed magically fixed to his head even in combat. He carried a rapier and wrist-mounted bracers that produced throwing knives. He presented himself as charming and heroic -- bowing with flourishes, signing autographs ("with love and admiration, Rowan Crooks -- for justice"), and performing impressive physical feats like backflips during combat.

In his final battle atop [[Zhentil Keep]], the facade dropped. He muttered villain monologues under his breath ("nobody ever loved me," "this world is meaningless"), revealing the bitterness underneath the stolen persona ([[S02E24 - Episode Fifty Four (S02E24)|S02E24]]).

### Abilities and Combat

The imposter Crooks was a powerful combatant with legendary actions:

- **Spellcasting:** Finger of death, cone of cold, hold person, misty step, disintegrate (attempted)
- **Weapons:** Rapier, wrist-mounted throwing daggers
- **Special abilities:** Legendary actions (multiple attacks per round), apparent immunity or high resistance to explosive damage (survived the Breathless Elder explosion unscathed), flight/hovering capability, teleportation circle carving
- **Equipment:** Feathered hat (magically adhered), cloak, the [[Hungering Shard]] (backpack-mounted), soul stones for capturing spirits
- **Undead nature:** [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]'s chill touch gave him disadvantage as undead ([[S02E24 - Episode Fifty Four (S02E24)|S02E24]])

### The Plan

Crooks' ultimate goal was to use the [[Hungering Shard]] at the [[Radiant Citadel]] to reach [[Kelemvor]]'s [[Kelemvor's Wall Of The Faithless|Wall of the Faithless]] and break through it, freeing the remaining fragments of Manshoon's soul trapped on the other side. The soul pylons the real Crooks had built at the Radiant Citadel were to be weaponized for this purpose. The plan was recorded in [[Friar Truck]]'s notes found in his laboratory beneath [[Zhentil Keep]] ([[S02E24 - Episode Fifty Four (S02E24)|S02E24]]).

## Role in the Story

### Season 1: The Folk Hero (Lore and First Encounter)

Rowan Crooks first appears in the campaign as a figure of legend -- the subject of Lark's hero worship, referenced through trading cards, broadsheet comics, and posters at [[Trollskull Manor]]. Scoot and Ylka are only dimly aware of who he is at first ("I still don't know who Rowan Crooks is" -- [[S01E02 - Episode Two|S01E02]]).

SWOJ meets Crooks in person for the first time in [[Malar]]'s wild, stormy afterlife realm, where he and the Jolly Gents are hunting tribesfolk who have been killing animals. He provides information about the plane, gives gifts -- including two impossibly sharp silver knives engraved "Rowan" and "Crooks," a net of far-realm jute, and a spell -- and guides the party through the Feywild before parting ways ([[S01E21 - Episode Twenty One|S01E21]]).

The Jolly Gents arrive dramatically at [[Trollskull Manor]] on the summer solstice through a Feywild portal guided by [[Lyrian]]. Crooks extracts a Gem of Seeing from [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s temple -- a device the Necromancer had planted to spy on SWOJ -- and reveals that the Necromancer's true identity is [[Manshoon]], founder of the [[Zhentarim]]. This act of apparent heroism cements the alliance ([[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]).

### Season 2: The Alliance (S02E01 -- S02E05)

SWOJ travels the Feywild with the Jolly Gents, hunting Manshoon clones. Crooks splits off with part of his crew, leaving [[Antediluvian Jake]], [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]], and [[Lyrian]] with the party ([[S02E01 - Episode Thirty One (S02E01)|S02E01]]).

When SWOJ encounters [[The Body Taker]] inside the [[Dynamo of Possibility]], [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] weaponizes Crooks' ballads as Vicious Mockery, exploiting the clone's extreme sensitivity to Crooks' name ("Don't mention that name!") ([[S02E04 - Episode Thirty Four (S02E04)|S02E04]]).

Crooks and the full Jolly Gents contingent rejoin SWOJ after being contacted via incantation by [[Antediluvian Jake]]. Inside the [[The Great Beast of Erua|Great Beast of Erua]], Crooks captures [[Fetch]]'s soul in a crystal shard. [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] attacks Crooks to protect Fetch, but [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] deflects the blow. [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] briefly takes the shard before surrendering it. [[Radecere]] casts Geas on Crooks to safeguard Fetch's safety. Jake teleports everyone to Waterdeep, where three years have passed ([[S02E05 - Episode Thirty Five (S02E05)|S02E05]]).

### Season 2: The Betrayal (S02E11 -- S02E12)

Inside the [[The Breathless Elder|Breathless Elder]] in the Far Realm, after SWOJ meets the cooperative Manshoon clone [[Shoonie|Shuni]], Crooks arrives with [[Little Joan]] and [[Friar Truck]]. Three figures drop down from the Elder's interior, absorbing Shuni's soul into a black crystal.

Crooks unmasks himself: "You have outlived your usefulness." The [[The Manshoon Compass|Manshoon Compass]] points directly at him. [[Friar Truck]] and [[Little Joan]] roll initiative alongside him. In the chaotic battle, Crooks throws daggers from wrist-mounted bracers, attacks with his rapier, and demonstrates legendary actions. He seems "totally unfazed" by the energy of the Elder, his cloak billowing while his feathered hat glows magically.

[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]], heartbroken and raging at his fallen hero, severs the Elder's power gem with the "Rowan Crooks dagger" -- still calling it that because "Rowan Crooks is still my hero and not fake Manshoon Rowan Crooks." The resulting catastrophic explosion kills SWOJ and [[Friar Truck]]. [[Little Joan]] barely survives. Crooks is completely unaffected.

As SWOJ's spectral souls hover helplessly over their own corpses, Crooks methodically loots their bodies -- taking the bag of holding, all their possessions, and [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s hat (which "doesn't come off," but Crooks worked at it). He recaptures [[Shoonie|Shuni]]'s ghost in a cracked soul stone, takes the barely-alive Little Joan, carves a teleportation circle, and teleports away, leaving SWOJ as spirits in the Far Realm ([[S02E11 - Episode Forty One (S02E11)|S02E11]], [[S02E12 - Episode Forty Two (S02E12)|S02E12]]).

### Season 2: The Intelligence (S02E16 -- S02E20)

During the Jolly Gents' ambush at the subterranean lake beneath the Neverwinter chasm, [[Antediluvian Jake]] secretly passes his obsidian tablet to [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] and whispers: "We're all prisoners of Crooks" ([[S02E16 - Episode Forty Six LIVE (S02E16)|S02E16]]).

When Lark deciphers the tablet, it reveals the full scope of the conspiracy: the original Jolly Gents were killed by Manshoon in the original attack when Manshoon split into clones. Rowan Crooks -- the imposter -- harvested their souls in phylacteries and had been killing and reviving them for years. Over time, the negative energy of their prison corrupted their spirits, making them easier to control. Some resisted (Jake used his tablet as a bulwark against this change), but most did what Crooks demanded. The secret base was [[Zhentil Keep]], a floating fortress above the [[Shadowfell]] ([[S02E20 - Episode Fifty (S02E20)|S02E20]]).

### Season 2: Zentil Keep and the Fall of Crooks (S02E22 -- S02E25)

SWOJ infiltrated [[Zhentil Keep]] disguised in Jolly Gents cloaks and bronze rings. While Crooks was initially away ("Crooks has been gone for a while. I bet he'll be back soon" -- [[Rambling Bill]]), he returned to the keep by horn-blast, prompting excitement among the Gents ("Crooks is back! That's going to be a party tonight!" -- [[S02E22 - Episode Fifty Two LIVE (S02E22)|S02E22]]).

Inside the keep, SWOJ discovered:

- **Crooks' sixth-floor office:** His feathered hats hung on hooks, [[Antediluvian Jake]]'s phylactery (a copper orb), and operational documents
- **The print shop:** Ghost archivists forced to produce Crooks propaganda, and the real Crooks' original journal
- **Friar Truck's laboratory:** Over a hundred phylacteries binding Jolly Gents souls, clone tanks growing Rowan Crooks clones in various stages of development, and Truck's notes revealing he orchestrated the real Crooks' downfall ([[S02E23 - Episode Fifty Three (S02E23)|S02E23]], [[S02E24 - Episode Fifty Four (S02E24)|S02E24]])

[[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] persuaded [[Little Joan]] to defect by appealing to her doubts about Crooks ("You don't feel good about being with Rowan"). Joan rallied approximately 20-30 Gents to SWOJ's side.

Crooks addressed the assembled Jolly Gents from beside the Soul Engine, his voice amplified, announcing his plan regarding the [[Kelemvor's Wall Of The Faithless|Wall of the Faithless]]. SWOJ confronted him publicly. Crooks responded: "To my old friends, the Silver Waves of Justice, your treachery is at an end. You, allies of the Necromancer Manshoon the masked."

The loyal Gents shouted "For Rowan! And for the Silver Waves of Justice!" as the civil war erupted. [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] jammed [[Little Dinky]] into the soul engine, destabilizing the island. The fortress began to collapse.

#### The Sky Battle

As [[Zhentil Keep]] plummeted through the [[Shadowfell]], the final battle played out across falling rubble:

- Crooks flew away from the melee, perching on wreckage, firing spells from above
- [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] cast faerie fire, turning Crooks pink, and summoned eight giant constrictor snakes around him
- Crooks used hold person on [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]], snatching the [[Hungering Shard]] via invisible force
- [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] cast chill touch, giving Crooks disadvantage as undead, then rammed her sunblade into him before falling unconscious
- [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] launched himself at Crooks using [[Little Dinky]] as a propulsion rod, striking with his rapier
- [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] cast moonbeam directly onto Crooks and used dimension door to tackle him onto a platform
- Crooks muttered under his breath: "Nobody ever loved me... this world is meaningless"

**The killing blow:** [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] launched himself upward and killed Crooks with his lightning-split axe, shouting "Bentos!" -- dealing 10 lightning damage. [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] caught the [[Hungering Shard]] as it fell from Crooks' hands ([[S02E24 - Episode Fifty Four (S02E24)|S02E24]]).

Crooks' phylactery was destroyed and his soul was trapped in the [[The Stone of Golorr|Soul Stone]], causing the last structural supports of [[Zhentil Keep]] to fail. The fortress crashed into a Shadowfell canyon. When the dust settled, SWOJ found themselves surrounded by the wreckage of "Rowan Crooks' laboratory and the keep and everything else."

A shadowy, bound spectral Rowan Crooks appeared before SWOJ with the Soul Stone resting on top of him. A ten-foot-tall crow-winged woman -- the [[Raven Queen]] -- landed opposite him. Scoot declared: "We got him, your majesty" ([[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]]).

### Season 2: Endgame Legacy (S02E35 -- S02E38)

At the [[Radiant Citadel]], [[Sholeh]] revealed the history of the real Rowan Crooks: how he arrived as a folk hero, built the soul pylons, and then disappeared before Manshoon corrupted his work. She warned that the original Manshoon "knew words of power, dangerous stuff -- it's how he defeated the original Rowan Crooks" ([[S02E35 - Episode Sixty Five (S02E35)|S02E35]]).

During the final battle, [[Manshoon]] desperately searched [[Arcturia]]'s portable hole for "Rowan Crooks' Soul Stone" -- the key to his plan for godhood. He did not find it in time ([[S02E36 - Episode Sixty Six (S02E36)|S02E36]]).

At the God's Night celebration marking the end of Season 2, [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] burned an unopened pack of Rowan Crooks trading cards in the campfire, catching a glimpse of a "super rare Rowan Crooks" card -- alternate art, super foil, reading "you're free" -- before it was consumed. [[Antediluvian Jake]] spoke a quiet eulogy memorializing the original Jolly Gents and the original Rowan Crooks. [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] burned his Jolly Gents outfit in their memory ([[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]]).

## Key Relationships

### With [[SWOJ]]

The imposter Crooks cultivated SWOJ's trust by performing acts of apparent heroism -- extracting the Gem of Seeing, revealing Manshoon's identity, giving gifts. [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] in particular was deeply emotionally invested, having idolized Crooks since childhood. The betrayal was devastating for Lark, who continued calling his silver dagger "the Rowan Crooks dagger" even after the reveal, because "Rowan Crooks is still my hero and not fake Manshoon Rowan Crooks."

### With [[The Jolly Gents]]

The imposter controlled the Gents through a combination of charisma, coercion, and soul-binding. Many members were not willing servants -- [[Antediluvian Jake]] risked everything to pass intelligence to SWOJ, and [[Little Joan]] defected when given the opportunity. Others, like [[Pill Garlic]] (described as one of Crooks' "closest lieutenants") and [[Slim Curly]], served as loyal operatives. [[Friar Truck]] was the hidden architect who enabled the entire deception, having orchestrated the real Crooks' downfall and replacement.

### With [[Manshoon]]

Crooks was the 14th and final clone of Manshoon. His plan -- to reach the [[Kelemvor's Wall Of The Faithless|Wall of the Faithless]] and free the remaining Manshoon soul fragments -- served the greater Manshoon entity's ambitions. After the imposter Crooks was destroyed, the "last surviving clone" of Manshoon continued to pursue this plan at the [[Radiant Citadel]], desperately seeking the real Crooks' soul stone ([[S02E36 - Episode Sixty Six (S02E36)|S02E36]]).

### With the [[Raven Queen]]

SWOJ delivered the defeated Crooks' soul to the Raven Queen, who accepted custody of him in the Soul Stone. Later, Manshoon's soul was also sent to the Raven Queen as final punishment.

## Significant Items

- **Two silver knives** -- Engraved "Rowan" and "Crooks," impossibly sharp. Given to SWOJ in the Feywild. Lark took the one reading "Rowan" and later lashed it to [[Little Dinky]] as an "extendo shank" weapon.
- **The feathered hat** -- His signature headwear, magically adhered. Multiple hats found hung on hooks in his office at [[Zhentil Keep]].
- **Rapier** -- His primary melee weapon.
- **Wrist-mounted bracers** -- Produced throwing knives on command.
- **The [[Hungering Shard]]** -- Carried in a backpack during the final battle. The key to his plan to break the Wall of the Faithless.
- **Soul stones** -- Used to capture spirits, including [[Fetch]]'s soul and [[Shoonie|Shuni]]'s ghost.
- **Rowan Crooks' original journal** -- The real Crooks' diary, found in the print shop. Given by Lark to [[Little Joan]] to rally defectors.
- **The [[The Stone of Golorr|Soul Stone]]** -- The vessel in which the imposter's soul was ultimately trapped after his phylactery's destruction.

## Raven Queen Dream: The Xanathar Vision

During the [[Raven Queen]]'s dream sequence following Crooks' defeat, SWOJ relived the Jolly Gents' legendary assault on the [[Xanathar's Lair|Xanathar's lair]]. Lark was dressed as Rowan Crooks, Ylka as [[Sixteen String Jack]], Xae as [[Friar Truck]], and Scoot as [[Little Joan]]. The Xanathar appeared wearing Crooks' signature hat and spoke in Crooks' voice: "You will always walk in the shadow of my heroism." A musician called "the Crooked" played a distant melody. The dream tested the party's ability to act heroically while wearing the identities of those who had betrayed them ([[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]]).
