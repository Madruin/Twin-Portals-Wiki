# Urstul Floxxin

Urstul Floxxin is a [[Zhentarim]] assassin and operative who served under [[Manshoon]] throughout both seasons of the campaign. Originally an antagonist who repeatedly clashed with [[Scoot Sparkles|SWOJ]], he gradually developed an unlikely rapport with the party -- particularly [[Scoot Sparkles]] -- and eventually defected to fight alongside them against his former master.

**First appearance:** [[S01E11 - Episode Eleven]] (unnamed, as the burned Zhentarim agent)
**First named appearance:** [[S01E22 - Episode Twenty Two]]
**Total appearances:** 11 episodes
**Faction affiliations:** [[Zhentarim]] (Manshoon's division, later defected)

## Description

Urstul is a human man with a heavily scarred face -- burns sustained during the fireball explosion at [[Trollskull Manor (Short Rest Tavern)|Trollskull Alley]] in Season 1. He is recognizable by his distinctive **red hat**, similar in style to [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s own hat but "not quite as nice." He wears a black cloak and black leather armor typical of Zhentarim agents.

He is an accomplished assassin who fights with **poison daggers**, **exploding crossbow bolts**, and carries multiple **vials of blade-coating poison** (2d6 poison damage, lasting about one minute). He possesses a notable supernatural ability -- likely via magical tattoos -- that allows him to **melt through floors and into shadows**, disappearing from sight entirely. He mentions getting these tattoos from "a guy" and confirms they are the source of his ability to phase through surfaces.

Originally from [[Amphail]], Urstul is described as surly and aggressive but not without a sense of humor. Over the course of the campaign, his relationship with SWOJ shifts from outright hostility to grudging respect and, eventually, genuine loyalty.

## Role in the Story

### Season 1: The Zhentarim Operative

- **[[S01E11 - Episode Eleven]]:** Following the devastating **fireball explosion** at Trollskull Alley, a dark figure in black leather armor -- a burned Zhentarim agent later identified as Urstul -- is seen kneeling over the body of the gnome spy [[Dalekar|Dalcar]] and taking the [[Stone of Golorr]] component before fleeing south. The [[Trollskull Manor (Short Rest Tavern)|Trollskull Alley Irregulars]] track him to [[Gralhund Villa]], where [[Hrabbaz]] lets him inside, wounding [[Squidly]] in the process.

- **[[S01E12 - Episode Twelve]]:** SWOJ infiltrates [[Gralhund Villa]] and discovers a massacre -- dead servants in the pantry, a pitched battle between Gralhund guards and Zhentarim agents. Urstul, the burned Zhentarim leader, corners [[Orond Gralhund]] in a guest room and **slashes his throat** (stabilized by [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]). A teleportation circle is found carved into the floor "for the master" ([[Manshoon]]). Urstul uses a **flying snake** in a cage to send the message: *"G's have the stone, send reinforcements."* When charmed by Ylka, he reveals the Stone of Golorr's chain of possession. During the fight, **[[Scoot Sparkles]] hits Urstul with a dresser** -- an event that becomes a running joke throughout the entire campaign and the defining moment of their relationship.

- **[[S01E22 - Episode Twenty Two]]:** SWOJ infiltrates the [[Kolat Towers]] via the Gralhund Villa teleportation circle (passphrase: *"Tuatha Erinji"*). Upon arrival, they encounter Urstul and his associate **[[Mookie]]**, a halfling fighter previously seen in the [[Xanathar Guild|Xanathar]]'s lair. Urstul guards the lower floors and is immediately hostile, preparing to throw a knife at the party -- but Mookie dashes down the stairs and stops him, saying: *"No, Urstal, hold. These two are guests of the master's. What they say is true, you idiot."* Urstul fumes as SWOJ explores the tower, watching them "like a hawk." The Necromancer's orders protect SWOJ from harm ("leave them without any permanent damage"), so Urstul can only seethe as [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] taunts him and flips him the bird.

- **[[S01E23 - Episode Twenty Three]]:** After SWOJ's confrontation with the [[Manshoon|Necromancer]] in his extra-dimensional sanctum, the Necromancer directs them to find Urstul regarding the second Eye of the Stone of Golorr, noting that Urstul was "one of a few survivors of the massacre at the Gralhund Villa" and was **demoted to cleaning duty** after the dresser incident. Near the tower exit, SWOJ finds Urstul with [[Squidly]], who had infiltrated Urstul's operation. After intimidation and persuasion, Squidly rejoins SWOJ and reveals he **pickpocketed the second Eye** from Urstul. The [[Kolat Towers]] are then destroyed by explosives as the party escapes.

### Season 2: From Enemy to Ally

- **[[S02E18 - Episode Forty Eight LIVE (S02E18)]]:** Urstul resurfaces in [[Neverwinter]], where he has been sent by [[Manshoon]] to steal the **[[Crown of Neverwinter|Crown of Never]]** from [[Castle Never]]. He is first glimpsed as a mysterious figure in a black cloak and red hat in the throne room, who melts into the shadows when spotted. Later, he **ambushes [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] in the crypts** beneath the castle -- stabbing him in the throat with a **poison dagger** and firing an **exploding crossbow bolt**. Despite seeking revenge, Urstul is **knocked unconscious by Scoot** rather than killed, and taken captive.

- **[[S02E19 - Episode Forty Nine (S02E19)]]:** SWOJ emerges from Castle Never with the Crown and their captive. Scoot inventories Urstul's possessions: several vials of strong poison, crossbow bolts rigged with explosive capsules, a purse with 100 gold, and his red hat (which Lark dismisses: "It sucks"). Under **interrogation**, Urstul reveals critical intelligence:
  - [[Manshoon]] ("the boss with the mask" / "Nancy") operates from [[Amphail]] -- his hometown
  - Manshoon commands the forces mustered in the [[Spiral Cavern]]
  - Manshoon met with Urstul personally to give him the Crown retrieval mission
  He also reveals that his magical tattoos are the source of his ability to melt through floors. Despite having just tried to kill them, SWOJ treats Urstul with unexpected mercy -- Scoot **returns his 100 gold and possessions** (keeping only two vials of poison), feeds him onion rings on the walk through Neverwinter, and ultimately **releases him** near the chasm. Urstul, clearly moved, says: *"You've all been a lot more kind to me than I expected."* He promises not to "turn coat on my friends -- and I mean that in both senses on both sides." He departs on foot while SWOJ flies to Amphail on dragonback. [[Mr. E]] later asks why Scoot has a tied-up man, prompting an exchange where Urstul confirms he was at the Gralhund Villa: *"Oh, it's been a long time. Yeah, I used to have cornrows but now I let it just let it flow."*

- **[[S02E31 - Episode Sixty One (S02E31)]]:** In a pivotal moment, Neverwinter guards bring a **surly, heavily scarred man in manacles** to the docks where SWOJ is assembling their crew for the assault on the [[Spiral Cavern|moonhole]]. The guards explain: *"This guy also volunteered to join your ship. He said he was a friend. We don't trust him. We heard he tried to kill you. But his name is Erstel Floxon."* SWOJ accepts him without hesitation. Scoot **removes his bindings**, and Urstul is visibly taken aback -- he regards the four of them with *"extreme thoughtfulness and possibly respect."* Scoot offers a fist bump, and Urstul gives him an awkward one back. He joins the crew of the [[Ethereal Galleon]].

- **[[S02E32 - Episode Sixty Two LIVE (S02E32)]]:** Aboard the [[Ethereal Galleon]] as the party approaches the [[Sword Mountains]] and the [[Spiral Cavern|moonhole]], Urstul steps up and offers tactical advice: *"The Zentarim are renowned for using ambush tactics and traps and trickery. So if they're waiting for us, which I think they are, what's our plan?"* During the battle against a dragon, **[[Squidly]] and Urstul man the ballistas together**, firing at the beast. Scoot and Urstul team up on the main ballista -- they miss the shot, but Urstul gives Scoot a **fist bump** anyway: *"You guys did well."*

- **[[S02E33 - Episode Sixty Three (S02E33)]]:** At the [[Radiant Citadel]], Urstul is sent on a **scouting mission alongside [[Squidly]]** to gather intelligence about the occupied city. He uses his Zhentarim connections and knowledge to eavesdrop on guards, returning with complementary intelligence about the invasion timeline, the pylons, the curfew, and the dracolich's arrival.

- **[[S02E36 - Episode Sixty Six (S02E36)]]:** During the climactic battle against [[Manshoon]] at the [[Radiant Citadel]], [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] discovers Urstul, [[Squidly]], and the [[Doom Raiders]] huddled under a tent at the bottom of the Citadel, **bound in magical tentacle restraints** with a cultist guarding them. Lark **frees them all**, turning the tide of the battle. Urstul fights alongside SWOJ's allies in the final confrontation that ends with Manshoon's destruction.

- **[[S02E37 - Episode Sixty Seven LIVE (S02E37)]]:** In the aftermath of Manshoon's defeat, Urstul is found with **Little Joan** and other crew members at the base of the Citadel. When SWOJ approaches, Urstul and Little Joan initially draw their weapons suspiciously -- because **imposters impersonating SWOJ** had passed through earlier. Urstul vouches for the real SWOJ, nudging Little Joan: *"You know, Little Joan, these ones do seem a lot more like SWOJ. The other ones were kind of acting weird, weren't they?"* He then tests them with the question that only the real SWOJ could answer: *"What furniture did you throw at me?"* -- and the dresser answer confirms their identity.

## Relationships

- **[[Scoot Sparkles]]** -- Urstul's most significant relationship. Their dynamic evolved from deadly enemies to grudging allies to something resembling genuine friendship. The **dresser incident** at Gralhund Villa (S01E12) became the defining thread of their relationship -- referenced in nearly every encounter. Scoot's decision to spare Urstul's life at Castle Never, return his belongings, and release him (S02E19) was a turning point. By S02E31, Urstul volunteers for SWOJ's crew, and they exchange fist bumps. One character quips: *"Tell if you're my nemesis or my best friend."*

- **[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]** -- Lark taunted Urstul at the Kolat Towers, flipping him the bird and joking about the dresser. Lark also dismissed Urstul's red hat as inferior. However, it was Lark who freed Urstul from Manshoon's restraints during the final battle (S02E36).

- **[[Mookie]]** -- Urstul's associate and superior at the [[Kolat Towers]]. The halfling fighter kept Urstul in line, repeatedly telling him to shut up and follow the Necromancer's orders. She showed clear exasperation with his hot-headedness.

- **[[Manshoon]]** ("Nancy") -- Urstul's employer and the leader of the Zhentarim. Urstul served Manshoon loyally for years, meeting him personally in Amphail to receive the Crown retrieval mission. After SWOJ showed him mercy and Manshoon's forces later captured and restrained him, Urstul's loyalty shifted entirely to SWOJ's side.

- **[[Squidly]]** -- From an adversarial relationship (Squidly pickpocketed the Eye from Urstul in S01E23) to wartime brothers-in-arms (they manned ballistas together in S02E32 and scouted together in S02E33). Both were freed by Lark from Manshoon's restraints.

- **[[Mr. E]]** -- Mr. E recalls meeting Urstul at Gralhund Villa during the Season 1 battle. They reconnect in S02E19, with Urstul reminiscing about having cornrows at the time.

## Notable Traits

- **The Dresser** -- Scoot hitting Urstul with a dresser at Gralhund Villa is the most frequently referenced callback in the campaign. It comes up in S01E22, S01E23, S02E18, S02E19, S02E31, and S02E37.
- **The Red Hat** -- Urstul wears a distinctive oversized red hat, similar to Lark's but of lesser quality. It is his visual signature.
- **Shadow Melting** -- Via magical tattoos, Urstul can melt into shadows and phase through solid floors, making him an extremely effective ambush assassin.
- **Poison Specialist** -- Carries multiple vials of blade-coating poison and crossbow bolts rigged with explosive capsules.
- **Originally from Amphail** -- His hometown, which became Manshoon's staging ground for the Spiral Cavern army.
