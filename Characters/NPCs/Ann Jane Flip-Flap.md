# Ann Jane Flip-Flap

> *"You can't help yourself. These people need help, Zay."*
> -- Ann Jane to [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] after the rescue from the [[The Great Beast of Erua|Great Beast of Erua]] ([[S02E05 - Episode Thirty Five (S02E05)|S02E05]])

**First appearance:** [[S02E01 - Episode Thirty One (S02E01)|S02E01]]
**Total appearances:** 8 episodes
**Faction affiliations:** [[The Jolly Gents]]
**Status:** Half-life -- bound her soul to a knife via [[Mordecai Gralhund]]'s soul-binding ritual ([[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]])

---

## Overview

Ann Jane Flip-Flap was a lieutenant in the [[The Jolly Gents|Jolly Gents]] and one of only two members -- along with [[Antediluvian Jake]] -- who retained their free will under [[Rowan Crooks]]' control. A whip fighter and skilled survivalist, she served as a quiet operative within the organization: setting traps, leading sparring drills at [[Zhentil Keep]], and feeding intelligence to [[SWOJ]] during their infiltration. She traveled with the party through the [[The Feywild|Feywild]] and [[The Shadowfell|Shadowfell]] in early Season 2 before being captured and replaced by a podling inside the [[The Great Beast of Erua|Great Beast of Erua]]. After her rescue, she continued to work covertly against Crooks from within [[Zhentil Keep]], ultimately siding with SWOJ when the Jolly Gents civil war erupted.

---

## Description

Ann Jane wielded a whip in combat and carried a knife of personal significance (later used as her soul-binding vessel). She was proficient with traps -- tripwires, net snares, and other woodland devices -- and skilled enough in survival to scout terrain and identify predator signs. At [[Zhentil Keep]], she led sparring exercises on the [[Bridge Of Destiny]] and was recognized as its reigning champion. She fought with a quarterstaff in the Bridge's traditional log-over-mud-pit duels.

Her personality combined pragmatic toughness with genuine compassion. When the freed prisoners from the Beast of Erua needed care, she coordinated the healing effort while [[Antediluvian Jake]] was still barely conscious. She chided [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] for seeking her out for conversation when injured people still needed help, and confronted [[Rowan Crooks]] directly, telling him to let SWOJ rest.

## Role in the Story

### The Feywild and Shadowfell (S02E01 - S02E02)

When [[Rowan Crooks]] split off with part of his crew in the [[The Feywild|Feywild]], he left Ann Jane, [[Antediluvian Jake]], and [[Lyrian]] with [[SWOJ]]. Ann Jane and Jake took the rear of the group as scouts, providing survival advice -- pointing out broken branches that signaled predators and guiding the party through unfamiliar terrain.

At the [[Shadow Way Inn]] on the Feywild-Shadowfell border, Ann Jane and Jake locked the door to guard the entrance from outside. While posted there, both were captured and replaced by plant-based podlings. The podling duplicates maintained the deception until the real replicas inside the inn were discovered, triggering a major battle.

### Capture and Rescue (S02E02 - S02E05)

The real Ann Jane was held captive in a glowing pod inside the [[The Great Beast of Erua|Great Beast of Erua]]. [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] cut her and approximately fifteen other prisoners free from the pods. After rescue, Ann Jane immediately took charge of the situation -- coordinating the effort to heal people and check on freed prisoners while Jake was still groggy and disoriented. She cared for [[Dorvina]], the innkeeper from [[The Way Inn]], and called out to Crooks to give the party a rest. She was present during the group's extraction from the Great Beast and the teleportation back to [[Waterdeep]].

Meanwhile, in the Shadowfell forest, [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] spotted the podling version of Ann Jane mechanically setting tripwire traps and covering net snares with leaves -- a distorted echo of her real skillset that the party recognized as wrong due to its robotic, mechanical quality.

### The Infiltration of Zentil Keep (S02E22)

When [[SWOJ]] infiltrated [[Zhentil Keep]] disguised in Jolly Gents cloaks, they found Ann Jane leading sparring exercises in [[The Bailey]]. She recognized [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] immediately and pulled him onto the [[Bridge Of Destiny]] for a staff-fighting match -- using the bout as cover to pass critical intelligence.

During the sparring, Ann Jane whispered:
- [[Rowan Crooks]]' office was on the sixth floor, next to the throne room
- The vault held all the treasures the Gents had collected over the years, including SWOJ's stolen gear
- The castle was full of traps, especially on the fifth floor
- Only she and [[Antediluvian Jake|Jake]] had any kind of free will among the Gents
- Jake had been taken in for "retraining" a couple of days earlier
- They had been working on [[Little Joan]], who was starting to develop free will after fighting alongside SWOJ
- The Gents were collecting souls, and it was "ruffling some feathers with the [[Raven Queen]]"

Lark, rolling a 29 on acrobatics, genuinely outfought Ann Jane in the match. She offered to let him win, and they arranged a deliberate-looking finish where she swept his feet after he double-blinked (Lark's failed attempt at a wink). After the match, she approached [[Rambling Bill]] to cover for the party's departure toward the keep.

### The Civil War (S02E24)

During [[Rowan Crooks]]' address to the assembled Jolly Gents about his plan to break [[Kelemvor's Wall Of The Faithless|Kelemvor's Wall of the Faithless]], Ann Jane positioned herself right beside Crooks -- maintaining her cover. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] noticed her exchanging glances with [[Little Joan]] across the Bailey, with Joan stationed by [[Zulfarn]] and Ann Jane standing next to Crooks. When SWOJ confronted Crooks publicly, Ann Jane was among the approximately 20-30 Jolly Gents who sided with the party, triggering the civil war that ended with Crooks' death and [[Zhentil Keep]]'s collapse.

### God's Night and the Soul-Binding (S02E38)

At the God's Night celebration in [[Waterdeep]], Ann Jane and [[Antediluvian Jake]] invited SWOJ to the rooftop of the [[The Short Rest|Short Rest]]. Jake eulogized the fallen Jolly Gents and the original [[Rowan Crooks]]. Then Ann Jane performed [[Mordecai Gralhund]]'s soul-binding ritual -- magic that [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] recognized as her grandfather's invention. Ann Jane bound her soul to a knife, and Jake bound his to his obsidian tablet. The ritual ended their undead, lich-like existence, transitioning them to a half-life state. [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] played one of her grandfather's songs on her concertina as part of the process. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] burned an unopened package of [[Rowan Crooks]] trading cards, and [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] burned his Jolly Gents outfit in the fire.

## Relationships

- **[[Antediluvian Jake]]** -- Her closest companion and fellow free-willed Gent. The two worked together throughout the story -- scouting for SWOJ, being captured and rescued together, coordinating intelligence at Zentil Keep, and ultimately performing the soul-binding ritual side by side.
- **[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]** -- Her primary contact in SWOJ. She passed critical intelligence to him during their Bridge of Destiny sparring match and showed genuine respect for his fighting skill.
- **[[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]** -- Sought Ann Jane out after the Beast of Erua rescue. Ann Jane's blunt but caring response revealed a rapport between them.
- **[[Little Joan]]** -- Ann Jane and Jake had been "working on" Little Joan, trying to break Crooks' hold on her. During the Bailey confrontation, the two exchanged glances from their positions -- Ann Jane beside Crooks, Joan beside Zulfarn -- before both defected.
- **[[Rowan Crooks]]** -- Though she served under his command, Ann Jane retained her free will and worked covertly against him. She maintained her cover by standing at his side even during his final address.
