# Rackjack

The eldest of the [[Brighanox]], a community of tiny fairy folk living in tunnels along the border between the [[Feywild]] and [[The Shadowfell]]. Rackjack led her people in sheltering [[SWOJ]] after they fought off [[Strigoi]] attackers, and she taught the party about [[wish stones]] and Brighanox society before sending them on their way with an open invitation to return.

**First appearance:** [[S02E02 - Episode Thirty Two (S02E02)|S02E02]]
**Total appearances:** 1 episode

## Description

Rackjack is the eldest elder of the Brighanox, a race of small fey creatures who live in underground tunnel networks in the borderlands between the Feywild and the Shadowfell. She is gracious and hospitable, bowing deeply when greeting guests and maintaining a culture of overflowing plates and generous hospitality. She wears a fanny pack in which she keeps small treasures. Despite the constant threat of Strigoi raids, Rackjack leads her community with warmth and gratitude toward those who help defend them.

## Role in the Story

- **S02E02:** Rackjack and the other Brighanox elders welcome SWOJ into their tunnels after witnessing the party fight off Strigoi creatures
- **S02E02:** The elders teach SWOJ about wish stones -- crystallized unfulfilled wishes that the Brighanox collect and use as magical energy sources to power their society's stoves, lights, and water pumps
- **S02E02:** Rackjack explains that the Strigoi have been raiding more frequently as the roars of the [[Beast of Erua]] grow louder
- **S02E02:** The Brighanox dig a tunnel to the Shadowfell border for the party to continue their quest
- **S02E02:** Rackjack introduces herself by name at the farewell, bows graciously, and offers SWOJ sanctuary in their tunnels whenever they need it
- **S02E02:** The Brighanox use druidcraft to mark nearby stones as subtle waypoints so SWOJ can find the tunnel entrance again, then cover it with camouflage canvas
- **S02E02:** [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] gives Rackjack a spare pearl from her concertina keys as a parting gift; Rackjack tucks it into her fanny pack with thanks
