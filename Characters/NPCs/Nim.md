# Nim

A wooden automaton -- one of a class of constructs called nimblewrights -- first discovered aboard [[Zardoz Zord]]'s ship in the psychic dream-version of [[Waterdeep]]. About halfling-sized, roughly four feet tall, Nim resembled a tiny Don Quixote: carved from wood and metal, wearing a little wooden hat, and carrying a rapier and dagger at its hip. When SWOJ first encountered it, Nim was broken and non-functional, but later revelations tied the construct to the [[Gralhund Family]]'s darkest secrets and the clone mystery at the heart of [[Ylka Gralhund]]'s identity.

**First appearance:** [[S01E03 - Episode Three|S01E03]]
**Total appearances:** 6 episodes (previously listed as 21 -- inflated by false matches)

## Description

Nim is a nimblewright, a type of autonomous wooden construct built by gnomish artificers from [[Lantan]], far to the west. Nimblewrights are small, bespoke figures crafted from wood and metal, each one handcrafted and possessing a modicum of self-autonomy. They can follow complex instructions, perform tricks and stunts, play music from internal mechanisms, and serve as autonomous servants.

When first found aboard the [[Sea Maiden's Fair]] in the psychic dreamscape, Nim was broken and faded -- [[Zardoz Zord]] flipped a lever inside its chest cavity, but the construct shuddered and fizzled out, prompting Zardos to curse, "Dammit, Nim." In the waking world, the "real" version of Nim was described as a small wooden creature with silver feathers for hair, beautifully bespoke with every piece handcrafted. Unlike the dream version that seemed to have personality, the real Nim spoke in the third person -- "This one does not know you" and "This one does not need food" -- and behaved more robotically.

Nim was ultimately revealed to be the [[Gralhund Family]] golem, found in pieces on a worktable in the construct workshop at the [[Kolat Towers]], where [[Zylka]] (the original person from whom [[Ylka Gralhund]] was cloned) was shackled and forced to repair it by [[Manshoon|the Necromancer]].

## History

### Discovery in the Dream World (S01E03)

During SWOJ's first trip into the psychic dream-version of [[Waterdeep]] -- a surreal place that smelled of cherry, populated by zombie-like citizens chanting "lovely day" -- the party rowed out to the ships of the [[Sea Maiden's Fair]]. Aboard one of the vessels, [[Zardoz Zord]] showed them Nim, pulling the automaton out of a small crate. The construct was about Xae's size, made of wood and metal, and equipped with a rapier and dagger. Zardos tried to activate it by flipping a lever inside its chest, but Nim shuddered and fizzled out. This first encounter planted the seed of the party's fascination with the little construct.

### The Cassalanter Party and Nimblewright Sales (S01E09)

At the [[Cassalanter Villa]] masquerade ball, [[Zardoz Zord]] appeared selling nimblewrights to the assembled nobility at 1,000 gold pieces each. Small robotic figures ran along the walls, performed cheerleading formations, catapulted off each other, and did acrobatic tricks. The party immediately recognized these automatons as "the same sort of beings as Nim." Xae grabbed one and pulled it close, while Lark asked, "What's Nim doing?" -- but all the nimblewrights looked alike, making it impossible to identify which, if any, was Nim specifically. Zardos explained that nimblewrights were servants from [[Lantan]] with a modicum of self-autonomy, capable of doing whatever their owners asked. The party briefly considered buying one, but the price was steep.

### Visiting Nim on the Eyecatcher (S01E10)

When SWOJ visited [[Zardoz Zord]] (now revealed as [[Jarlaxle]]) aboard [[The Eyecatcher]], they specifically asked to meet Nim again. A crewman fetched the nimblewright from below decks -- a small wooden creature with silver feathers for hair, beautifully handcrafted. But this Nim was different from the dream version: robotic and impersonal, speaking in the third person. When the party greeted it enthusiastically, it simply stated, "This one does not know you." Zardos explained he had several nimblewrights, calling them "nimblewright" generically and noting they were built by artificers in Lantan. To the party's dismay, the real asking price was 25,000 gold. During the lunch, a nimblewright refilled Zardos's wine glass, and the party discussed the dream world -- Zardos believed the Nim they met there was merely a psychic model of his ship and crew, not the real construct.

### The Fireball and Nimblewright Involvement (S01E11)

After the catastrophic fireball explosion at the grand opening of [[Trollskull Manor|The Short Rest]], witnesses reported seeing a "puppet without strings" -- a nimblewright -- on the rooftop of the flower shop, hurling something into the crowd. SWOJ found a ruby eye from the nimblewright on the rooftops. In the aftermath, the party discussed Zardos's nimblewright-selling operation and its connection to the attack, though the specific nimblewright responsible was not identified as Nim.

### The Gralhund Nimblewright (S01E13)

[[Yalah Gralhund]] secretly dispatched a nimblewright carrying the [[Stone of Golorr]] and a handwritten map pointing to the [[Gralhund Crypt]] in the [[City of the Dead]]. This nimblewright was later found destroyed inside the crypt, confirming the Gralhund family's direct possession of nimblewright constructs -- a thread that would connect back to Nim's true origin.

### Nim's Remains in the Kolat Towers (S01E23)

The full truth about Nim emerged during SWOJ's assault on the [[Kolat Towers]]. Behind an illusory wall, the party discovered a construct workshop where [[Zylka]] -- the original person from whom [[Ylka Gralhund]] was cloned by their grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund]] -- was shackled and being forced to repair Nim. The golem's remains lay on a table in the center of the workshop.

During the battle with animated armor constructs, Zylka leaped over the table where Nim's remains were laid out. After the fight, a clicking sound emanated from Nim in the center of the room -- suggesting that Zylka's work may have partially reactivated the construct. Nim was identified in this scene as "the [[Gralhund Family]] golem," tying the construct directly to [[Mordecai Gralhund]]'s legacy of forbidden arcane experimentation.

## Connections

- **[[Zardoz Zord]] / [[Jarlaxle]]** -- Nim was first found aboard Zardos's ship, and Jarlaxle's [[Bregan D'aerthe]] operation sold nimblewrights across [[Waterdeep]]. The relationship between Jarlaxle's nimblewright trade and the Gralhund family's possession of Nim is unclear but suggestive.
- **[[Gralhund Family]]** -- Nim is identified as the Gralhund family golem. The Gralhunds possessed at least one nimblewright (used by [[Yalah Gralhund]] to transport the [[Stone of Golorr]]) and had deep ties to [[Manshoon]], who held [[Zylka]] captive to work on Nim.
- **[[Mordecai Gralhund]]** -- Ylka's grandfather, who created the clone magic and the [[Song Sword]]. Nim appears to be connected to Mordecai's legacy of arcane invention.
- **[[Zylka]]** -- The original person from whom [[Ylka Gralhund]] was cloned. Found shackled in the [[Kolat Towers]] construct workshop, forced by [[Manshoon|the Necromancer]] to repair Nim.
- **[[Manshoon]] (the Necromancer)** -- Held [[Zylka]] captive in the [[Kolat Towers]] and forced her to work on Nim. The Necromancer's interest in the Gralhund golem suggests Nim held strategic or magical value.
- **[[Kolat Towers]]** -- The [[Zhentarim]] base and gateway to the Necromancer's sanctum, where Nim's remains were kept in the construct workshop.
