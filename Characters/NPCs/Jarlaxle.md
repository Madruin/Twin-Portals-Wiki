# Jarlaxle

Jarlaxle Baenre is the founder and leader of [[Bregan D'Aerthe]], a drow mercenary organization composed of outcasts from the great houses of Menzoberranzan. A master of disguise, intelligence, and political maneuvering, he operates across the Sword Coast under multiple identities -- most prominently as **Zardos Zord**, the flamboyant ringmaster of the [[Sea Maiden'S Fair|Sea Maiden's Fair]], and **J.B. Nevercott**, a mysterious haberdasher. He is one of [[SWOJ]]'s most enduring and consequential allies across both seasons of the campaign, and the inventor of the paired bags of holding -- the "twin portals" -- that give the podcast its name.

**First appearance:** [[S01E03 - Episode Three|S01E03]] (as Zardos Zord)
**True identity revealed:** [[S01E10 - Episode Ten|S01E10]]
**Total appearances:** 17 episodes
**Faction affiliations:** [[Bregan D'Aerthe]] (leader), [[Sea Maiden'S Fair|Sea Maiden's Fair]] (captain)

## Description

### Appearance

In his true drow form, Jarlaxle has silvery purple skin, red eyes, and wears an eye patch. He favors a large, ostentatious hat -- a signature accessory that also serves as his primary disguise tool. When he removes the hat, his visage warps from whatever persona he is wearing back into his true drow form; when he puts it back on, the disguise returns instantly.

As Zardos Zord, he presents as a flamboyant human showman wearing red and yellow satin knickers, a harness festooned with knives and vials, and a hat with a huge red plume. By Season 2, his signature headwear has evolved into a huge purple hat.

He is described as "charming, calculating, and underdressed" -- a characterization perfectly encapsulated by the scene in S02E14 where he greets SWOJ wearing nothing but a towel, having just rolled out of bed.

### Personality

Jarlaxle is a consummate dealmaker who trades in information, favors, and political leverage rather than brute force. Every act of generosity comes with a strategic calculus -- he gives away bags of holding because they contain twin portals he can exploit, equips SWOJ because their success serves his ambitions, and defends Neverwinter because demonstrating loyalty earns him trading rights. Yet his warmth toward SWOJ appears genuine: he bets on their survival and wins, shares lavender tea, provides equipment from his personal wardrobe, and ultimately stands beside them at the Masked Lords' council as himself, eye patch and all.

He has a playful sense of humor. When SWOJ arrives late to the council celebration in S02E38, Jarlaxle quips "So was I" about the drinking. He previously introduced [[Mr. E]] (Mystery) to the party -- a connection that became a recurring guest PC role -- and the two share a warm, hugging relationship.

### Goals and Motivations

Jarlaxle's overarching goal is to establish a **legitimate drow presence in surface cities**. In Waterdeep (Season 1), he seeks to position Bregan D'Aerthe as allies of the city rather than threats, telling SWOJ he wants to see Waterdeep "prosper and flourish." He is keenly interested in acquiring the [[The Stone of Golorr]] and the political leverage it represents.

By Season 2, this ambition has expanded to **Neverwinter**, where his ships are barred from the port on suspicion of being Waterdeep spies. He asks SWOJ to put in a good word with the city's leadership -- a request that ultimately bears fruit when he appears openly at the Masked Lords' council in the Season 2 finale.

## Disguises

Jarlaxle operates under multiple identities throughout the campaign, each tailored to a different role in his network of influence.

### Zardos Zord (Primary Cover Identity)

His most prominent and long-running persona. As Zardos, Jarlaxle presents himself as the flamboyant human ringmaster and captain of the Sea Maiden's Fair -- a fleet of three ships (the [[Eyecatcher]], the Heartbreaker, and the [[Hell Razor]]) that functions as a traveling carnival, entertainment troupe, and trading enterprise. The crew of all three ships are disguised drow using magic to appear human.

SWOJ first encounters Zardos in S01E03, inside a psychic illusion of Waterdeep, where he recognizes the party as not belonging and gives them a bag of holding -- their first encounter with twin portal technology. He uses this identity consistently through both seasons, including at the Cassalanter party (S01E09), the Blubber Buckle tavern in Driftwood (S02E09), war councils in Neverwinter (S02E19-20), and when departing for Waterdeep carrying urgent messages (S02E31).

In S01E10, when Xae uses the Ring of Disguise Self to impersonate Zardos, a crew member immediately recognizes the deception and sends a magical message to the real Jarlaxle -- demonstrating both the crew's loyalty and the tightness of the disguise's integration with Bregan D'Aerthe operations.

### J.B. Nevercott (Haberdasher Persona)

A specialist persona used for covert support operations. First appears in **S01E09**, delivering critical anti-doppelganger gear to SWOJ before the Cassalanter party: a fancy hat with lead foil lining (which blocks Detect Thoughts and thought-reading) and three pistols loaded with true sight rounds that reveal doppelgangers by dispersing a pink dust.

In **S01E17**, JB Nevercott appears seated in the audience of the Xanathar's Arena of Blood and Fortune. He winks at Lark and Xae from the corner of his eye, then uses the Message spell to telepathically warn SWOJ that the lair is rigged with smoke powder and reveals the location of a secret exit -- the south wall, marked with a flaming eye symbol. This intelligence saves the party's lives when the lair detonates moments later.

### Jarlaxle Baenre (True Identity)

His true drow form. He first reveals it aboard the Eyecatcher in S01E10, dropping his Zardos disguise by removing his hat -- his visage warping to silvery purple skin and red eyes. He appears as himself at key moments: emerging from the twin portals with Bregan D'Aerthe soldiers in the Cassalanter crypt (S01E29), hosting SWOJ in his cabin outside Neverwinter (S02E14), and standing at the Masked Lords' council in the Season 2 finale (S02E38) -- the first time he appears publicly as a drow before the assembled leadership of the Sword Coast Alliance.

## The Twin Portals

Jarlaxle is the source of the technology that gives the podcast its name. In **S01E29**, he reveals that he has "always found it convenient to give away bags of holding that have twin portals" -- paired bags of holding with connected dimensional spaces that allow passage between them. The bag of holding he gave SWOJ in S01E03 was paired with one he kept, creating a permanent extradimensional link between the two.

When dragons flew overhead near the Sea Maiden's Fair during the Cassalanter climax, Jarlaxle checked his end of the portal, saw a way in, and emerged with his soldiers into the Cassalanter crypt -- dramatically revealing both the technology and the depth of his long-term planning.

By S02E14, SWOJ uses this same twin portal connection to escape the bag dimension directly into Jarlaxle's cabin aboard the Eyecatcher outside Neverwinter. Jarlaxle later mentions that bags of holding are "in short supply" and that he has been looking for one himself (S02E20), suggesting the twin portal network is a finite and valuable resource.

## Faction: [[Bregan D'Aerthe]]

Jarlaxle founded and leads Bregan D'Aerthe, whose members describe themselves as "outcasts" -- the "much derided and discarded castaways of the great houses" of Menzoberranzan who have severed ties with the drow matriarchy of the Underdark.

He commands an extensive intelligence network, a fleet of three ships, advanced firearms technology, and agents embedded across the Sword Coast. Key lieutenants include:

- **[[Fel'Rekt Lafeen]]** -- His most prominent lieutenant and SWOJ's primary Bregan D'Aerthe contact. A drow who left the Underdark because he "couldn't stand the way that the matriarchy in the drow culture treated men" and chose to live as a man. Jarlaxle admired the "panache" Fel'Rekt showed in making that change and took him in.
- **[[Narl Xibrindas]]** -- A white-haired drow operating as a spy embedded within the Xanathar Guild.
- **[[Sarith Ab'Jean]]** -- Captain of the Hell Razor, described as a swashbuckling drow with a clearly trimmed mustache.
- **[[Tari Dorr]]** -- A member encountered fighting in Neverwinter's streets during the chasm eruption, acting on Jarlaxle's standing orders.
- **[[The Milliner]]** -- A hat-maker aboard the Eyecatcher who crafted Lark's original hat and later creates a replacement. Goes solely by "the Milliner" -- his craft is his entire personality.

## Key Relationships

### SWOJ (Silver Waves of Justice)

Bregan D'Aerthe is one of SWOJ's most consistent allies across both seasons, though the relationship is built on mutual benefit rather than pure altruism. Jarlaxle provides intelligence, equipment, passage, and military support; in return, SWOJ advances his political interests, trades him the Mask of the Lords, and advocates for his trading rights. By the Season 2 finale, the alliance has matured into genuine mutual respect -- Jarlaxle toasts SWOJ at the Masked Lords' council alongside Renaer Neverember.

### [[Mr. E]] (Mystery)

A guest character played by Robert Lee, introduced to SWOJ by Jarlaxle. Mr. E operates as Jarlaxle's associate -- sent by Zardos to help SWOJ infiltrate the Gralhund Villa (S01E12), later fighting alongside them through the Vault of Dragons and the Cassalanter confrontation. Though not a formal Bregan D'Aerthe member, Mr. E and Jarlaxle share a warm, hugging relationship. Mr. E's name is a play on the word "mystery" -- a pun the players take a moment to catch.

### [[Fel'Rekt Lafeen]]

Jarlaxle's closest lieutenant and most trusted operative. Fel'Rekt serves as the operational bridge between Jarlaxle and SWOJ throughout both seasons -- from intelligence drops at Trollskull Manor to disguised reconnaissance in Neverwinter to crewing the ballista aboard the Ethereal Galleon. Jarlaxle took Fel'Rekt in because he admired the panache he showed in choosing to live as a man and rejecting the drow matriarchy.

### [[Renaer Neverember]]

Both operate as shadow allies of SWOJ with overlapping political interests. They stand together at the Masked Lords' council in S02E38, jointly toasting SWOJ's heroism.

### The Sword Coast Political Establishment

Jarlaxle navigates a complex relationship with surface-world power structures. In Waterdeep, he sought the Stone of Golorr and traded for the Mask of the Lords. In Neverwinter, his ships are barred from port as suspected spies, but he mobilizes his forces to defend the city during the chasm eruption -- demonstrating loyalty that ultimately earns him a seat at the table. By the Season 2 finale, he has achieved what seemed impossible: a drow mercenary leader standing openly at the Masked Lords' council.

## Trivia

- The podcast's name, **Twin Portals**, comes from Jarlaxle's paired bags of holding technology, revealed in S01E29.
- The players frequently mispronounce Jarlaxle's name in entertaining ways, including "Jalaxle," "jar laxel," and "Dr. Axel." His organization fares no better: "Bregan Darth," "Greg and Dere," "Greg and Derek," "Droughts," "Regan d'Arth," and "Bragan Derrith."
- Jarlaxle's Zardos Zord persona is also occasionally spelled "Zardoz Zord" in transcripts, a natural variation from the audio source material.
- In S02E14, Jarlaxle is described as "charming, calculating, and underdressed" -- perhaps the most efficient three-word summary of his character.
- Jarlaxle previously introduced Mr. E (Mystery) to SWOJ, establishing a connection that became a recurring guest PC role played by Robert Lee.
