# Malark Wavesilver

**Player:** Justin
**Race:** Human
**Class:** Rogue (Swashbuckler)
**Background:** Noble
**Also known as:** Lark, Malark
**Party:** [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]]

## Description

Malark "Lark" Wavesilver was a human rogue of noble birth, the scion of [[House Wavesilver]] -- a once-prominent [[Waterdeep]] family in severe financial decline. Charming, rakish, and quick with a blade, Lark embodied the swashbuckler archetype: he fought with flair, talked his way out of trouble more often than into it, and nursed a deep insecurity about his family's fading prestige. He wielded the [[Ouroboros]] rapier, a +1 weapon with an exploding d8 damage die, and carried [[Little Dinky]], an extendable staff. After his resurrection via clone spell, Lark bore silver-tipped hair from the [[Mask of the Cultivator]], a physical mark of his brush with death and rebirth. His father [[Grim Wavesilver]] was cold and disapproving, while his mother [[Aswel Wavesilver|Isvel]] secretly operated as the [[The Black Viper]], a notorious vigilante who trained Lark in her craft. He carried an autographed copy of [[Volothamp Geddarm|Volo]]'s Guide to Monsters and harbored a collector's passion for rare memorabilia, including [[Rowan Crooks]] trading cards.

## History

Lark grew up in the shadow of [[House Wavesilver]]'s declining fortunes. His father [[Grim Wavesilver|Grim]] was stern and distant, while his mother [[Aswel Wavesilver|Isvel]] hid a secret life as the [[The Black Viper]], a legendary thief and vigilante of [[Waterdeep]]. Lark's childhood friendship with [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] drew him away from the suffocating expectations of noble society and into the streets of the [[Dock Ward]], where the two developed the bond that would anchor the party for years. When [[Volothamp Geddarm|Volo]] recruited the group to rescue [[Floon Blagmaar]], Lark brought his noble connections, roguish skills, and an irrepressible sense of showmanship.

During the early investigation into [[Floon Blagmaar]]'s disappearance, Lark proved himself a capable infiltrator and smooth talker, navigating the politics of [[Waterdeep]]'s underworld factions. He chose the Swashbuckler archetype at third level, formalizing his dashing combat style. The hunt for the [[The Stone of Golorr]] brought SWOJ into contact with the city's most dangerous factions -- the [[Zhentarim]], the [[Xanathar Guild]], and the [[Cassalanters]] family -- and Lark's noble background provided crucial social leverage in each encounter. He identified the [[Gralhund]] connection, uncovered leads at the [[Gralhund Villa]], and helped track the Stone through the streets of [[Waterdeep]].

Lark's most defining moment came during the assault on the [[Xanathar's Lair]]. In a terrible instant, the [[The Xanathar]] turned its disintegration eye ray on Lark. His last words -- "For justice" -- echoed through the chamber as his body was reduced to dust. His death was a seismic shock to the party. SWOJ refused to accept his loss. They tracked his soul to the realm of [[Malar]], the Beast Lord, fighting through the dangerous divine domain to find Lark's soul trapped inside his own hat. The party carried him back, and Lark was resurrected via a clone spell performed by [[Manshoon|the Necromancer]] in the [[Kolat Towers]]. The resurrection left him changed: the [[Mask of the Cultivator]] gave him silver-tipped hair, and the experience of death and rebirth left psychological scars he carried for the rest of the campaign.

What Lark did not immediately know was that [[Manshoon|the Necromancer]] had implanted a [[Gem of Seeing]] in his temple during the resurrection process, using it to spy on SWOJ's movements and plans. This betrayal was not revealed until much later, when the party discovered the gem and realized how deeply [[Manshoon]]'s influence had penetrated their group. Lark's mother [[Aswel Wavesilver|Isvel]] revealed her identity as the [[The Black Viper]] and began training him in vigilantism, giving him skills that proved crucial in Season 2.

An earlier transformation also tested Lark's identity: he was temporarily turned into a wererat after being bitten by a [[Shard Shunners]] during the party's investigation of the [[Xanathar Guild]]. Though the lycanthropy was eventually cured, the experience left him wary of shapeshifting magic.

In Season 2, Lark continued to develop as both a combatant and a leader. When SWOJ was killed by [[Rowan Crooks]] and resurrected in new bodies in the [[The Bag Dimension]], Lark received a double-ended silver-plated dagger from [[Fioleth]]. His collector's instincts kicked in when he found a sealed pack of rare [[Rowan Crooks]] trading cards -- including the potentially priceless misprint of the [[Antediluvian Jake]] card. His rogue skills proved essential in navigating the [[Jolly Gents]]' fortress [[Zhentil Keep]] in the [[Shadowfell]]: he bluffed past sentries using [[Antediluvian Jake]]'s obsidian tablet, won a staff sparring match against [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]] on the Bridge of Destiny, and disabled shadow thread traps on the fifth floor, finding boots of silence and a mithril [[Jolly Gents]] brooch.

During the [[Neverwinter Chasm]] descent, [[Antediluvian Jake]] dropped an obsidian tablet to Lark and whispered "we're all prisoners of Crooks" -- a revelation that reframed the [[Jolly Gents]] as victims rather than enemies. Lark carried this knowledge forward, becoming a key advocate for allying with the Jolly Gents members who had broken free of [[Rowan Crooks]]'s control.

In the campaign's final arc, Lark infiltrated enemy positions during the battle at the [[Radiant Citadel]], freeing [[Squidly]], [[Urstul Floxxin|Erstel Floxon]], and the [[Doom Raiders]] from magical restraints. His acrobatic combat skills were on full display during the [[Concord Jewel]] battle, performing daring feats across ropes in the [[The Bag Dimension]]. When the battered heroes crash-landed in the [[Spiral Cavern]], Lark took a bow for the cheering crowd -- ever the showman.

In the Season 2 epilogue, Lark returned to [[Trollskull Manor]] and was reunited with his parents [[Grim Wavesilver|Grim]] and [[Aswel Wavesilver|Aspell]]. He visited [[Froon]]'s tavern across the street from Trollskull -- a rival establishment now thriving thanks to SWOJ's early investment. At the God's Night celebration, Lark burned his [[Jolly Gents]] outfit in the fire, symbolically closing that chapter. He expressed his desire to continue adventuring and recruiting for the newly chartered Guild of the [[Silver Waves Of Justice]].

## Relationships

- **[[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]** -- Childhood best friend and the emotional anchor of the party. Scoot's grief at Lark's death drove the quest to recover his soul, and their reunion after resurrection was one of the campaign's most powerful moments.
- **[[Grim Wavesilver]]** -- Lark's cold, disapproving father. Their strained relationship reflected [[House Wavesilver]]'s decline and Grim's inability to connect with his adventurous son.
- **[[Aswel Wavesilver|Isvel / Aspell Wavesilver]]** -- Lark's mother, secretly the [[The Black Viper]]. She trained Lark in vigilantism after his resurrection, and her double life as a noblewoman and masked thief mirrored Lark's own struggle between respectability and roguish instinct.
- **[[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]** -- Fellow SWOJ member. Ylka's bardic inspiration and healing magic kept Lark alive through countless battles, and their shared noble backgrounds created a natural rapport.
- **[[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]** -- Fellow SWOJ member. Xae's mysterious origins contrasted with Lark's well-documented lineage, but their mutual loyalty was never in question.
- **[[Manshoon|The Necromancer]]** -- The Manshoon clone who resurrected Lark via clone spell but planted a [[Gem of Seeing]] in his temple to spy on SWOJ. This violation of trust became a major plot point.
- **[[Antediluvian Jake]]** -- The legendary [[Jolly Gents]] figure who secretly passed Lark an obsidian tablet and whispered that the Gents were all prisoners of [[Rowan Crooks]]. This moment of trust changed Lark's understanding of the conflict.
- **[[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]]** -- A [[Jolly Gents]] lieutenant who challenged Lark to a sparring match and grew to respect him after his victory.
- **[[Anya Ermbrusk]]** -- A half-elf who knew Lark from old family reunions, encountered in [[Arcturia]]'s cavern.
- **[[Volothamp Geddarm|Volo]]** -- The famous author who hired SWOJ for their first job. Lark carried an autographed copy of Volo's Guide to Monsters throughout the campaign.
