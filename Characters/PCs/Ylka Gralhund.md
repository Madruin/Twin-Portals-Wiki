# Ylka Gralhund

**Player:** Beth
**Race:** Human
**Class:** Bard (College of War)
**Also known as:** Ilka, Ilco
**Party:** [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]]

## Description

Ylka Gralhund was a human bard of the [[Gralhund]] noble family, recently resurrected at the start of the campaign under circumstances she did not fully understand. She carried a concertina -- a battered squeeze-box that contained the disembodied soul of her grandfather, [[Mordecai Gralhund]], who whispered guidance and warnings from within the instrument. Ylka was a performer at heart, quick with a cutting word or an inspiring melody, but she possessed a steel core that emerged in moments of crisis. She chose the College of War at third level, blending martial prowess with bardic magic in a way that reflected her complicated heritage. Her signature weapon was the [[Song Sword]], a magical blade engraved with the initials "M.G." -- her grandfather's -- that produced musical tones when struck against surfaces. Over the course of the campaign, Ylka evolved from a confused young woman searching for her identity into a fearless warrior-bard who delivered speeches before courts, composed epic ballads, and killed a beholder with a thrown dagger.

## History

Ylka entered the campaign as a recently resurrected woman with fragmented memories and a mysterious connection to the [[Gralhund]] noble house. She joined [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]], [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]], and eventually [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] in answering [[Volothamp Geddarm|Volo]]'s call to rescue [[Floon Blagmaar]] from the [[Xanathar Guild]]. From the start, Ylka's bardic abilities proved invaluable -- her healing word and cutting remarks kept the party alive through their earliest battles in the [[Zhentarim]] warehouse and the [[Xanathar Guild]] sewers beneath [[Waterdeep]]. The mysterious [[The Black Viper]] took an early interest in Ylka, leaving a black onyx gemstone rose for her at [[Trollskull Manor]]. When the party renovated the manor and discovered the ghost [[Lif]] haunting its halls, it was from Lif's hidden chest that Ylka retrieved the [[Song Sword]] bearing Mordecai Gralhund's initials.

The truth of Ylka's identity unraveled slowly and painfully. [[Yalah Gralhund]] confronted her publicly, calling her a traitor to the family. The full revelation came when SWOJ stormed the [[Kolat Towers]]: Ylka discovered that she was not merely a member of the [[Gralhund]] family but a clone of [[Zylka]], created by their grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund]] to protect Zylka's soul from some unnamed threat. The real Zylka was found shackled in the towers, forced to work on [[Nim]], a magical golem. This discovery shook Ylka to her core, forcing her to confront questions of identity, autonomy, and purpose that would drive her character arc for the remainder of the campaign.

At third level, Ylka chose the College of War, gaining proficiency with martial weapons and medium armor. She learned [[Leomund's Tiny Hut]], which she nicknamed "Yurtle's Tiny Shell," providing the party with a reliable safe haven during dangerous excursions. Her combat prowess grew steadily -- she invented the "Murderation" combat tactic, combining a murder of crows with a murmuration pattern to devastating effect. Her finest hour in Season 1 came during the assault on the [[Xanathar's Lair]], where she delivered the killing blow to the [[The Xanathar]] itself with a thrown dagger, slaying the beholder crime lord who had terrorized [[Waterdeep]]'s underworld for years. She later performed a legendary speech at the [[Cassalanters]] trial and composed an epic ballad chronicling SWOJ's exploits.

In Season 2, Ylka's role expanded as SWOJ ventured beyond [[Waterdeep]]. She smashed [[Arcturia]]'s enchanted mirror in the [[Spiral Cavern]] with her [[Song Sword]], breaking the transmutation magic that had plagued the [[Kryptwood]] and freeing dozens of captives. At the [[Radiant Citadel]], she captured one of Arcturia's pink butterflies and participated in the Battle Prawns competition at [[Sid Squid]]'s, chopping giant crawfish with her [[Song Sword]]. After SWOJ was killed by [[Rowan Crooks]] and resurrected in new bodies, Ylka claimed a marching marimba and a long whip with fifteen-foot reach from [[Fioleth]]'s junkyard in the [[The Bag Dimension]], along with a heater shield bearing the [[Gralhund]] family crest.

Ylka's concertina -- and her grandfather's soul within it -- was stolen by [[Sixteen String Jack]] of the [[Jolly Gents]]. She recovered it during a fierce battle at the subterranean lake beneath the [[Neverwinter Chasm]], shattering Jack's stolen loot to reclaim her most precious possession. At [[Zhentil Keep]] in the [[Shadowfell]], Ylka fought alongside the party through trapped hallways and the soul armor guardian, recovering their stolen gear from [[Rowan Crooks]]'s vault.

During the climactic siege of the [[Radiant Citadel]], Ylka demonstrated the full breadth of her power. She used the [[Mask of Dragon Kind]] (stolen from the [[Cult Of The Dragon]] at [[Castle Naerytar]]) to summon allied dragons to SWOJ's aid. She cast Shatter on the dracolich phylactery in the [[The Sunken City]], triggering a cascading collapse. She polymorphed into a Tyrannosaurus Rex and bit [[Amalia Cassalanter]] during the battle atop the [[Radiant Citadel]]. In the final confrontation with [[Manshoon]], Ylka used Dimension Door and Shatter spells aboard the [[Concord Jewel]], and when the battered heroes crash-landed in the [[Spiral Cavern]], she was still playing music with her last three hit points as the crowd cheered.

In the Season 2 epilogue, Ylka performed with [[Three Strings]] to a packed crowd in [[Waterdeep]] and played her grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund|Mordecai]]'s music at the God's Night fire. [[Antediluvian Jake]] and [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap|Angie Fliplap]] performed Mordecai's soul-binding ritual, completing a chapter of Ylka's family story. She resolved to investigate the [[Gralhund]] family's dark history and her grandfather's legacy going forward.

## Relationships

- **[[Mordecai Gralhund]]** -- Her grandfather, whose disembodied soul resided in her concertina. Mordecai created Ylka as a clone of [[Zylka]] to protect the family bloodline. His [[Song Sword]] became Ylka's signature weapon, and his music was performed at the God's Night celebration.
- **[[Zylka]]** -- The original person Ylka was cloned from. Zylka was discovered shackled in the [[Kolat Towers]], forced to build the golem [[Nim]]. The revelation of Ylka's true nature as a clone was one of the campaign's most dramatic twists.
- **[[Yalah Gralhund]]** -- A [[Gralhund]] family member who publicly denounced Ylka as a traitor. Their antagonistic relationship reflected the family's fractured politics.
- **[[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]** -- Fellow SWOJ member and loyal friend. Ylka polymorphed Scoot into a giant ape for the final battle and consistently supported him with bardic inspiration.
- **[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]** -- Fellow SWOJ member. Ylka was devastated by Lark's death at the hands of the [[The Xanathar]] and played a crucial role in the quest to recover his soul.
- **[[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]** -- Fellow SWOJ member and trusted companion through all three seasons.
- **[[The Black Viper]]** (Isvel/[[Aswel Wavesilver]]) -- The mysterious vigilante who left Ylka the black onyx rose, hinting at a deeper connection to the [[House Wavesilver]] family.
- **[[Lif]]** -- The ghost of [[Trollskull Manor]], in whose hidden chest Ylka found the [[Song Sword]].
- **[[Sixteen String Jack]]** -- The [[Jolly Gents]] bard who stole Ylka's concertina. Their rivalry culminated in Ylka recovering her instrument by force.
- **[[Three Strings]]** -- A musician in [[Waterdeep]] with whom Ylka performed during the Season 2 epilogue.
