# Hungering Shard

The **Hungering Shard** is a powerful artifact connected to [[Manshoon]]'s soul stone network, carried by [[Rowan Crooks]] (the 14th Manshoon clone) as the key component in his plan to break [[Kelemvor]]'s [[Kelemvor's Wall Of The Faithless|Wall of the Faithless]] and free the remaining fragments of Manshoon's soul trapped on the other side. The shard was fiercely contested during the sky-falling battle above the [[Shadowfell]] as [[Zhentil Keep]] collapsed, changing hands multiple times before [[SWOJ]] secured it and delivered it to [[Neverwinter]].

## Description

The Hungering Shard is an artifact connected to [[Manshoon]]'s soul stones -- the crystalline vessels used to capture and bind spirits. [[Rowan Crooks]] carried it in a backpack during the final battle atop [[Zhentil Keep]]. Its specific connection to the soul pylons at the [[Radiant Citadel]] made it uniquely dangerous: when combined with the pylon infrastructure that the real Rowan Crooks had originally built at the Citadel, the shard could theoretically punch through the barrier between the mortal planes and [[Kelemvor]]'s domain, breaching the [[Kelemvor's Wall Of The Faithless|Wall of the Faithless]].

The shard's purpose was recorded in [[Friar Truck]]'s private notes, discovered in his phylactery laboratory beneath [[Zhentil Keep]]. Those notes detailed how Truck -- the hidden architect of the Jolly Gents' corruption -- had orchestrated the entire conspiracy to enable Crooks' plan: travel to the [[Radiant Citadel]], weaponize the soul pylons, and use the Hungering Shard to shatter the Wall and retrieve the remaining Manshoon soul fragments imprisoned within it.

## History

### Discovery of the Plan (S02E24)

After killing [[Friar Truck]] in his phylactery laboratory, [[SWOJ]] explored the room more thoroughly and found clone tanks containing [[Rowan Crooks]] duplicates in various stages of development. Among Truck's notes, the party pieced together the full scope of the conspiracy: Truck had manipulated the real Rowan Crooks into a doomed assault on [[Zhentil Keep]], enabling his murder and replacement by the 14th Manshoon clone. The notes described the Hungering Shard, its connection to Manshoon's soul stones, and the plan to use it at the [[Radiant Citadel]] to break [[Kelemvor]]'s [[Kelemvor's Wall Of The Faithless|Wall of the Faithless]].

### The Sky Battle (S02E24)

When SWOJ confronted [[Rowan Crooks]] publicly in [[The Bailey]] atop [[Zhentil Keep]], a civil war erupted among [[The Jolly Gents]]. [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] jammed [[Little Dinky]] into the soul engine, destabilizing the floating fortress. As the island began to collapse, [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] grabbed the Hungering Shard backpack from near Crooks during the chaos.

The shard became a contested prize during the plummeting sky battle. Crooks cast hold person on Scoot, freezing him in place and snatching the shard back via invisible force. The party fought desperately to recover it: [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] rammed her sunblade into Crooks before falling unconscious, [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] cast faerie fire to turn Crooks pink and summoned giant constrictor snakes to grapple him, and [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] launched himself at Crooks using Little Dinky as propulsion.

In the climactic moment, Scoot launched himself upward and killed Crooks with his lightning-split axe, shouting "Bentos!" [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] caught the Hungering Shard as it fell from the dead clone's hands.

### Aftermath (S02E25 -- S02E26)

With Crooks destroyed and his soul trapped in the [[The Stone of Golorr|Soul Stone]], SWOJ secured the Hungering Shard among the wreckage of [[Zhentil Keep]] in the [[Shadowfell]]. After passing through the [[Raven Queen]]'s dream trials, the party returned to [[Neverwinter]], where [[Renaer Neverember]] took custody of both the Soul Stone containing Crooks and the party's recovered artifacts at the [[Hall of Justice]].

The shard's intended purpose -- breaking the Wall of the Faithless -- was ultimately accomplished by other means. [[Arcturia]] performed a ritual at [[Kelemvor]]'s skeleton on the Wall itself, and [[Kelemvor]] manifested in divine form to present SWOJ with the choice. They voted unanimously to break the Wall, and silver waves of energy shattered it, freeing the trapped souls. [[Manshoon]]'s soul was sent to the [[Raven Queen]] in final judgment.
