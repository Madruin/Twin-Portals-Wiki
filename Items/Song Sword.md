# Song Sword

The Song Sword is an enchanted cold-steel short sword that belonged to [[Mordecai Gralhund]], bearing his initials "M.G." on a brass plaque at the hilt. Discovered by the [[Silver Waves of Justice]] in [[Lif Lindgren|Lif]]'s hidden workshop beneath [[Trollskull Manor]], the blade produces melodies when swung -- the same melodies Mordecai once sang to his granddaughter [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]. It became Ylka's signature weapon throughout the campaign, a tangible link between her bardic magic and her grandfather's legacy.

## Description

The Song Sword is an ornate short sword forged from blue steel. The hilt features a small gemstone and a brass plaque engraved with the initials "M.G." for [[Mordecai Gralhund]]. The blade is cold to the touch, enchanted to deal cold damage on strikes. It possesses the finesse property, making it well-suited to Ylka's fighting style.

The sword's most distinctive quality is its music. When swung or struck against a surface, the blade produces melodies that shift to match the mood of the wielder and the intensity of combat. In tense moments, it has played frantic renditions resembling "Carol of the Bells," escalating in agitation alongside its wielder's emotions. In triumphant moments, it has rung out with victorious fanfares. When tapped against crystal panels in the [[Radiant Citadel]], different thicknesses produced different resonant tones, and Ylka used the sword like a tuning fork to explore their acoustics.

The melody the sword plays by default is one that [[Mordecai Gralhund|Mordecai]] used to sing to Ylka as a child -- a deeply personal musical signature that allowed Ylka to identify the blade's origin the moment she first heard it.

## History

### Creation

The Song Sword was crafted by or for [[Mordecai Gralhund]], a wizard of [[House Gralhund]] with deep knowledge of soul magic and arcane ritual. The sword's enchantment -- cold damage, musical properties, and the finesse trait -- reflects Mordecai's blend of martial and magical traditions, a combination his granddaughter [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] would later embody through her College of War training.

### In Lif's Keeping

At some point before his death, Mordecai entrusted the sword to [[Lif Lindgren]], the elven bartender he had helped find work at [[Trollskull Manor]]. Lif stored the sword in a small chest in his hidden workshop in the manor's cellar, alongside his diary and personal effects. When Lif died and became a ghost bound to the manor, the sword remained sealed in his chest for approximately ninety-five years, waiting to be found.

### Discovery by SWOJ (S01E05)

When the [[Silver Waves of Justice]] first explored [[Trollskull Manor]] in [[S01E05 - Episode Five|S01E05]], they discovered Lif's hidden workshop in the cellar. Inside the chest, [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] found the sword and swung it, causing it to produce a melody. [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] was struck by two immediate realizations: the melody was one her grandfather used to sing to her, and the initials "M.G." belonged to her relative Mordecai. This was the first concrete clue linking Ylka to the [[Gralhund Family|Gralhund]] family's deeper mysteries -- a thread that would take dozens of episodes to fully unravel.

### Ylka's Signature Weapon

Ylka claimed the Song Sword as her own and wielded it throughout Seasons 1 and 2. When she chose the College of War at third level, gaining martial weapon and medium armor proficiency, the Song Sword became central to her combat identity as a warrior-bard. She carried it alongside her concertina into every major battle, from the sewers of the [[Xanathar Guild]] to the heights of the [[Radiant Citadel]].

### Loss and Replacement

After SWOJ was killed by [[Rowan Crooks]] and resurrected in new bodies, Ylka's original gear -- including the Song Sword -- was seized by Rowan and stored in his vault at [[Zhentil Keep]]. During this period, Ylka noted the loss with regret. When offered a sunblade at [[S02E20 - Episode Fifty (S02E20)|S02E20]], she lamented that the party had never recovered her Song Sword, though she noted both weapons shared the finesse property.

SWOJ eventually recovered their stolen gear from [[Rowan Crooks]]'s vault at [[Zhentil Keep]] in the [[The Shadowfell|Shadowfell]], and Ylka was reunited with the Song Sword. By [[S02E24 - Episode Fifty Four (S02E24)|S02E24]], she was once again wielding the blade in combat, parrying [[Rowan Crooks]]'s sunblade attacks while the Song Sword sang out defiantly.
