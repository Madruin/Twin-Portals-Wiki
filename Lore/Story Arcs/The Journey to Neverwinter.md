# The Journey to Neverwinter

**Seasons:** 2
**Episodes:** [[S02E05 - Episode Thirty Five (S02E05)|S02E05]] - [[S02E21 - Episode Fifty One (S02E21)|S02E21]]
**Status:** Resolved

## Summary

After returning from the Shadowfell to discover three years had passed, SWOJ found themselves thrust into a Sword Coast embroiled in war. [[Waterdeep]] and [[Neverwinter]] were fighting over the [[Cassalanters]] diamond mines, [[Fela Lefaliir]] had been kidnapped, Scoot's mother [[Tagga]] was bedridden with a spore illness, and [[Renaer Neverember]] had gone missing. This sprawling arc took SWOJ from Waterdeep to [[Amphail]], through the [[Kryptwood]], across the seas to the [[Radiant Citadel]], on pirate adventures at [[Driftwood]], and ultimately into the heart of Neverwinter's political intrigue.

The journey north to Amphail introduced the [[Hare Company]] -- Waterdeep army deserters who SWOJ convinced to return to duty -- and the town of Amphail under military occupation. There, mysterious mists from the [[Kryptwood]] were causing disappearances and memory loss. SWOJ tracked the source to [[Arcturia]] (Arcturia), Xae's sister, who operated from a [[Spiral Cavern]] beneath the [[Sword Mountains]]. Arcturia used transmutation magic to transform captives, including the druids of the [[Circle Of Dusk]] and Scoot's shipmate [[Leah]], who was turned into a six-eyed beast. The party discovered that [[Farron]], their amnesiac guide, was actually [[Zelifarn]] the bronze dragon under Arcturia's enchantment. When Ylka smashed the enchanted mirror sustaining the illusions, Arcturia retreated into a [[Concord Jewel]] -- a crystalline transport vessel -- dragging SWOJ to the [[Radiant Citadel]] in the Deep Ethereal Plane.

The Radiant Citadel arc saw SWOJ explore this egalitarian floating city, battle shadow creatures at the Preserve of the Ancestors, and sail with [[Jarlaxle]] and the [[Bregan D'Aerthe]] to [[Devil's' Rock]], where they entered the [[Deep Dragon Temple]]. There, the Breathless Elder -- a dead viper fish deity from the [[The Far Realm]] -- held dragon spirits captive via tethers to its body. SWOJ freed the spirits but were killed when Lark severed the Elder's power gem, only to be resurrected in the Museum of the Multiverse's Stadium of Souls. After escaping through the [[The Bag Dimension]] with the help of [[Fioleth]], they emerged aboard Jarlaxle's ship outside Neverwinter.

In Neverwinter, SWOJ navigated a web of political intrigue: [[Dagult Neverember]] ruled from the [[Hall Of Justice]], the [[The Masks]] mercenary faction controlled the city from the shadows, and Renaer led an underground resistance from tunnels beneath [[Castle Never]]. SWOJ was framed for the murder of [[Slippery Shim]] and became fugitives. They descended into the [[Neverwinter]] chasm with a Manshoon clone ("Man-two"), retrieved the [[Castle Neverwinter]] from Castle Never's crypts (confronting the banshee of [[Lady Lissandra Never]]), and participated in a war council that united Dagult, Renaer, Jarlaxle, and [[Remalia Haventree]] against the combined threats of Manshoon's forces and the chasm's instability.

## Resolution

The journey culminated in a war council that united the fractured forces of the Sword Coast against Manshoon's army. [[Melifarn]] offered to seal the Neverwinter chasm using the [[Castle Neverwinter]] and crystals from the Amphail cavern. SWOJ infiltrated the Spiral Cavern, harvested the crystals, and witnessed Manshoon conferring with an ancient black dragon -- revealing the scope of the enemy's forces. The arc set the stage for the final confrontation with Rowan Crooks at [[Zhentil Keep]] and the subsequent assault on the [[Cult Of The Dragon]] at [[Castle Naerytar]].
