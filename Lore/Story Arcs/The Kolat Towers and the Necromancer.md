# The Kolat Towers and the Necromancer

**Seasons:** 1
**Episodes:** [[S01E21 - Episode Twenty One|S01E21]] - [[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]
**Status:** Resolved (leads into the Hunt for Manshoon)

## Summary

The mysterious Necromancer was first contacted by [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] through a scrying pool in the Short Rest's cellar, offering to help resurrect [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]. This young-looking wizard with a mechanical hand operated from an extra-dimensional sanctum accessible through the [[Kolat Towers]] -- the [[Zhentarim]]'s base of operations in [[Waterdeep]].

SWOJ infiltrated the towers by teleporting from [[Gralhund Villa]]'s teleportation circle using the passphrase "Tuatha Erinji." Inside, they encountered [[Urstul Floxin]] and other Zhentarim operatives, ascended seven trapped floors (including a flesh golem), and passed through a portal using M-emblazoned rings into the Necromancer's sanctum. There, the Necromancer performed the clone spell that resurrected Lark.

The most shocking revelation came when SWOJ discovered [[Zilka]] -- the original person from whom Ylka had been cloned -- shackled in a construct workshop, forced to repair [[Nim]] (a golem). The Necromancer confirmed that Ylka was a clone created by her grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund]] to protect Zilka's soul. His vault contained a ledger with bookmarked names including [[Grim Wavesilver]] (Lark's father), and the [[Mask of the Cultivator]] that transformed Lark's appearance.

The towers exploded as SWOJ escaped, with [[Squidly]] triumphantly revealing he had pickpocketed the second Eye of the [[Stone of Golorr]] from Urstul Floxin. The Necromancer's true identity was not revealed until [[Rowan Crooks]] extracted a Gem of Seeing from Lark's temple at the end of Season 1 and declared the Necromancer to be [[Manshoon]], founder of the Zhentarim -- the man who had been spying on SWOJ through Lark's head ever since the resurrection.

## Resolution

The Kolat Towers were destroyed in an explosion. The Necromancer's identity as Manshoon was revealed by Rowan Crooks, setting up the Hunt for Manshoon that would drive Season 2. Ylka's identity as a clone became a defining element of her character, and the Mask of the Cultivator permanently changed Lark's appearance.
