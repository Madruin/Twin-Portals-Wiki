# The Hunt for Manshoon

**Seasons:** 1-2
**Episodes:** [[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]] - [[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]]
**Status:** Resolved

## Summary

The hunt for [[Manshoon]], founder of the [[Zhentarim]] and the wizard behind the Necromancer identity, was the overarching antagonist thread that bridged Seasons 1 and 2. Manshoon's defining trait was his use of clones -- thirteen copies of himself scattered across the planes, each with different abilities and forms. Defeating Manshoon meant tracking down and eliminating his clones while unraveling his web of alliances.

At the end of Season 1, [[Rowan Crooks]] revealed that the Necromancer who resurrected Lark was Manshoon, and that he had implanted a Gem of Seeing in Lark's temple to spy on SWOJ. The party stepped through twin portals into the [[Feywild]] to begin the hunt, accompanied by the [[Jolly Gents]]. Using a compass crafted from Lark's gemstone and blood, they tracked Manshoon clones across the planes.

Season 2 saw SWOJ encounter multiple Manshoon clones: the Body Taker (a plant-based clone inside the [[Great Beast of Erua]]'s Dynamo of Possibility), a clone called "Man-two" made of ice and water who accompanied them into the [[Neverwinter]] chasm before being destroyed by an anti-magic rune, and ultimately Rowan Crooks himself -- who was revealed to be yet another Manshoon clone, the leader of the Jolly Gents having been a clone all along.

The climactic confrontation came at [[Zentil Keep]] in the [[Shadowfell]], where SWOJ defeated Rowan Crooks atop the floating fortress and trapped him inside a Soul Stone after destroying his phylactery. This victory was followed by a dream test from the [[Raven Queen]], who acknowledged SWOJ's worthiness. Manshoon's forces -- including his alliance with [[Amalia Cassalanter]] (as Velia Shadowmantle) and the [[Cult of the Dragon]] -- continued to threaten the Sword Coast through the Radiant Citadel arc.

## Resolution

Rowan Crooks was defeated and trapped in a Soul Stone at Zentil Keep. Manshoon's alliance with the Cult of the Dragon was dismantled during the Radiant Citadel arc. After the defeat of his forces, the [[Doom Raiders]] filled the Zhentarim power vacuum in Waterdeep. The Soul Stone containing Crooks became a critical item that enemies repeatedly tried to steal.
