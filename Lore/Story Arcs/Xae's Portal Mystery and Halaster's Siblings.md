# Xae's Portal Mystery and Halaster's Siblings

**Seasons:** 1-2
**Episodes:** [[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]] - [[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]]
**Status:** Ongoing (contract still active)

## Summary

[[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]'s personal arc was one of the campaign's most far-reaching storylines, threading through both seasons and connecting to the campaign's deepest mythology. It began at the Founder's Day feast in Season 1, when Xae signed a contract with an avatar of [[Asmodeus]] himself to save the [[Cassalanters]] children [[Terenzio Cassalanter|Terenzio]] and [[Elzerina Cassalanter|Elzerina]]. The terms were devastating: free six "brothers and sisters" -- [[Jhesiyra]], [[Rantantar]], [[Lyrian]], [[Trobriand]], [[Muiral]], and [[Arcturia]] -- within thirteen Founder's Days, or forfeit her own soul.

These six figures turned out to be [[Halaster Blackcloak]]'s apprentices, legendary wizards trapped or scattered across the planes. Xae's quest to find and free them drove much of the campaign's planar exploration and introduced the concept of the twin portals -- the very name of the campaign. The portals connected different planes of existence, and SWOJ's journey through them formed the backbone of their adventures.

The connection to [[Arcturia]] (Arcturia) was the most personal revelation. When SWOJ encountered the transmutation witch in the [[Spiral Cavern]] beneath the [[Sword Mountains]], tormenting the people of [[Amphail]] with memory-altering mists, Arcturia was revealed as Xae's sister. Arcturia told Xae telepathically to "follow me -- break the wall" and declared she served "the master" -- Halaster, "the most powerful wizard in the multiverse." This encounter linked Xae's contract obligation, her family history, and the campaign's overarching mythology into a single thread.

The Concord Jewels -- crystalline vessels that transported travelers between the material plane and the [[Radiant Citadel]] -- were tied to Halaster's apprentices as well. [[Fetch]], the fragment of [[Manshoon]]'s soul inside the [[Dynamo Of Possibility]], had previously worked for [[Rantantar]], one of the six Xae needed to free. The [[The Breathless Elder]] in the [[The Far Realm]] and the [[Wall Of The Faithless]] both connected to the campaign's deepest cosmological mysteries about the boundaries between planes.

Throughout Season 2, portal instability plagued the Sword Coast. [[Grinda Garloth]] of the Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors noted the growing instability, and the chasm beneath [[Neverwinter]] -- where planes of existence intersected -- was a physical manifestation of this disruption. The campaign's name, Twin Portals, referenced these connections between worlds that SWOJ traversed throughout their journey.

## Resolution

As of the end of Season 2, Xae's contract with Asmodeus remained active. Arcturia was encountered but not freed, and the full list of Halaster's apprentices -- Jhesiyra, Rantantar, Mayrin, Trobriand, Muiral, and Arcturia -- had not yet been located and liberated. The breaking of the Wall of the Faithless may have advanced or complicated this quest. The contract's thirteen-Founder's-Day deadline continued to hang over Xae's soul, making this one of the campaign's most personal and unresolved threads heading into any future continuation.
