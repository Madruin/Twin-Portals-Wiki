# The Jolly Gents

**Seasons:** 1-2
**Episodes:** [[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]] - [[S02E25 - Episode Fifty Five LIVE (S02E25)|S02E25]]
**Status:** Resolved

## Summary

The Jolly Gents were a legendary adventuring group led by [[Rowan Crooks]] who allied with SWOJ at the end of Season 1 to hunt [[Manshoon]] clones across the planes. What began as a heroic partnership gradually revealed itself to be one of the campaign's greatest betrayals: Rowan Crooks was himself the fourteenth clone of Manshoon, having murdered the real Rowan Crooks long ago and assumed his identity.

The Jolly Gents joined SWOJ as they stepped through twin portals into the [[Feywild]], with Rowan splitting off to pursue a separate lead while leaving three members -- [[Antidiluvian Jake]], [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]], and [[Lerian]] -- to accompany SWOJ. The group's members were presented as colorful, capable adventurers: Jake the scholarly lore keeper with his obsidian tablet, Ann Jane the dependable fighter, and [[Little Joan]], [[Friar Truck]], and [[Sixteen String Jack]] operating in other capacities. The party even encountered Jolly Gents trading cards, suggesting they were cultural icons in the setting.

Cracks in the alliance appeared gradually. Inside the [[Great Beast of Erua]], Jake and Ann Jane were captured and replaced by podlings. After their rescue, Rowan Crooks captured [[Fetch]]'s soul over SWOJ's objections. The Jolly Gents ambushed SWOJ at the [[Deep Dragon Temple]], where Rowan Crooks, Little Joan, and Friar Truck murdered the Manshoon clone Shuni and attacked the party. Rowan was unmasked as the fourteenth Manshoon clone, and the resulting catastrophe killed SWOJ (who were later resurrected). In the [[Neverwinter]] chasm, the remaining Jolly Gents -- Joan, Jack, and Jake -- ambushed SWOJ again, stealing green power stones from the [[Hungry Elder]], though Jake secretly passed his obsidian tablet to [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] and whispered "we're all prisoners of Crooks."

Jake's confession revealed that not all Jolly Gents served Manshoon willingly. Some were coerced, suggesting a more complex relationship between the organization and its secretly villainous leader.

## Resolution

The Jolly Gents were effectively dismantled by the end of Season 2. Rowan Crooks was defeated at [[Zentil Keep]] and trapped inside a [[Soul Stone]]. Friar Truck died in the Breathless Elder explosion. Little Joan was left barely alive after the same blast. Jake's secret rebellion against Crooks -- passing intelligence to SWOJ and whispering "we're all prisoners of Crooks" -- revealed that at least some members were coerced rather than complicit. The organization's legacy was complicated: they were genuinely legendary adventurers whose leader had been replaced by an evil clone, turning their reputation into a weapon against the very heroes they were supposed to be.
