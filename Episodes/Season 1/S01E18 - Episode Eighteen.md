## Summary

This is not a regular gameplay episode. It is a behind-the-scenes discussion between DM Matias Valero and player/producer Scott Hebert, recorded during the COVID-19 pandemic lockdown in April 2020, approximately seven weeks after their last recording session. They discuss the origins of their D&D inspirations -- Matias cites Chris Perkins' Dice Camera Action and Matt Mercer's Critical Role as influences, while Scott mentions The Adventure Zone and Hello from the Magic Tavern as gateway media. They reflect on the live show performance where Lark was disintegrated by the Xanathar's beam, discussing the dramatic weight of character death in D&D and how Justin (Lark's player) handled it.

Scott and Matias discuss the possibility of bringing Lark back from disintegration, noting that True Resurrection would be needed since only ashes remain. They brainstorm potential story avenues including selling Trollskull Manor to fund the resurrection. They also discuss plans to continue recording remotely during the pandemic, acknowledging the challenges of transitioning from in-person to online play. The episode serves as a bridge between the dramatic events of Episode 17 and the continuation of the campaign.

### Key Events

- Meta-discussion episode recorded during COVID-19 lockdown (April 2020, ~7 weeks since last session)
- DM and producer discuss D&D inspirations: Chris Perkins (Dice Camera Action), Matt Mercer (Critical Role), The Adventure Zone, Hello from the Magic Tavern
- Reflection on Lark's disintegration in the live show and its dramatic impact
- Discussion of bringing Lark back via True Resurrection (since only ashes remain, Raise Dead or Revivify won't work)
- Brainstorming selling Trollskull Manor to fund the resurrection
- Plans to continue recording remotely during the pandemic
