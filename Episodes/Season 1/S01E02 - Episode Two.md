## Summary

The party continued their exploration of the Zhentarim warehouse on Candle Lane, picking up from the discovery of three corpses in black leather armor. Moving deeper inside, they found the warehouse interior was a two-story space with a balcony overlooking a large storage area. On the ground level, twelve more corpses lay scattered among overturned tables and chairs, weapons strewn nearby. The bodies belonged to two different factions -- some in black leather armor (Zhentarim) and others in ragtag padded armor and splint mail -- suggesting the two groups had fought each other. Ylka cast dancing lights to illuminate the dim space, and Xaerine cast speak with animals to try communicating with whatever had been making noise.

The party was ambushed by four kenku -- crow-headed humanoid creatures that mimic voices they have heard. One slashed Xaerine's neck from behind, declaring "The Zhentarim sends its regards" in a stolen voice, while another dropped onto Scoot from the ceiling. Ylka healed Xaerine with a healing word, and Lark killed one kenku with a rapier sneak attack. The party fought their way through the kenku, with Scoot using his greataxe and Xaerine striking back with her dagger. A fourth kenku, wearing a distinctive pink hat, was captured alive. This kenku, dubbed "Pinker," revealed through its mimicked phrases that it worked for the Xanathar Guild and that the party should "follow the yellow signs in the sewers" to find their boss. Pinker bore a tattoo of a circle with ten spokes -- the symbol of the Xanathar Guild.

Beneath the balcony staircase, the party discovered a closet where Renaer Neverember had been hiding. Renaer was a handsome man with hip-length red-gold blonde hair, the son of Dagult Neverember, the deposed former Open Lord of Waterdeep. He explained that the Zhentarim had kidnapped him and Floon from Fillet Lane after they left the Skewered Dragon. The Xanathar Guild then attacked the warehouse, and the kenku took Floon -- mistaking him for Renaer -- presumably to the Xanathar's lair in the sewers. Renaer revealed that both factions were searching for the Stone of Golorr, an artifact needed to locate a massive hoard of gold (dragons) that Dagult Neverember had embezzled and hidden in the city. The party released Pinker with misleading phrases and a gold coin, and Renaer armed himself to join the search for Floon. Shortly after, Captain Eustace Staggerd of the City Watch arrived with approximately thirteen men in a raid formation, but upon recognizing Renaer and Lark's Wavesilver signet, he stood down and allowed them to leave.
