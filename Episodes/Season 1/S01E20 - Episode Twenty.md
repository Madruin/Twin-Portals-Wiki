## Summary

Episode 20 is an out-of-character reunion/check-in episode recorded February 1, 2021 -- the first time all five cast members have been together in 315 days since the last live recording on March 1, 2020. Beth celebrates her 32nd birthday during the recording. The cast shares pandemic life updates: Beth moved a couple hours south of Duluth so her husband John (Brofie, who played Thaelin) could attend theater grad school, and she continues teaching middle and high school band and choir; Kendra works from home for the University of Minnesota Duluth while raising her three-and-a-half-year-old daughter; Matias works from home as an environmental engineer on the North Shore; Scott continues at the hospital; and Justin has been making short films, launching internet businesses, and doing a murder mystery fundraiser over Zoom.

They discuss their canceled theater shows -- Beth had just opened her one-woman show about Rachel Corrie before theaters shut down, Kendra had performed in Matilda the Musical (getting 8 performances in before shutdown), and Justin's Spamalot production never got to open. Beth has been studying Greek as a creative outlet, Kendra has been DMing bedtime D&D adventures for her preschooler, Scott has been using his fire pit weekly, and Justin has been making puzzles and movies.

The conversation pivots to Twin Portals: they discuss the 'Lark Lives' sticker campaign, speculate about how Lark could return (comparing it to Gandalf, Orpheus, Theseus), and joke about various resurrection scenarios. They discuss what they've been doing for fun during the pandemic -- board games (Azul, Patchwork, Codenames Duets, Gloomhaven, Hero Quest), video games, baking experiments, and Beth's viral Facebook group 'How Absolute Dare' about angry animals which grew to 25,000 members. The episode ends with the cast expressing how much they miss each other and Scott noting this is his first time editing five separate audio sources.
