## Summary

This is a live episode. The morning begins at Trollskull Manor with the ghost Lif cooking eggs for the party. A pigeon arrives from Pog Waggletop, a goblin ex-captain of the ship Sea Plum, reporting that his ship has sunk in Deepwater Harbor under mysterious circumstances. The party travels to the Sea Maiden's Faire on the docks to find Pog, where they meet Zardos Zord, the enigmatic carnival master. Zardos gives them a handkerchief to deliver to Grinda Garloth, an elderly woman living in a lighthouse at Mistshore, in exchange for help with the investigation.

At the lighthouse, Grinda reveals something troubling: Ylka apparently visited her the previous night and borrowed the Apparatus of Kwalish (a lobster-shaped submersible barrel), but Ylka has no memory of this. Despite the mystery, Grinda allows the party to use the Apparatus to investigate the wreck. The party takes potions of water breathing and descends into Deepwater Harbor, where they encounter a great white shark and then Zelifarn, a bronze dragon who serves as the self-appointed guardian of the harbor.

Zelifarn leads them to the Sea Plum wreck, where they discover the cargo hold has been emptied and the hull was breached from the outside. The dragon then guides them to a smuggler's cave beneath the city, which they follow upward through tunnels until they emerge inside the God Catcher -- a massive statue in the Castle Ward shaped like a figure reaching skyward. Inside, they encounter a colony of wererats who are aggressive but ultimately bypassed. Further up, they discover imprisoned sailors and a drow named Fel'rekt who was captured by cultists. A cultist wearing House Wavesilver insignia is killed during the encounter, deepening the mystery surrounding Lark's family.

The party ascends to the palm of the God Catcher's outstretched hand, where they confront a woman who looks exactly like Ylka performing a blood ritual. A fight ensues with this Ylka doppelganger, during which Lark is knocked off the hand and nearly killed (saved by feather fall). The doppelganger leaps off the hand and vanishes. Below, the party finds Terry the Zuber driver has been murdered -- run through with a blade. The episode ends on this dark cliffhanger.
