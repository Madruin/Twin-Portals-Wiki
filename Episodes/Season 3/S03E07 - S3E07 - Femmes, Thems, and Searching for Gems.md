## Summary

Beth Brophy hosts a roundtable discussion about the experiences of women and femme-identifying people in tabletop gaming. She is joined by Bailey Fazio, Laura O'Brien (Scott Hebert's partner), and Scott as producer. Each participant shares their D&D origin story: Beth grew up in small-town Byron, Minnesota, majored in bassoon, played World of Warcraft, and joined Twin Portals through theater connections. Bailey was introduced to D&D by Matias through Duluth theater circles around 2012-2013. Laura started playing in the early 2000s, learned to DM from her first group, and has played numerous systems including D&D, GURPS, White Wolf, and Feng Shui.

The heart of the episode focuses on building safe and inclusive gaming tables. Laura discusses forming an all-women D&D group (a wizard's tower dungeon crawl) and how the dynamic at an all-femme table was notably different and immediately comfortable. They share stories of uncomfortable or unsafe table experiences, including Laura walking out on a DM who graphically described assault, and having to remove a player who used slurs at a table where her queer child was present. They discuss the importance of session zeros, setting boundaries, the evolution of D&D from a male-dominated space to near gender parity at conventions like Comic-Con and Dragon Con.

The conversation also covers DMing philosophy, creating inclusive spaces for queer and non-binary players, the therapeutic value of D&D for young people, and the broader cultural shift making tabletop gaming accessible to everyone. They discuss how every high school and most middle schools in Duluth now have D&D clubs.

### Key Events

- Beth hosts first femme-focused panel in Twin Portals history
- Each participant shares their D&D origin story and path to tabletop gaming
- Laura describes forming an all-women D&D group as a transformative experience
- Candid stories about leaving unsafe tables and removing problem players
- Discussion of D&D as therapeutic tool for queer youth
- Statistics shared showing near-equal gender representation at major gaming conventions
- Emphasis on session zero practices and ongoing communication as essential for safe tables
