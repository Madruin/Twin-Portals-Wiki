## Summary

Matias and Scott return for another casual mini-painting session, discussing the current state and future plans for Twin Portals. The episode opens with acknowledgment that Twin Portals is no longer funded by the Minnesota State Arts Board, giving them more freedom with content. They paint various miniatures including a shale heart, a periton, and an aboleth, discussing painting techniques like dry brushing, washes, and base coating.

The conversation covers a wide range of planning topics: upcoming episodes including a session zero discussion episode and a potential FTG (Femmes, Thems, and Gems) follow-up, getting back to regular story content, and the possibility of 'spicy' adult-themed D&D content using alternative game systems like Vices and Violence. Matias discusses plans for a West Marches collaborative campaign that would connect Discord community games with recorded studio episodes, creating a shared universe where player actions in online games affect the recorded show.

They also discuss ExcaliburCon 2026 (August 15-16) where Matias is heavily involved in TTRPG programming, the idea of a 'Twin Portals lore dump' episode revealing unused DM material, building a five-room dungeon from unused encounter ideas, and Scott's fascination with sumo wrestling and the rise of Ukrainian wrestler Atamifuji (referred to as Ianishiki). They reflect on the importance of continuing to create art during difficult times, with Anders having recently noted it feels like 'fiddling while Rome burns.' Chrono CCG is mentioned as approaching beta by end of March.

### Key Events

- Twin Portals transitions away from arts board funding, gaining content freedom
- Plans laid out for West Marches campaign connecting Discord and studio episodes
- Discussion of session zero episode and communication-focused content
- Exploration of adult-themed D&D content as possible future direction
- ExcaliburCon 2026 planning with call for DMs and players
- Lore dump episode concept: revealing unused DM material and building encounters from cutting room floor
- Reflection on continuing creative work during difficult political/social times
- Original 2018 session zero document discovered during studio cleaning
