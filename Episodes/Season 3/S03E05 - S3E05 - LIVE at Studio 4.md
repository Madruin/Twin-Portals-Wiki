## Summary

This is a LIVE GAMEPLAY episode, not a meta-discussion episode like the others in Season 3. It was recorded on stage at Studio 4 by the Depot in Duluth with a live audience. This is the first episode of a brand new campaign set in Icewind Dale during an unseasonably warm autumn in 1497 DR, where the snow is melting and the region is in disarray from high waters.

The episode introduces four new player characters: Bitty Boy (a human barbarian played by Scott, described as muscular and pulling a colorful wagon), Parfornell (a Kenku cleric played by Beth Brophy who can only mimic phrases she has heard), Ederly Stonefeather (an Owlin wizard played by Bailey Fazio, a three-foot-tall burrowing owl person), and Mormo (a human warlock played by Anders Holtstrom, interested in archaeology and theater, blessed by the Luxon). They are joined on stage by Matias as DM.

The party travels north toward the town of Caer-Konig, encounters mysterious blue wisps in the wilderness, explores ancient ruins with draconic inscriptions, and eventually discovers they are standing on the back of a sleeping tarrasque. They battle a vine creature with three masks (happy, sad, and angry) that was trying to wake and control the tarrasque, using a combination of fire magic, bardic performance, and a draconic lullaby carved into the tarrasque's scales to calm the beast and send it peacefully on its way.

### Key Events

- New campaign begins in Icewind Dale with entirely new characters at level 3
- Party encounters mysterious blue wisps on the road north
- They discover a Fey amphitheater run by hags where they must perform monthly
- Exploration of ancient ruins with draconic inscriptions about a guardian
- Revelation that the valley floor is actually the back of a sleeping tarrasque
- Battle against a vine creature with three masks trying to control the tarrasque
- Party uses draconic lullaby and fire magic to defeat the vine creature
- Tarrasque is calmed and walks away peacefully after the party sings to it
- Kiro the half-orc artificer arrives at the end
