## Summary

Beth Brophy and Justin join Matias for a roundtable discussion about the intersection of parenthood and creative pursuits, particularly tabletop gaming. The conversation opens with candid stories about their experiences in the delivery room, with Justin nearly passing out and Beth falling asleep during labor. They share the raw, honest experiences of becoming parents while maintaining their creative lives.

The three discuss how having children has changed their approach to D&D and creative projects, including the challenges of scheduling game sessions around childcare, finding time for hobbies, and the guilt that comes with taking personal creative time away from family. They talk about introducing their children to nerdy interests like D&D, video games, and mythology, and the joy of watching their kids develop their own geeky passions.

The episode also covers broader parenting topics including sleep deprivation, managing screen time, the evolution of their identities as both parents and artists, and how Twin Portals has served as an important creative outlet and social connection for adults with families. Scott was notably absent from this episode, giving the parents space to have this conversation among themselves.

### Key Events

- Candid delivery room stories from all three parents
- Discussion of how parenthood changed their relationship to creative hobbies
- Sharing strategies for introducing kids to D&D and nerdy culture
- Reflection on Twin Portals as an important adult social outlet
- Scott absent from episode, giving parents space for the conversation
