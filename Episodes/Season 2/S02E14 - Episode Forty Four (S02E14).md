## Summary

SWOJ tumbles out of the bag of holding into Jarlaxle Baenre's cabin aboard the Eyecatcher, one of the ships of the Sea Maiden's Fair, anchored outside Neverwinter. Jarlaxle, freshly out of bed and wearing only a towel, greets them warmly and wins a bet that the party would survive. The party catches Jarlaxle up on their adventures: encountering the Rowan Crooks/Manshoon clone, dying, being resurrected, and escaping through the bag dimension. Teriador, in his drow body, stays behind on the ship to learn drow ways, while Phylith accompanies SWOJ ashore to seek work as a craftsman.

Jarlaxle provides the party with equipment and exposition about Neverwinter. Scoot finds a displacer beast fur cloak that grants the avoidance trait (half damage on failed saves, no damage on successful ones). Lark gets a new red hat from the ship's Milliner -- featuring hidden compartments, a collapsible design, and 50 gold coins lining the brim. The anchor pipe maul is silvered. Jarlaxle explains the political situation: Lord Dagult Neverember rules from the Hall of Justice (not Castle Never, which is haunted), the war with Waterdeep continues over the Cassalanter diamond mines, the Masks are a mercenary faction with troubling influence over the city, and Renaer Neverember leads an underground resistance against his father. Jarlaxle asks SWOJ to put in a good word with Neverwinter's leadership so his ships can trade openly.

The party takes the Scarlet Marpenoth, Jarlaxle's submersible, to the northwestern shore of Neverwinter under cover of darkness. Using pass without trace, they make their way through the city, noting propaganda posters, guards, and the key landmarks: the Sleeping Dragon Bridge, Castle Never (dark and abandoned), the Hall of Justice (a colosseum-like fortress), and the Moonstone Mask (a floating island inn chained above the harbor). At the Moonstone Mask, they discover that the staff uses sending stones to share information. They reunite with Felrecht Laphine, a Bregan D'aerthe agent disguised with a braided beard, who introduces them to Slippery Shim -- a towering black-furred tabaxi smuggler and information broker.

Slippery Shim shares intelligence: he witnessed an assassin ambush a Masks lieutenant on the road from the war front, but when stabbed, the lieutenant didn't bleed. The assassin was captured and taken to the Hall of Justice dungeon. The Masks send this lieutenant back and forth monthly under cover of night. Felrecht directs SWOJ to the House of a Thousand Faces, a clothing boutique that serves as the Harpers' base in Neverwinter. There they reunite with Squidly, now a teenage Harper agent, who provides them with Masks uniforms and disguises for infiltrating the Hall of Justice.

**NPCs:**

Appears at the Moonstone Mask disguised as a human with a braided beard and half-face mask. Has been in Neverwinter for a tenday gathering information. Introduces SWOJ to Slippery Shim. His backward alias is 'Left to Kerr.' Later disappears after the murder at the Moonstone Mask.
- [[Slippery Shim]] — A towering black-furred tabaxi smuggler and independent information broker wearing a trench coat. Met through Felrecht at the Moonstone Mask. Witnessed an assassin attack a Masks lieutenant on the road from the war front -- the lieutenant didn't bleed when stabbed. Provides intelligence about the Masks' monthly convoys and the assassin in the Hall of Justice dungeon.
- [[Squidly]] — A former child NPC from Season 1, now a teenager working as a Harper agent at the House of a Thousand Faces in Neverwinter. Provides SWOJ with Masks uniforms and disguises for their infiltration plan.
- [[Teriador]] — The Rose Knight in a drow body. Stays aboard Jarlaxle's ship to learn drow ways and better adapt to his new form rather than accompanying SWOJ into Neverwinter.
- [[Fioleth]] — The ragamuffin from the bag dimension who accompanies SWOJ ashore. Identifies as a 'ragamuffin' species. Plans to find work as a craftsman in Neverwinter. Folds up into a pile of clothes to sleep.
- [[Lord Dagult Neverember]] — Lord of Neverwinter who rules from the Hall of Justice. Credited with rebuilding the city after Mount Hotenow erupted 30 years ago and sealing the demonic rift in the chasm. Has imposed harsher taxes on the guilds but is seen as a people's leader. His relationship with his son Renaer is strained.
- [[Renaer Neverember]] — Son of Lord Neverember. Came to Neverwinter years ago trying to oust his father. Now leads an underground resistance against the Masks' influence. Keeps a very low profile. SWOJ knows him from Waterdeep. Not physically encountered this episode.
