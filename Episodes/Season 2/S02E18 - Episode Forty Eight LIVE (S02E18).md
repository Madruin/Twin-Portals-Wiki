## Summary

After resting overnight following the chasm crisis, SWOJ is summoned by Lord Dagult Neverember, who hires them to retrieve the Crown of Neverwinter from Castle Never. The crown can control heat and cold and will melt or freeze the head of anyone who is not the rightful heir. Payment is 5,000 gold each plus property in the city. Neverember also warns that the Masks -- a mercenary force -- are pulling out of the city and may be preparing to claim it. Renaer Neverember contacts the party via whisper magic, asking them to find the crown for him instead, and provides historical context: the Allegondar family ruled Neverwinter, but the original founders were Lord Halueth Never and his wife Lissandra Never.

Guard captain Tresa escorts SWOJ to Castle Never, where they are guided by a halfling carpenter. The castle is filled with ash, spider webs, and possibly haunted by the old royal court. After meeting a rhyming raven who confirms the crown is likely in the original tower's throne room, the party ascends through increasingly ash-covered corridors. They find the dead beholder that was rumored to inhabit the castle -- long deceased and picked clean by thousands of small spiders. Among the beholder's hoard, they discover a ghostly pan flute, a vine-wrapped staff, a shadow inkwell with a vulture quill, gold coins, and a beholder tooth.

Entering the throne room, SWOJ spots a mysterious figure in a black cloak and red hat who melts into the shadows -- likely connected to the Zentarim assassin Erstell Floxen. The throne room contains a giant spider named Coralith the Starweaver and a silver scale with an inscription reading 'What must a ruler balance?' with the heavy side labeled 'Power.' Xae wild shapes into a spider to read the inscription, then communicates with Coralith via Speak with Animals. Lark offers his constellation watch as a gift, satisfying Coralith, who reveals the scales control a mechanism within the throne and have been out of balance for 30 years since the Neverembers took over. Coralith smashes the throne, revealing a hidden compartment containing a key and a map to the crypts below.

Following the map through the haunted castle -- encountering the ghost of the dead beholder Talgath the Undying, spectral courtiers in a grand ballroom where Scoot and Ylka impress with their dancing (earning a ghostly band escort), and fire-throwing skeletal hands -- SWOJ reaches the crypts. They find two doors: gold scales (Tyr, god of justice) and silver scales (Kelemvor, god of the dead and judgment). They open both, finding Lord Halueth Never's ornate tomb behind the gold door and the simple stone coffin of Lady Lissandra Never behind the silver door. They open Lissandra's coffin and find a skeleton wearing the mithril Crown of Neverwinter with a large silver gemstone and red-and-blue gems in a wavy pattern.

As Xae lifts the crown, the ghost of Lady Lissandra Never rises from the skeleton and lets out a banshee wail, dropping Scoot to 1 HP (saved by halfling Relentless Endurance). The party battles her while also being ambushed by Erstell Floxen, the Zentarim assassin in the red hat, who slashes Scoot's throat with a poison dagger and fires an exploding crossbow bolt. Scoot rages and fights Floxen with his great axe, while Ylka heals Scoot and casts Spirit Guardians. Lark eventually persuades Lady Lissandra to stand down by playing the ghostly pan flute and telling their full story. Lissandra calms, nods approvingly, and places a ghostly hand on Lark's shoulder before fading. Scoot knocks Floxen unconscious rather than killing him. The episode ends with SWOJ holding the Crown of Neverwinter, uncertain whether to give it to Dagult or Renaer Neverember, and Lark declining to try it on.

**NPCs:**
- [[Lord Dagult Neverember]] — Lord Protector of Neverwinter. Hires SWOJ to retrieve the Crown of Neverwinter from Castle Never, offering 5,000 gold each plus property. Warns about the Masks mercenary force pulling out of the city.
- [[Renaer Neverember]] — Son of Lord Dagult Neverember. Contacts SWOJ via whisper magic, also wanting the Crown of Neverwinter. Provides historical context about the original founding family -- Lord Halueth Never and his wife -- and the Allegondar family who ruled after them.
- [[Tressa]] — Guard captain who escorts SWOJ to Castle Never. Has a wandering accent described as going from Chicago to other American cities.
- [[Halfling Carpenter]] — A halfling carpenter working on Castle Never's restoration who shows SWOJ around the castle's lower levels before they ascend to the upper floors.
- [[Rhyming Raven]] — A mysterious raven in Castle Never that speaks in rhymes. Confirms that the crown is likely in the original tower's throne room. Xae communicates with it using Speak with Animals. Disappears after they enter the throne room.
- [[Quorlith The Starweaver]] — A giant spider inhabiting the throne room of Castle Never. Eyes the size of beach balls, dozens of them. She has thousands of baby spiders and weaves constellations and star maps into her webs on the ceiling. Speaks via Xae's Speak with Animals spell. Accepts Lark's constellation watch as a gift and smashes the throne to reveal the hidden map and key. She has been living in the throne room for at least 30 years.
- [[Talgath The Undying]] — The ghost of the dead beholder found in the antechamber. A spectral beholder floating above his own decomposed corpse. Has no eyes -- all hollowed-out pits -- and claims to be psychic. Demands an offering for safe passage but is easily appeased. Confined to his room by an invisible barrier. Has a Baltimore accent.
- [[Urstul Floxxin]] — A Zentarim assassin with a scarred face who wears a red hat similar to Lark's. Previously fought SWOJ at the Gralhund Villa (Season 1) and the Kolat Towers. Can melt through floors. Ambushes Scoot in the crypts with a poison dagger and fires an exploding crossbow bolt. Knocked unconscious by Scoot rather than killed. Seeks revenge against SWOJ.
- [[Lady Lissandra Never]] — The ghostly spirit of the wife of Lord Halueth Never, the founder of Neverwinter. Rises as a banshee from her coffin when the Crown of Neverwinter is removed. Her wail deals 83 points of damage. After Lark tells their full story and plays the ghostly pan flute, she calms down, nods approvingly, aligns her ghostly crown with the physical one (making it warmer), places a hand on Lark's shoulder, and fades away.
- [[Lord Halueth Never]] — The first king of Neverwinter and founder of the city. His ornate tomb lies behind the gold-scaled door in the crypts, surrounded by nine silver swords in a circle. His inscription reads: 'Swear the oath of his knights and join him in rest.'
