## Summary

Riding the giant scarab beetles Ga and Kra atop the Beast of Erua, SWOJ approached the fortress lashed to the creature's back. The compass pointed directly at the stone structure. They entered the fortress through an arched gateway and found an antechamber containing a sarcophagus and a tapestry depicting the heroic tale of the Rose Knight, Teriador, and his legendary battle against the Beast of Erua. They opened the sarcophagus and the ghost of Teriador emerged, bewildered and missing his sword and armor.

Down a hallway from the antechamber, they found Teriador's reanimated corpse encased in his own chain mail armor, wielding his ruby-pommeled sword. The corpse's hand was covered in rings of sky-blue stone. Teriador's ghost possessed Ylka's body to communicate, and the animated body asked cryptic philosophical questions about justice before attacking. The floor of the fortress collapsed, sending the party plummeting into the Beast of Erua's ribcage -- a vast internal chamber filled with crawling hands, giant hands, and swarms of hairless cats, all made of plant material.

A fierce battle ensued inside the beast's chest cavity. Thail cast a fireball from his wish stone crystal. The animated knight fired crossbow bolts from the far end of the ribcage while the party fought swarms of crawling hands and remaining cat swarms. Xae transformed into a crag cat to charge the knight, while Ylka cast witch bolt, connecting a continuous beam to the armored figure. Scoot used reckless attacks with his flaming great axe to destroy one of the giant hands. Lark danced through combat with rapier and dagger, devastating the hand swarms. Ylka cast dissonant whispers to obliterate the animated knight, which collapsed into plant goo -- revealing it too was a podling, not Teriador's actual body. Only the ring-covered hand tried to crawl away before being destroyed.

After the battle, Xae discovered that the rings -- four matching sky-blue and one darker blue -- were magical. In the far reaches of the ribcage, Xae spotted a laboratory with chemical concoctions, journals, a stone teleportation circle, and a workshop. Nearby were mulch beds with podling bodies growing from hands sticking out of the ground. They also found glowing pods containing captured prisoners, including the real Antidiluvian Jake, Ann Jane Flip-Flap, Volo, and about fifteen others from The Way Inn. Lark cut them all free. Radissera, upon waking, revealed his stolen invention -- the Dynamo of Possibility -- a silvery platinum device capable of imbuing the suggestion of a soul onto inanimate objects. The party realized this was what animated the Beast of Erua and created the podlings. Antidiluvian Jake suggested calling Rowan Crooks back, believing they were close to Manshoon's location, and began an incantation near the teleportation circle. Ylka carried Teriador's armor and sword to return to his sarcophagus, while Thail scouted the staircase leading up through the beast's trachea, hearing ominous sounds from above.
