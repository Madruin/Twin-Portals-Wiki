# Bregan D'Aerthe

A drow mercenary organization led by [[Jarlaxle Baenre]], operating covertly across the Sword Coast. Composed of outcasts from the great drow houses of Menzoberranzan, Bregan D'Aerthe pursues legitimate drow presence in surface cities while running intelligence networks and mercantile operations under various fronts. They are long-standing allies of [[SWOJ]], providing intelligence, operatives, and material support throughout both seasons of the campaign.

**First appearance:** [[S01E07 - Episode Seven|S01E07]]
**Total appearances:** 17 episodes

## Goals and Philosophy

Bregan D'Aerthe's members are self-described "outcasts" -- the "much derided and discarded castaways of the great houses" of Menzoberranzan. They have severed ties with the drow matriarchy of the Underdark and seek to establish themselves on the surface.

Their primary goal is to secure **legitimate drow presence in Waterdeep** and other Sword Coast cities. As Jarlaxle explains when he first reveals himself to SWOJ (S01E10): they want to see Waterdeep "prosper and flourish," positioning themselves as allies of the city rather than threats.

By Season 2, this ambition has expanded to **Neverwinter**, where Jarlaxle seeks trading rights for his fleet. However, his ships are barred from Neverwinter's port on suspicion of being Waterdeep spies. He asks SWOJ to put in a good word with the city's leadership (S02E14).

## Organization and Operations

### The Sea Maiden's Fair

The [[Sea Maiden'S Fair]] is Bregan D'Aerthe's primary cover operation -- a fleet of three ships that serves as a traveling carnival, entertainment troupe, and trading enterprise:

- **The Eyecatcher** -- Jarlaxle's flagship, where he holds court as "Zardos Zord." Contains his personal cabin, a wardrobe full of magical items, and the Milliner's hat-making workshop. Also houses a manta ray-shaped submersible beneath its hull (discovered by SWOJ via [[Zelifarn]] in S01E10).
- **The Heartbreaker** -- One of the three ships in the fleet.
- **The Hell Razor** -- Captained by [[Sarith Ab'Jean]], described as a swashbuckling fellow with a clearly trimmed mustache who runs a tight ship.

The crew of all three ships are **disguised drow**, using magic to appear human. When Xae uses the Ring of Disguise Self to impersonate Zardos in S01E10, a crew member immediately recognizes the deception and sends a magical message to the real Jarlaxle -- demonstrating the crew's loyalty and vigilance.

In Waterdeep (Season 1), the Sea Maiden's Fair operates as a carnival docked in the harbor. By S02E09, the fleet is stationed at [[Driftwood]], a lawless floating pirate island built on the shell of the dragon turtle [[Kurrihan]]. The organization is notably **more heavily armed** than before, with crew members carrying advanced firearms including multi-shot pistols and even a bazooka-like weapon (S02E09).

### Intelligence Network

Bregan D'Aerthe runs an extensive intelligence operation:

- **Tracking factions**: Fel'Rekt Lafeen explains that Zardos tasked him with keeping tabs on the Xanathar Guild and the Zhentarim from the Short Rest area (S01E11).
- **Noble surveillance**: They have intelligence on devil worship among Waterdeep's nobles and the Wavesilver family crest connection (S01E07).
- **Embedded agents**: Nar'l Xibrindas operates as a spy/double agent within the Xanathar Guild itself (S01E16).
- **Arms dealing and trade**: By Season 2, the organization functions as arms dealers and traders in Driftwood, conducting business with halflings and pirates at the Blubber Buckle tavern.

### Twin Portals Technology

Jarlaxle reveals in S01E29 that he has "always found it convenient to give away bags of holding that have twin portals" -- paired bags of holding with connected dimensional spaces that allow passage between them. This is the technology that gives the podcast its name. When dragons flew overhead near the Sea Maiden's Fair, Jarlaxle checked his end of the portal, saw a way in, and emerged with his soldiers into the Cassalanter crypt.

By S02E14, SWOJ uses this same twin portal connection to escape the bag dimension directly into Jarlaxle's cabin aboard the Eyecatcher outside Neverwinter.

## Known Members

### Leadership

- **[[Jarlaxle Baenre]]** -- Founder and leader of Bregan D'Aerthe. See "Jarlaxle's Disguises" below for his many identities.

### Officers and Agents

- **[[Fel'Rekt Lafeen]]** -- Jarlaxle's most prominent lieutenant and SWOJ's primary Bregan D'Aerthe contact. A drow who was born a woman in Menzoberranzan but left the Underdark because he "couldn't stand the way that the matriarchy in the drow culture treated men" and chose to live as a man. Jarlaxle admired the "panache" Fel'Rekt showed in making that change and took him in (S01E11). Fel'Rekt has traveled extensively -- from Menzoberranzan to Waterdeep and across the sea. In Season 1, he operates covertly in Waterdeep gathering intelligence. In S02E09, he greets SWOJ at the Eyecatcher in Driftwood, now carrying four pistols and notably more armed than before, with an eye patch hiding a glowing red drow eye. In S02E14, he is in Neverwinter disguised as a human with a braided beard and half-face mask, using the backward alias "Left to Kerr." He joins SWOJ's crew aboard the Ethereal Galleon for the final assault on the moonhole (S02E31).
- **[[Narl Xibrindas]]** (also: Nar'l Xibrindas) -- A white-haired drow operating as a spy or double agent embedded within the Xanathar Guild. The carny working for Fel'Rekt asks SWOJ to find him inside the Xanathar's lair, and Thorvin Twinbeard also asks the party to send Nar'l his way if they find him (S01E16).
- **[[Sarith Ab'Jean]]** -- Captain of the Hell Razor. A swashbuckling drow with a clearly trimmed mustache.
- **[[Tari Dorr]]** -- A Bregan D'Aerthe member encountered fighting in the streets of Neverwinter during the chasm eruption, acting on Jarlaxle's standing orders to help defend the city (S02E17).
- **[[Teriador]]** -- The Rose Knight, resurrected in a drow body, stays aboard Jarlaxle's ship to learn drow culture and adapt to his new form rather than accompanying SWOJ into Neverwinter (S02E14).
- **The Carny** -- An unnamed carnival worker operating as Fel'Rekt's agent. Gives Scoot a pistol and asks SWOJ to look for Nar'l Xibrindas in the Xanathar's lair (S01E16).
- **The Milliner** -- A hat-maker aboard the Eyecatcher who crafted Lark's original hat and later creates a replacement red hat with hidden compartments and a collapsible design. Goes solely by "the Milliner" -- his craft is his entire personality (S02E14).

### Associates

- **[[Mr. E]]** (Mystery) -- A guest character played by Robert Lee. Introduced to SWOJ by Jarlaxle, Mr. E is sent by Zardos to help SWOJ infiltrate the Gralhund Villa (S01E12). A skilled rogue and lockpicker, he fights alongside SWOJ through the villa, the Vault of Dragons (S01E26), and the Cassalanter confrontation (S01E27-29). His sensei, Mr. Ziyagi, dies during the campaign. Though not a formal Bregan D'Aerthe member, he operates as Jarlaxle's associate and the two share a warm, hugging relationship.

## Jarlaxle's Disguises

Jarlaxle is a master of disguise, operating under multiple identities throughout the campaign:

### Zardos Zord
His primary cover identity -- a flamboyant human sea captain and ringmaster of the Sea Maiden's Fair. SWOJ first meets him in this guise. In S01E10, he drops the disguise by removing his hat, which causes his visage to warp into his true drow form with "silvery purple skin" and red eyes. He puts the hat back on and instantly returns to looking like Zardos. He uses this identity consistently through both seasons, including at the Blubber Buckle in Driftwood (S02E09) and when departing Neverwinter for Waterdeep (S02E31).

### J.B. Nevercott
A haberdasher persona. First appears in S01E09, delivering specialized anti-doppelganger gear to SWOJ: a fancy hat with lead foil lining (blocks Detect Thoughts) and three pistols loaded with true sight rounds that reveal doppelgangers by dispersing a pink dust. In S01E17, JB Nevercott appears in the audience of the Xanathar's Arena of Blood and Fortune. He winks at Lark and Xae from the corner of his eye, then uses the Message spell to warn SWOJ that the lair is rigged with smoke powder and reveals the location of a secret exit (south wall, flaming eye symbol) -- intelligence that saves their lives.

### Jarlaxle Baenre (True Identity)
His true drow form -- silvery purple skin, red eyes, an eye patch, and a large hat. He appears as himself at the Masked Lords' council meeting in the Season 2 finale (S02E38), where he gives a toast to SWOJ.

## Relationship with SWOJ

Bregan D'Aerthe is one of SWOJ's most consistent allies across both seasons, though the relationship is built on mutual benefit rather than pure altruism.

### Season 1: Waterdeep

- **S01E06:** SWOJ rescues [[Fel'Rekt]] -- a drow male with guns -- from cultist imprisonment inside the God Catcher, establishing first contact.
- **S01E07:** Fel'Rekt Lafeen visits Trollskull Manor at noon, reveals Bregan D'Aerthe has been tracking SWOJ, and shares intelligence about doppelgangers, devil worship among nobles, and the Wavesilver crest connection. Reveals that [[Yagra]] sent the "Y" note.
- **S01E09:** JB Nevercott delivers the lead-foil hat and true sight pistols -- anti-doppelganger gear critical for SWOJ's investigations.
- **S01E10:** SWOJ boards the Eyecatcher and meets Jarlaxle/Zardos. After Xae's disguise gambit exposes the crew as drow, Jarlaxle drops his persona and reveals his true identity and goals. He expresses interest in the Stone of Golorr.
- **S01E11:** Jarlaxle offers a counter-deal for the Stone of Golorr, explaining the Xanathar Guild threat and Nihiloor the mind flayer's role. Both Jarlaxle and Fel'Rekt attend the Short Rest grand opening. After the devastating fireball explosion, Fel'Rekt feels deeply guilty, believing he failed in his duty to protect SWOJ, and stays behind to help clean up and guard the tavern.
- **S01E12:** Jarlaxle returns to the Short Rest with Mr. E and Fel'Rekt. He sends Mr. E to help SWOJ infiltrate the Gralhund Villa, where Mr. E proves instrumental -- picking locks, fighting Zhentarim thugs, and stealing a key and a locked book. Fel'Rekt promises to defend the Short Rest while SWOJ investigates.
- **S01E16:** The carny (Fel'Rekt's agent) gives Scoot a pistol and asks SWOJ to find Nar'l Xibrindas, their embedded spy in the Xanathar's lair.
- **S01E17:** JB Nevercott, seated in the audience of the Xanathar's arena, telepathically warns SWOJ about the smoke powder trap and reveals the secret exit -- saving the party's lives.
- **S01E24:** Zardos Zord visits the Short Rest and shoots an imp disguised as a crow with his pistol, revealing that the Cassalanters use imp spies to surveil SWOJ. He provides intelligence that the Cassalanters have the third Eye of the Stone of Golorr and suggests infiltrating during the Founder's Day feast.
- **S01E26-27:** SWOJ discusses using Jarlaxle's doppelganger potions for disguises (ultimately they don't). Mr. E participates in the Vault of Dragons heist and the battle against the Xanathar at Zadio City Music Hall.
- **S01E29:** Jarlaxle emerges from the bag of holding through the twin portals with Bregan D'Aerthe drow soldiers into the Cassalanter crypt. SWOJ trades the Mask of the Lords of Waterdeep to Jarlaxle in exchange for information about the Necromancer. Jarlaxle also offers Zardos's entertainment troupe for SWOJ's victory celebration.

### Season 2: Neverwinter and Beyond

- **S02E09:** SWOJ reunites with Fel'Rekt and the Bregan D'Aerthe fleet at Driftwood. Fel'Rekt informs them about Driftwood and Kurrihan. SWOJ finds Jarlaxle (as Zardos) at the Blubber Buckle conducting business. Jarlaxle offers free passage to Devil's Rock as a detour on his way north to the Sword Coast. The organization is notably more militarized, with advanced firearms. SWOJ travels aboard the fleet to Devil's Rock.
- **S02E14:** SWOJ emerges from the bag of holding directly into Jarlaxle's cabin aboard the Eyecatcher, anchored outside Neverwinter. Jarlaxle wins a bet that the party would survive. He provides lavender tea, hospitality, equipment (including a displacer beast cloak for Scoot and a new red hat for Lark from the Milliner), and extensive political intelligence about Neverwinter: Neverember rules from the Hall of Justice, the war with Waterdeep continues, the Masks control city affairs, and Renaer leads the resistance. His ships are barred from Neverwinter's port as suspected Waterdeep spies, and he asks SWOJ to advocate for his trading interests. Fel'Rekt is already in Neverwinter gathering intelligence under disguise. SWOJ departs via the Scarlet Marpenoth submersible.
- **S02E17:** During the chasm eruption, SWOJ encounters Bregan D'Aerthe members Tari Dorr and Fel'Rekt fighting in the streets of Neverwinter. They are acting on Jarlaxle's standing orders -- as soon as the sky turned red, Jarlaxle mobilized his forces to defend the city. Jarlaxle himself moved toward the chasm. Under Zone of Truth interrogation, SWOJ is asked "Do you consort with Bregan D'Aerthe?" -- highlighting the organization's complicated reputation.
- **S02E20:** At a war council, Zardos participates alongside SWOJ, Remalia, and others. SWOJ visits Jarlaxle, who allows them to speak with [[Arcanthos]], an ancient red dragon trapped in a bag of holding. Jarlaxle mentions bags of holding are "in short supply" and that he has been looking for one himself.
- **S02E25:** In a dream sequence orchestrated by the Raven Queen, Bregan D'Aerthe appears as a rival act in a "battle of the bands" scenario in Waterdeep.
- **S02E31:** Zardos (with his signature huge purple hat) encounters SWOJ on the docks and departs for Waterdeep via Mellafarne, carrying messages to the Masked Lords, Blackstaff Tower, and the Griffin Riders requesting military reinforcements. Fel'Rekt Lafeen stays behind and joins SWOJ's crew aboard the Ethereal Galleon for the assault on the moonhole, helping operate the ballista.
- **S02E38 (Season Finale):** Jarlaxle appears at the Masked Lords' council meeting **as himself** -- eye patch and all -- alongside representatives of the Sword Coast Alliance. He gives a toast to SWOJ alongside Renaer Neverember, thanking them for protecting Waterdeep and Neverwinter. When SWOJ arrives late, Jarlaxle quips "So was I" about drinking.

## Trivia

- The podcast's name, **Twin Portals**, comes from Jarlaxle's paired bags of holding technology revealed in S01E29.
- The players frequently mispronounce "Bregan D'Aerthe" in entertaining ways, including: "Bregan Darth," "Greg and Dere," "Greg and Derek," "Droughts," "Regan d'Arth," and "Bragan Derrith."
- Fel'Rekt Lafeen's backstory as a trans man who rejected the drow matriarchy is one of the more personal character details revealed in the campaign (S01E11).
- In S02E14, Jarlaxle is described as "charming, calculating, and underdressed."
- Jarlaxle previously introduced Mr. E (Mystery) to SWOJ, establishing a connection that became a recurring guest PC role played by Robert Lee.
- Mr. E's name is a play on the word "mystery" -- when SWOJ first meets him, the players go back and forth repeating "Mr. E" before realizing the pun.
