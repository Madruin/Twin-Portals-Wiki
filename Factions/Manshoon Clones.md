# Manshoon Clones

Multiple clones of the ancient wizard Manshoon, founder of the [[Zhentarim]], operating across the multiverse with varying agendas and allegiances. The clones serve as the primary antagonist thread running through both seasons of Twin Portals, from the mysterious "Necromancer" of Season 1 to the revelation of [[Rowan Crooks]] as the 14th clone and the final undead Manshoon of Season 2.

**First appearance:** Episode [[S01E21|S01E21]] (The Necromancer), [[S02E11|S02E11]] (Shuni, Rowan reveal)
**Total appearances:** 15+ episodes across both seasons

## Overview

Manshoon was the founder of the [[Zhentarim]] (the Black Network) and a wizard of immense power. Through dark magic, he created numerous clones of himself scattered across the planes. Each clone possesses Manshoon's magical knowledge but develops its own personality, goals, and methods. The players affectionately refer to him as "Nancy" or "Manchun" (from the way the name sounds in the podcast audio).

The Manshoon clone threat is the unresolved antagonist thread from Season 1 that drives the entire Season 2 narrative -- from the Feywild hunt to the final confrontation at the [[Radiant Citadel]].

## Known Clones

### The Necromancer (Season 1 Clone)
**Episodes:** [[S01E21]], [[S01E22]], [[S01E23]], [[S01E26]], [[S01E29]]
**Status:** Defeated / fled

The first Manshoon clone encountered by SWOJ, known only as "The Necromancer" for most of Season 1. A young-looking wizard with a mechanical hand, connected to [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]'s grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund]]. He operates from an extra-dimensional sanctum accessed through the [[Kolat Towers]].

- Contacted by Ylka via scrying pool; offered to create a clone of Lark's body and send SWOJ into the afterlife to retrieve his soul.
- Performed the clone spell to resurrect [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]].
- Revealed that Ylka is a clone of [[Zilka]], created by Mordecai Gralhund.
- Had a simulacrum wearing an iron mask that fought the city watch.
- Planted a [[Gem of Seeing]] in Lark's temple during the resurrection to spy on SWOJ.
- Appeared during the theater battle at the Vault of Dragons, casting Bigby's Hand against [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]. Retreated after [[Remalia Haventree]]'s fireball blasted the balcony.
- His true name -- Manshoon -- was revealed by [[Rowan Crooks]] on the summer solstice (S01E29).

### Shuni
**Episodes:** [[S02E11]], [[S02E12]]
**Status:** Deceased (ghost recaptured in soul stone)

A Manshoon clone living inside the corpse of the [[Breathless Elder]] dragon. Surprisingly friendly and cooperative with SWOJ -- unlike most Manshoon clones, Shuni appeared to have benign intentions.

- Explained the gem/lure mechanism controlling the dragon tethers.
- Murdered by [[Rowan Crooks]] and his allies; his spirit was recaptured in a cracked soul stone.
- As a ghost, briefly advised SWOJ about soul mechanics in the Far Realm before being recaptured by Rowan Crooks.
- Warned about madness from prolonged soul-body separation and soul-eating entities.

### Man-Two (Ice Clone)
**Episodes:** [[S02E16]]
**Status:** Destroyed

A clone of Manshoon made of ice and water who accompanied SWOJ into the chasm beneath [[Neverwinter]].

- Used hypnotic pattern to neutralize guards during the infiltration.
- Destroyed when he stepped on an anti-magic rune trap, causing his magical ice-and-water body to disintegrate.

### Rowan Crooks (14th Clone)
**Episodes:** [[S01E21]], [[S01E29]], [[S01E30]], [[S02E01]], [[S02E03]], [[S02E04]], [[S02E05]], [[S02E11]], [[S02E12]], [[S02E22]], [[S02E24]], [[S02E25]], [[S02E35]], [[S02E36]]
**Status:** Defeated -- soul trapped in the Soul Stone

The 14th and most dangerous Manshoon clone, who murdered the real Rowan Crooks long ago and assumed his identity. He masqueraded as a folk hero and leader of the [[Jolly Gents]] adventuring party, building trust with SWOJ before his catastrophic betrayal.

**Season 1:**
- First encountered by Ylka in the afterlife / Malar's realm (S01E21).
- Arrived through a Feywild portal on the summer solstice. Extracted the Gem of Seeing from Lark's temple. Revealed the Necromancer's true name as Manshoon. (S01E29)

**Season 2:**
- Led the Jolly Gents alongside SWOJ in the Feywild, hunting Manshoon clones.
- Split off from SWOJ to pursue a separate lead on Manshoon.
- Captured [[Fetch]]'s soul in a crystal shard inside the Great Beast. Subjected to [[Radisare]]'s Geas spell as a safeguard. (S02E11)
- **The Betrayal (S02E12):** Revealed as the 14th Manshoon clone. Survived the catastrophic explosion that killed SWOJ, looted their corpses, captured [[Shuni]]'s spirit in a soul stone, took the barely-alive [[Little Joan]], and teleported away with all of SWOJ's possessions.
- Occupied the sixth floor of [[Zentil Keep]] as his throne room. (S02E22)
- Addressed the Jolly Gents about his plan to break [[Kelemvor]]'s [[Wall of the Faithless]] using the [[Hungering Shard]] at the Radiant Citadel to free remaining Manshoon soul fragments. (S02E24-25)
- **Final Battle (S02E35-36):** Used finger of death, cone of cold, hold person, misty step, and attempted disintegrate during the sky battle. Had legendary actions. Killed by [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]'s lightning-split axe attack. Defeated atop Zentil Keep and trapped inside the Soul Stone after his phylactery was destroyed, causing the floating fortress to collapse.
- His soul stone was later taken into custody by [[Renaer Neverember]].

**At the Radiant Citadel:** Crooks had originally arrived as a folk hero, building trust with the speakers before introducing the soul pylons. He then disappeared, and Manshoon later took over and weaponized the infrastructure Crooks had built.

### The Final Manshoon (Undead Clone)
**Episodes:** [[S02E36]]
**Status:** Defeated -- soul sent to the Raven Queen

The last surviving clone of Manshoon, described as undead, wearing a half-mask over his face and possessing a metal arm. He had been channeling Rowan Crooks' soul pylon to ascend to godhood.

- Teleported around the battlefield, casting synaptic static and exhaustion effects.
- Desperately searched for Rowan Crooks' soul stone.
- Ultimately defeated when [[Kelemvor]] appeared and his soul was sent to the [[Raven Queen]].

## Key Items

- **[[Gem of Seeing]]** -- Implanted in Lark's temple by the Necromancer during Lark's resurrection to spy on SWOJ. Extracted by Rowan Crooks on the summer solstice.
- **[[Compass]]** -- Crafted from Lark's gemstone and blood, points toward the nearest Manshoon clone.
- **[[Soul Stone]]** -- Contains captured souls; used by Rowan Crooks to trap Shuni's ghost and later used to trap Rowan Crooks himself.
- **[[Hungering Shard]]** -- Rowan Crooks' tool for breaking the Wall of the Faithless at the Radiant Citadel.
- **[[Necromancer's Ledger]]** -- Found in the Necromancer's sanctum, bookmarked with notable names including [[Grim Wavesilver]].
