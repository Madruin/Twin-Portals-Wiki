# House Wavesilver

A noble house of [[Waterdeep]] and the family of [[Malark Wavesilver|Malark "Lark" Wavesilver]], the party's rogue. Once a proud shipping dynasty, House Wavesilver fell into severe financial ruin before being restored through SWOJ's efforts. The family harbors a secret: Lark's mother [[Isvel Wavesilver]] is the infamous masked vigilante known as [[The Black Viper]].

**First appearance:** Episode [[S01E04|S01E04]]
**Total appearances:** 6+ episodes

## Overview

House Wavesilver is a Waterdhavian noble family whose fortunes are tied to their shipping business. By the time SWOJ's story begins, the family is deeply in debt -- their shipping enterprise is "almost completely underwater." They owe a crushing loan to [[House Cassalanter]] at 500% interest per tenday, and their patriarch [[Grim Wavesilver]] is cold, disappointed, and disapproving of his son Lark's adventuring lifestyle.

The family's connection to darker forces is hinted at early on: a cultist inside the [[God Catcher|Godcatcher]] was found wearing Wavesilver insignia, and the [[Necromancer's Ledger]] found in [[The Necromancer]]'s sanctum was bookmarked at [[Grim Wavesilver]]'s name -- suggesting the family was a target for blackmail or manipulation by Waterdeep's criminal underworld.

By the end of Season 1, the Wavesilver shipping business has recovered and prospers. The family commissions a new ship, the [[Silver Plum]], named in honor of SWOJ's efforts.

## The Black Viper

[[Isvel Wavesilver]] leads a double life as [[The Black Viper]], a masked vigilante who operates on Waterdeep's rooftops at night. She first appears anonymously in S01E04, helping SWOJ fight a grell in the sewers and leaving a black onyx gemstone rose for [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]. Her identity as Lark's mother is revealed later, and she is shown to have trained Lark in vigilantism during midnight rooftop sessions.

In S01E27, the Black Viper breaks into a high-security anti-magic prison to rescue SWOJ after about a week of imprisonment, demonstrating her skills as a master thief. She then accompanies them to the [[Cassalanters|Cassalanter]] Founder's Day feast, entering with Lark's father.

## Financial Troubles and Recovery

The Wavesilver shipping business was in crisis through much of Season 1:
- **[[S01E04]]:** A letter from [[House Cassalanter]] demands repayment of a loan at 500% interest.
- **[[S01E06]]:** A cultist inside the Godcatcher is found wearing Wavesilver insignia, suggesting entanglement with dark forces.
- **[[S01E08]]:** Lark receives an invitation to the Cassalanter soiree through House Wavesilver.
- **[[S01E09]]:** Lark's parents arrive at the Cassalanter party. Grim is cold and disapproving. The shipping business is "almost completely underwater." Grim charges Lark 500% interest per tenday.
- **[[S01E23]]:** The Necromancer's private study contains a ledger bookmarked with Grim Wavesilver's name, alongside other notable Waterdhavians.
- **[[S01E27]]:** Loan documents to House Wavesilver's shipping business are found in [[Victoro Cassalanter]]'s locked drawer, confirming the Cassalanters' financial stranglehold.
- **[[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]:** The Wavesilver shipping business is now prospering. Lark reconnects with his family and trains in vigilantism with Isvel. A new ship, the [[Silver Plum]], is commissioned in honor of SWOJ.

## The Wavesilver Household

The family home is described in the S01E29 downtime: Lark's parents were waiting at [[Trollskull Manor]] with a candle burned low. The shipping business had recovered. In S02E38, [[Grim Wavesilver]] and [[Aspell Wavesilver]] are again found waiting at Trollskull Manor for SWOJ's return.

## Lark's Legacy

[[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] was disintegrated by the [[Xanathar]]'s beholder eye ray during the Season 1 live episode, then resurrected through [[The Necromancer]]'s clone spell. He was later killed again when [[Rowan Crooks]] (the 14th Manshoon clone) betrayed SWOJ. His iconic hat became a vessel for his soul -- the hat communicates telepathically, as Lark's body did not make the return trip. An urn containing his remains is kept on the mantle of the [[Short Rest]] tavern.
