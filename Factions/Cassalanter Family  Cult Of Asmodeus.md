# Cassalanter Family / Cult Of Asmodeus

One of the most powerful noble families in Waterdeep and the primary villain faction of Season 1's final arc. Beneath their veneer of wealth and aristocratic respectability, [[Victoro Cassalanter]] and [[Amalia Cassalanter]] harbored a devastating secret: they had made a pact with [[Asmodeus]], Lord of Nessus, trading their children's souls for wealth and power. Their eldest son [[Osvaldo Cassalanter|Osvaldo]] had already been transformed into a chain devil as a consequence of the bargain, and the pact demanded the souls of their remaining children -- [[Terenzio Cassalanter|Terenzio]] and [[Elzerina Cassalanter|Elzerina]] -- unless a massive ransom of gold was paid to the archdevil. The family operated a hidden shrine to Asmodeus beneath [[Cassalanter Villa]], maintained a network of doppelganger agents and imp spies, bought off city watch officers and magistrates, and orchestrated an alliance with [[House Gralhund]] and the broader [[Cult Of Asmodeus]] to pursue their goals. Their conspiracy was ultimately exposed and dismantled by [[Silver Waves Of Justice (SWOJ)|SWOJ]], though Amalia resurfaced in Season 2 as the leader of a [[Cult Of The Dragon]] cell under the alias "Velia Shadowmantle."

**First appearance:** [[S01E04 - Episode Four|S01E04]] (House Cassalanter letter demanding 500% interest)
**Total appearances:** 15+ episodes across Seasons 1 and 2
**Status:** Defeated -- Victoro and Amalia sentenced to life in prison ([[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]); Amalia later escaped and resurfaced as a Cult of the Dragon leader

## Overview

### The Pact with Asmodeus

The Cassalanters were among Waterdeep's wealthiest and most politically connected noble families. At some point before the events of the campaign, Victoro and Amalia entered into a diabolical contract with Asmodeus, the Lord of Nessus, exchanging their children's souls for wealth and power. The consequences were immediate and horrifying: their eldest son Osvaldo was transformed into a chain devil -- a nine-foot-tall hulking humanoid wrapped in barbed, animated chains that bled black ichor and emanated an aura that dampened healing magic. The Cassalanters kept Osvaldo imprisoned in a pentagram circle in the attic of their villa, telling the outside world he had died.

The pact further demanded the souls of their remaining children, Terenzio and Elzerina, unless the family could deliver a massive quantity of gold to Asmodeus. This desperation drove the Cassalanters to seek [[Lord Dagult Neverember|Dagult Neverember]]'s hidden hoard of gold -- the same treasure sought by every major faction in Waterdeep -- and to hire SWOJ as unwitting pawns in their scheme.

### Wealth, Power, and Corruption

The Cassalanters wielded enormous political and financial influence across Waterdeep. Their operations included:

- **Financial predation:** Sending letters to lesser noble houses (including [[House Wavesilver]]) demanding repayment of loans at usurious 500% interest rates
- **City watch corruption:** Buying off magistrates assigned to cases involving their enemies, placing agents on the city watch payroll, and ensorcelling guards like [[Bricks]] and [[Bracks]] to betray the [[Harpers]]
- **Espionage network:** Deploying imp spies disguised as crows for surveillance, and using their doppelganger majordomo [[Willifort Crowell]] to infiltrate institutions and impersonate officials
- **Alliance with House Gralhund:** Working with [[Yalah Gralhund]] and [[House Gralhund]] through the Cult of Asmodeus. A threatening letter from Victoro (signed "V") found in [[Orond Gralhund]]'s study confirmed their alliance and referenced "the Lord of Nessus"
- **Diamond mine operations:** Controlling diamond mines whose disputed ownership eventually contributed to tensions between Waterdeep and Neverwinter

### The Shrine Beneath the Villa

Beneath [[Cassalanter Villa]] lay a massive underground shrine to Asmodeus: a church-like space with pews, a red carpet, wrought iron candelabras, a 10-foot diameter stone bowl of fire, and a towering 30-foot statue of the archdevil. Bloody cells lined the walls, and a natural cave opening led deeper underground. The third Eye of the [[The Stone of Golorr]] was kept on a pedestal within the fire bowl, guarded by three bearded devils in crimson robes. A deeper temple contained an altar bowl of fire filled with golden coins -- the site where Amalia ultimately pleaded with Asmodeus and where [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] signed the devil contract.

## Known Members

### The Cassalanter Family
- [[Victoro Cassalanter]] -- Noble patriarch. Gambled with [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] at Tymora's Fancy (losing 160gp). Wielded a Ruby-Tipped Staff that could cause the ground to shake and trap enemies. His locked office contained loan documents and a gold Asmodeus cloven hoof icon in a secret compartment. Authored the threatening letter to the Gralhunds signed "V." Controlled the city watch army that arrested SWOJ after the theater battle ([[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]]). At the Founder's Day feast, used a [[Charming Cane]] to mesmerize the crowd and a [[Rod of Rulership]] to compel obedience. Cast mirror image during combat. Fought SWOJ but was knocked unconscious by [[Mr. E]], [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]], and Scoot, who kicked him through his own portal into the temple of Asmodeus. Stripped of his [[Ring of Protection]] and Rod of Rulership. Sentenced to life in prison ([[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]).
- [[Amalia Cassalanter]] -- Noble matriarch. Tall, Morticia Addams-like appearance. A powerful spellcaster capable of casting charm person, haste, fireball (10d6 damage), cone of cold, and telekinesis. Hired SWOJ to find Dagult Neverember's gold for 150gp/day. Confronted SWOJ in the underground shrine when they infiltrated the villa ([[S01E24 - Episode Twenty Four|S01E24]]). Found pleading with Asmodeus to spare her children in the temple ([[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]]). Sentenced to life in prison but later escaped, resurfacing as "[[Velia Shadowmantle]]" -- leader of a [[Cult Of The Dragon]] cell at [[Castle Naerytar]]. Oversaw dracolich creation rituals. Her disguise was burned away by moonbeam ([[S02E29 - Episode Fifty Nine LIVE (S02E29)|S02E29]]). Fled east atop a partially-transformed dracolich. Later allied with [[Manshoon]] at the [[Radiant Citadel]], where [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] polymorphed into a T-Rex and attacked her ([[S02E34 - Episode Sixty Four LIVE (S02E34)|S02E34]]).
- [[Osvaldo Cassalanter]] -- Eldest son, transformed into a chain devil by the Asmodeus pact. Nine-foot-tall hulking humanoid with animated barbed chains, bloodshot bulging eyes, blue veins, and black ichor for blood. Imprisoned in a pentagram circle in the villa attic. His howling could be heard throughout the estate. SWOJ discovered him during the soiree ([[S01E09 - Episode Nine|S01E09]]) and fought him alongside [[Zylka]] before his parents arrived to restore the pentagram.
- [[Terenzio Cassalanter]] -- The older, more solemn son. Led Lark to Victoro's locked office and recognized the Asmodeus hoof icon from nightmares. Mentioned dreaming of his mother descending into a secret passage in his bedroom. His soul was saved by Xae's contract with Asmodeus. Placed under city protection after his parents' sentencing and joined the [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars]].
- [[Elzerina Cassalanter]] -- The younger, bossy daughter. Enthusiastically let SWOJ into the mansion to play "ghosts" (a hide-and-seek variant). Her soul was saved by Xae's contract. Placed under city protection and befriended the [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars]] at [[Trollskull Manor]].

### Agents and Servants
- [[Willifort Crowell]] -- Tiefling majordomo of Cassalanter Villa. Wore thin-rimmed glasses and a black suit -- matching the description of the tiefling who gave [[Blossom Zuber]] the devil ring that summoned scarecrows. Received the [[The Stone of Golorr]] (referred to as the "Stone of Galore") from a spined devil atop a building and fled in a wagon down the High Road during the Feast of the Moon parade, accompanied by a bearded devil ([[S01E13 - Episode Thirteen|S01E13]]). Later revealed to be a doppelganger who murdered [[Captain Hyustus Stagget]] and impersonated him in the prison. Exposed during SWOJ's trial when he could not read the Gralhund note, attempted to flee through a window, was caught by a grell and Scoot, and taken into custody ([[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]).
- [[Phelerek]] -- A guard at Cassalanter Villa who befriended Xae in her golden retriever wild shape form.
- A tiefling nanny -- An older tiefling woman who served as nanny to the Cassalanter children, scolding them for playing instead of studying.
- [[Bricks]] and [[Bracks]] -- [[Harpers]] agents and guards of [[Remalia Haventree]], ensorcelled by the Cassalanters into betraying Remalia to the city watch after the theater battle ([[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]]). Later exposed as traitors and went on the run.
- Corrupt city watch officers -- Multiple members of the [[City Watch]] were found on the Cassalanter payroll after their ledgers were discovered. [[Ulkoria Stonemarrow]] led the trusted contingent and promised to sweep the force for remaining Cassalanter agents.

### Cult Forces
- [[Zylka]] -- Ylka's doppelganger, deployed as an agent of [[House Gralhund]] at the Cassalanter party. Crashed through the ballroom windows with cultists wearing golden grinning masks and red leather armor ([[S01E09 - Episode Nine|S01E09]]). Wielded a magical rapier with an infinity symbol. Later found dying in the Gralhund Crypt, where she drew the symbol of Asmodeus before expiring ([[S01E13 - Episode Thirteen|S01E13]]). A letter from Victoro confirmed Zylka was "intended to cause harmless chaos, not lead adventurers to our attic."
- Golden-masked cultists -- 8-10 cultists plus Zylka and 3 other figures who attacked the Cassalanter soiree. Wore golden grinning masks and red leather robes. Painted blood patterns on walls and performed occult rituals.
- Bearded devils -- Three bearded devils in crimson robes guarding the Asmodeus shrine. Dark purple skin, greenish spiky tentacle beards, dog-like legs, glaives. Their attacks inflicted infernal fire and poison that prevented magical healing.
- Spined devils and imps -- Summoned at the Founder's Day feast by Victoro. Fought by SWOJ and destroyed by dragon reinforcements ([[Melifarn]] and [[Aurinax]]).
- Imp spies -- Deployed as surveillance agents, disguised as crows. One was shot by [[Zardoz Zord]] outside the Short Rest, revealing the Cassalanters' espionage operations ([[S01E24 - Episode Twenty Four|S01E24]]).

## Connections to the Broader Plot

### The Stone of Golorr
The Cassalanters were one of several factions competing for the [[The Stone of Golorr]] -- the sentient artifact key to locating [[Lord Dagult Neverember|Dagult Neverember]]'s hidden gold hoard beneath Waterdeep. They possessed the third and final Eye of the Stone, kept in the fire bowl of their Asmodeus shrine. Their majordomo [[Willifort Crowell]] received the Stone itself from a spined devil during the Feast of the Moon parade. The Cassalanters needed Neverember's gold to pay their debt to Asmodeus and save their children's souls.

### The Gralhund Alliance
The Cassalanters maintained a secret alliance with [[House Gralhund]] through the [[Cult Of Asmodeus]]. [[Yalah Gralhund]] worked with the cult and orchestrated the nimblewright's escape with the Stone of Golorr. The Cassalanters deployed [[Zylka]] -- Ylka's doppelganger and a Gralhund agent -- at their own soiree, though this plan backfired spectacularly. Victoro's letter to the Gralhunds expressed fury at their incompetence.

### Xae's Contract and the Apprentice Quest
[[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]'s contract with [[Asmodeus]] -- signed to save the Cassalanter children -- became one of the most consequential events of the entire campaign. The contract required freeing six "brothers and sisters" (Jhesiyra, Rantantar, Mayrin, Trobriand, Muiral, and Arcturia -- [[Halaster Blackcloak]]'s apprentices) within 13 Founder's Days, or Xae would forfeit her own soul. This set up the quest that drove much of the subsequent campaign through [[Undermountain]] and beyond.

### Amalia's Second Act
Even from prison, the Cassalanter legacy proved impossible to contain. Amalia escaped and reinvented herself as "Velia Shadowmantle," leading a [[Cult Of The Dragon]] cell that performed dracolich creation rituals, destroyed the town of [[Amphail]], and eventually allied with [[Manshoon]] to seize the [[Radiant Citadel]]. The Cassalanter diamond mines also contributed to a war between Waterdeep and Neverwinter during the three-year time gap between seasons.
