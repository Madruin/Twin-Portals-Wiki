# The Jolly Gents

> *"We're all prisoners of Crooks."*
> -- [[Antediluvian Jake]], whispered to [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] during the ambush at the subterranean lake (S02E16)

**First appearance:** [[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]
**Active episodes:** S01E29 through S02E38
**Base of operations:** [[Zhentil Keep]] (floating fortress above the [[Shadowfell]])
**Status:** Disbanded -- remnants reformed as "The Ripples" under [[Little Joan]]

---

## Overview

The Jolly Gents were a legendary adventuring company whose public reputation as folk heroes concealed a horrifying truth: their charismatic leader, [[Rowan Crooks]], had been murdered and replaced by the 14th clone of [[Manshoon]], the founder of the [[Zhentarim]]. Under this imposter's command, the organization was transformed from a band of heroic adventurers into a soul-harvesting operation serving Manshoon's ambitions.

### Public Reputation

To the people of the Sword Coast, the Jolly Gents were cultural icons. They had their own trading cards, ballads were written about their exploits, and their leader Rowan Crooks was celebrated as a legendary hero who had fought Manshoon on multiple occasions. [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] frequently invoked songs about Crooks' defeats of Manshoon for her Vicious Mockery spells. They arrived at [[Trollskull Manor]] through a Feywild portal on the summer solstice at the end of Season 1, presenting themselves as allies in the hunt against Manshoon -- and [[SWOJ]] had every reason to trust them.

### The Hidden Truth

In reality, the Jolly Gents had been subverted from within. [[Friar Truck]], a demonic cleric serving the "Lord of Nexus," was the original manipulator who orchestrated the real Rowan Crooks' downfall and replacement by a Manshoon clone. The 14th Manshoon clone murdered the genuine Rowan Crooks long ago and assumed his identity, turning the Jolly Gents into instruments of his will. Many members were not complicit -- their souls were harvested and bound into phylacteries, with over a hundred such containers found in Friar Truck's laboratory beneath [[Zhentil Keep]]. Some members, like [[Antediluvian Jake]], were coerced prisoners rather than willing servants.

The organization's ultimate purpose under Manshoon was to gather power and resources for a plan to reach the [[Radiant Citadel]] and use the [[Hungering Shard]] to break [[Kelemvor]]'s [[Kelemvor's Wall Of The Faithless|Wall of the Faithless]], freeing the remaining fragments of Manshoon's soul trapped on the other side.

## Known Members

### Leadership

- **[[Rowan Crooks]]** -- Ostensible leader. The real Rowan Crooks was a mustachioed folk hero who fought against Manshoon. He was murdered and replaced by the 14th Manshoon clone, who assumed his identity and corrupted the organization. The imposter was ultimately killed by [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]]'s lightning-split axe atop Zentil Keep (S02E24) and his soul was trapped in the [[The Stone of Golorr|Soul Stone]] (S02E25). See: [[#The Revelation: Rowan Crooks Is the 14th Manshoon Clone]].
- **[[Friar Truck]]** -- Demonic cleric and the hidden architect of the organization's corruption. Served the "Lord of Nexus" and orchestrated the real Crooks' replacement by a Manshoon clone. Maintained a phylactery laboratory containing over a hundred bound Jolly Gents souls. Wielded a mace called "the Truck" and cast spirit guardians, banishment, flame strike, and wall of fire. Immune to fear effects. Killed in the Breathless Elder explosion (S02E11), then encountered again as a guardian of the phylactery lab and killed by [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]]'s moonbeam (S02E23).

### Core Members

- **[[Little Joan]]** -- A physically imposing warrior despite her name. Wielded a dual-bladed axe. Initially participated in the betrayal at the [[The Breathless Elder|Breathless Elder]] (S02E11), barely surviving the explosion. Later developed growing doubts about Crooks' leadership. When SWOJ infiltrated Zentil Keep, Scoot persuaded her to switch sides through a show of strength. She rallied approximately 20-30 Gents against Crooks and became the leader of the allied contingent known as "[[Jolly Gents (The Ripples)|The Ripples]]," continuing to accompany SWOJ through the remainder of Season 2.
- **[[Antediluvian Jake]]** -- The scholarly lore keeper of the Jolly Gents. Carried an obsidian tablet containing historical records and lore about the aboleth-dragon conflict, the Breathless Elder, and Manshoon's operations. Though he participated in the ambush at the subterranean lake (S02E16), he secretly passed his tablet to Lark and whispered "we're all prisoners of Crooks," providing the intelligence that revealed Zentil Keep's location and the soul-harvesting operation. His phylactery (a copper orb) was found in Crooks' office. At the Season 2 finale, Jake performed Mordecai Gralhund's soul-binding ritual to end his undead state, eulogizing the original Jolly Gents (S02E38).
- **[[Sixteen String Jack]]** -- A bard who fought with a lute. Stole [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]'s concertina at some point before the events of the story. During the lake ambush (S02E16), Ylka shattered his stolen loot and recovered her instrument. Jack teleported away with Jake and the green power stones. His phylactery emitted a twinging musical sound when disturbed in Friar Truck's laboratory.
- **[[Giggling Gus]]** -- A top-ranking member known for his distinctive giggling laugh. Appeared in the Raven Queen's dream sequence alongside Antediluvian Jake during a vision of the Jolly Gents' legendary assault on the [[Xanathar's Lair|Xanathar's lair]] (S02E25).

### Other Named Members

- **[[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]]** -- A whip-wielding lieutenant. Traveled with SWOJ early in Season 2, was captured and replaced by a podling inside the [[The Great Beast of Erua|Great Beast of Erua]] (S02E03), later rescued. At Zentil Keep, she provided intelligence about the fortress layout, challenged Lark to a staff sparring match on the [[Bridge Of Destiny]], and sided with SWOJ during the civil war (S02E22-S02E24). At the Season 2 finale, she participated in the soul-binding ritual with Jake (S02E38).
- **[[Pill Garlic]]** -- A member stationed at Zentil Keep, overheard during a wyvern patrol in the underground caverns with Slim Curly (S02E23).
- **[[Slim Curly]]** -- A halfling spy master and undead operative. Spotted SWOJ during a patrol beneath Zentil Keep and was chased and killed by the party. His body was fed to mushrooms to dispose of evidence (S02E23).
- **[[Quick Quill Quincy]]** -- Known for record-keeping and clerical duties within the organization. Mentioned during conversations in the Bailey of Zentil Keep (S02E22).
- **[[Rambling Bill]]** -- A talkative and somewhat gullible sentry stationed on the chain entrance to Zentil Keep. Accepted SWOJ's cover story about being new recruits (S02E22).
- **[[Long Mary]]** -- A tall, stern sentry partnered with Rambling Bill on chain guard duty. More suspicious than Bill but ultimately let SWOJ pass (S02E22).
- **[[Dorvina]]** -- An associate present during the return to Waterdeep from the Shadowfell (S02E05).

### Allied Non-Members

- **[[Lyrian]]** -- A unicorn ally of [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]], connected to the Feywild. Guided the Jolly Gents to Trollskull through a Feywild portal and traveled with the group in the Feywild.
- **[[Radecere]]** -- A rock gnome inventor allied with the Jolly Gents. Creator of the [[Dynamo of Possibility]]. Cast [[Geas]] on Rowan Crooks to safeguard [[Fetch]]'s safety (S02E05).

## The Revelation: Rowan Crooks Is the 14th Manshoon Clone

The central twist of the Jolly Gents storyline was the revelation that their beloved leader was the enemy they had been hunting all along.

### Setup (S01E29 - S02E05)

Rowan Crooks arrived at [[Trollskull Manor]] on the summer solstice through a Feywild portal, accompanied by the Jolly Gents. He extracted a Gem of Seeing from [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s temple -- a device the Necromancer had planted to spy on SWOJ -- and dramatically revealed the Necromancer's true identity as [[Manshoon]]. This act of apparent heroism cemented the alliance. SWOJ stepped through twin portals into the Feywild, and Crooks split off with part of his crew while leaving Jake, Ann Jane, and Lyrian with the party.

Early warning signs appeared: Crooks captured [[Fetch]]'s soul in a crystal shard over SWOJ's objections (S02E05), and Jake and Ann Jane were captured and replaced by podlings inside the Great Beast (S02E03), suggesting Manshoon's forces were targeting the group from within.

### The Betrayal (S02E11)

Inside the [[The Breathless Elder|Breathless Elder]] in the Far Realm, after SWOJ met the cooperative Manshoon clone [[Shoonie|Shuni]], Rowan Crooks arrived with Little Joan and Friar Truck and revealed his true nature. They murdered Shuni and attacked SWOJ. Lark severed the Elder's power gem in a desperate move, triggering a catastrophic explosion that killed SWOJ and Friar Truck. Little Joan barely survived. Crooks -- apparently unaffected -- looted SWOJ's corpses, recaptured Shuni's spirit in a soul stone, and teleported away with everything (S02E11-S02E12).

### The Intelligence (S02E16 - S02E20)

During the ambush at the subterranean lake beneath the Neverwinter chasm, [[Antediluvian Jake]] secretly passed his obsidian tablet to Lark and whispered "we're all prisoners of Crooks" (S02E16). When Lark later deciphered the tablet, it revealed the full scope of the conspiracy: the original Jolly Gents were killed by Manshoon, their souls were being harvested by Rowan Crooks, and the secret base was [[Zhentil Keep]] -- a floating fortress above the Shadowfell (S02E20).

### The Infiltration and Fall (S02E22 - S02E25)

Armed with this intelligence, SWOJ infiltrated Zentil Keep disguised in Jolly Gents cloaks and bronze rings. Inside, they discovered:

- **Crooks' office** on the sixth floor, containing Antediluvian Jake's phylactery (a copper orb) and operational documents
- **Friar Truck's phylactery laboratory** containing over a hundred phylacteries with bound Jolly Gents souls
- **Clone tanks** with Rowan Crooks clones in various stages of development
- **Friar Truck's notes** revealing he orchestrated the real Crooks' downfall and replacement
- **A print shop** staffed by ghost archivists forced to produce propaganda, and the real Rowan Crooks' original journal detailing his heroic ideals

Scoot persuaded Little Joan to defect. SWOJ confronted Crooks publicly, triggering a civil war among the Gents. Lark destabilized Zentil Keep by jamming his extendable rod into the soul engine. In the sky-falling battle that followed, Crooks deployed finger of death, cone of cold, hold person, and legendary actions, but Scoot delivered the killing blow with a lightning-split axe strike. Crooks' phylactery was destroyed and his soul was trapped in the Soul Stone, causing the fortress to collapse (S02E24-S02E25).

## Organization and Infrastructure

### Zentil Keep

The Jolly Gents' base of operations was [[Zhentil Keep]], a floating fortress above the [[Shadowfell]]. The ruined keep sat atop an island held aloft by three soul obelisks connected by massive chains. It contained:

- **The Bailey** -- Main courtyard where dozens of Gents assembled. Featured the [[Bridge Of Destiny]], a staff-fighting platform important to Jolly Gents tradition.
- **The Fifth Floor** -- Trapped corridors filled with shadow thread tripwires that triggered alarms.
- **Crooks' Office (Sixth Floor)** -- Contained operational documents, Antediluvian Jake's phylactery, and the throne room/vault where SWOJ's stolen gear was kept.
- **Underground Caverns** -- Bioluminescent mushroom caves with a subterranean lake, patrolled by wyvern riders.
- **The Soul Engine Room** -- Massive chamber where magical chains fed soul energy into a central sphere keeping the island afloat.
- **Friar Truck's Phylactery Laboratory** -- Hidden beneath the keep. Contained over a hundred phylacteries binding Jolly Gents souls, clone tanks growing Rowan Crooks clones, and Truck's blood magic spellbook.
- **The Print Shop** -- Staffed by ghost archivists forced to produce propaganda. Also housed the real Rowan Crooks' original journal.

### Identifying Marks

Jolly Gents wore distinctive **cloaks and bronze ring insignias**. They also used **camouflage clothing** for field operations (found in the Neverwinter chasm). A **mithril Jolly Gents brooch** was discovered in the fifth floor hallways of Zentil Keep.

### Wyvern Patrols

Shadow-native wyverns served as aerial mounts and patrol animals around Zentil Keep. Members like [[Pill Garlic]] and [[Slim Curly]] rode them through the underground caverns.

## Legacy

The Jolly Gents' story is one of the campaign's great tragedies. They were genuinely legendary adventurers whose leader was murdered and replaced by an evil clone, turning their heroic reputation into a weapon against the very people they were supposed to protect. The revelation that many members were prisoners rather than accomplices -- their souls bound in phylacteries, their wills subverted -- added a layer of pathos to what could have been a simple villain faction.

After Crooks' defeat, the surviving loyalists who turned against him reformed under [[Little Joan]] as "**The Ripples**," fighting alongside SWOJ through the Castle Naerytar and Radiant Citadel arcs. At the Season 2 finale, the original Jolly Gents were honored at the God's Night ceremony, where [[Antediluvian Jake]] eulogized his fallen comrades and chose to end his undead existence through a soul-binding ritual. [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] burned his Jolly Gents outfit in their memory.

The real Rowan Crooks' legacy lived on: at the [[Radiant Citadel]], it was revealed that the genuine Crooks had once arrived there as a folk hero and built the soul pylon infrastructure before Manshoon took over and weaponized it. His soul stone became a key item that Manshoon desperately sought during the endgame (S02E35-S02E36).
