# Gralhund Family

The Gralhund Family is a noble house of [[Waterdeep]] whose tangled web of alliances, betrayals, and dark magic made them one of the most consequential factions in the Twin Portals campaign. Outwardly a respectable noble house -- their guards wearing **purple and silver livery** -- the Gralhunds were secretly bankrolling the [[Zhentarim]], allied with the [[Cult of Asmodeus]] through the [[Cassalanter Family]], and conducting arcane experiments involving clones, golems, and soul-binding rituals. The family's legacy is most deeply felt through [[Ylka Gralhund]], the bard of [[Silver Waves Of Justice|SWOJ]], who discovered over the course of the campaign that she was not truly a Gralhund by birth but a **clone** created by her grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund]] to protect the soul of the original [[Zylka]].

**First appearance:** [[S01E05 - Episode Five]] (Mordecai's initials on the Song Sword) / [[S01E09 - Episode Nine]] (House Gralhund announced at party)
**Total appearances:** 14+ episodes across both seasons
**Also known as:** House Gralhund, the Gralhunds

---

## Overview

The Gralhund Family occupied a prominent position among the Waterdavian nobility, with their estate -- [[Gralhund Villa]] -- located at the end of Del Zorn Street, very close to [[Trollskull Alley]]. The villa was a two-story mansion enclosed by twenty-foot walls, containing a carriage house, servants' quarters, a great hall, library, study, master bedroom, guest room with a **teleportation circle**, and a second-floor balcony.

The family operated on multiple levels of intrigue simultaneously:

- **Noble patrons of the Zhentarim** -- The Gralhunds bankrolled the Black Network for months before Lady [[Yalah Gralhund]] double-crossed them and seized the [[Stone of Golorr]].
- **Allies of the Cassalanters and the Cult of Asmodeus** -- A threatening letter from [[Victoro Cassalanter]] found in Lord [[Orond Gralhund]]'s study confirmed a formal alliance between the two houses under the banner of Asmodeus, Lord of the Nine Hells.
- **Practitioners of forbidden magic** -- Patriarch [[Mordecai Gralhund]] was a wizard who invented a soul-binding ritual, created clones, and built the golem [[Nim]], blurring the line between arcane research and necromancy.

The coincidental naming of the family was itself a memorable moment in the podcast's history: player Beth Broffey independently chose "Gralhund" as Ylka's surname before discovering it was already written into the *Waterdeep: Dragon Heist* adventure module, leading to one of the campaign's richest and most personal storylines.

## History in the Campaign

### The Song Sword and Mordecai's Legacy (S01E05)

The Gralhund name first surfaces when SWOJ explores the cellar of [[Trollskull Manor]] and discovers a hidden workshop belonging to the ghost [[Lif]]. Inside a chest, [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] finds a **blue steel short sword** with a gemstone pommel and a brass plaque inscribed with the initials "M.G." -- standing for **Mordecai Gralhund**, Ylka's grandfather. The blade is cold to the touch, deals cold damage, and plays a haunting melody when swung -- a tune Ylka recognizes as one her grandfather used to sing. This weapon becomes Ylka's signature throughout the campaign, later known as the Song Sword. A diary found alongside it reveals that Mordecai helped Lif find work at Trollskull Manor, connecting the Gralhund family to the tavern's history.

### House Gralhund at the Cassalanter Party (S01E09)

During the lavish soiree at [[Cassalanter Villa]], the tiefling majordomo [[Willifort Crowell]] announces the arrival of "House Gralhund" among the noble guests. This is the first public acknowledgment of the family's standing among Waterdeep's elite. The same evening, [[Zylka|Zilka]] -- a figure who looks exactly like Ylka -- crashes through the ballroom windows alongside cultists wearing golden grinning masks and red leather armor. Zilka wields a magical rapier with an **infinity symbol** on the hilt and fights [[Osvaldo Cassalanter]] alongside SWOJ before being knocked unconscious and captured. Her two rings -- a silver Ring of Disguise Self and a brass ring of unknown function -- are confiscated by SWOJ.

The true connection between Zilka and the Gralhund family will not be revealed for many episodes, but the seeds of the mystery are planted here.

### The Cult of Asmodeus Connection (S01E10)

As SWOJ investigates the aftermath of the Cassalanter party, they learn that the [[Cult of Asmodeus]] holds meetings in the **Gralhund crypts** at the [[Fields of Glory]]. [[Yalah Gralhund]] confronts [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] and publicly **denounces her as a traitor** to the family -- the first indication that Ylka's relationship with House Gralhund is far more complicated and hostile than it appears. Meanwhile, Zilka escapes from city watch custody, further deepening the mystery of the Gralhund doppelganger.

### The Battle of Gralhund Villa (S01E12)

The Gralhund Villa becomes the site of one of Season 1's most pivotal episodes when the [[Zhentarim]] -- whom the Gralhunds had been secretly bankrolling -- turn on the family.

After the devastating **fireball explosion** at Trollskull Alley kills the gnome spy [[Dalekar|Dalcar]], the [[Trollskull Alley Irregulars]] track a burned Zhentarim agent south to the villa, where [[Hrabbaz]], the Gralhunds' half-orc bodyguard, takes him inside. [[Squidly]] is wounded by Hrabbaz during the pursuit.

With the help of **[[Mr. E]]**, a drow rogue sent by [[Jarlaxle|Jarlaxle/Zardos]], SWOJ infiltrates the villa through the servants' entrance and discovers:

- **Dead servants in the pantry** -- the Zhentarim had been murdering Gralhund household staff
- A pitched battle between Gralhund guards in purple and silver livery and Zhentarim agents
- **Nine Gralhund servants** rescued and sent to the Short Rest for safety
- A high-ceilinged, wood-paneled **[[Gralhund Library]]** containing paintings of the Gralhund lineage (some faces deliberately scratched out), a locked book on a lectern guarded by an imp disguised as a tarantula, and sliding ladders
- A **threatening letter** from [[Victoro Cassalanter]] ('V') in Lord [[Orond Gralhund]]'s study, reading:

> *"You fools. Your agent was intended to cause harmless chaos, not lead adventurers to our attic before getting arrested. We have arranged for her release, but this inconvenience will not be forgotten. The Lord of Nessus despises incompetence. V."*

This letter confirmed three critical facts: the Gralhund-Cassalanter alliance under the [[Cult of Asmodeus]], that [[Zylka|Zilka]] was a **Gralhund agent** sent to the Cassalanter party, and that the Cassalanters had arranged Zilka's release from city watch custody.

In the villa's upper floors, the burned Zhentarim leader corners Lord [[Orond Gralhund]] in a guest room and **slashes his throat**. Ylka stabilizes Orond with medicine, but notably, Lady [[Yalah Gralhund]] appears **disappointed that he survived**. [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] battles [[Hrabbaz]] in the master bedroom while [[Mr. E]] steals a **silver key** from Yalah's neck and escapes through a window with the locked book from the library as the city watch floods the villa.

Charmed by Ylka, the Zhentarim leader reveals the **chain of possession** of the [[Stone of Golorr]]:

> **[[Xanathar Guild]]** -> stolen by **Dalcar** -> taken by the **Zhentarim** -> double-crossed by **Yalah Gralhund**

A **teleportation circle** carved into the guest room floor is discovered, described as being "for the master" -- later revealed to be [[Manshoon]].

### The Nimblewright and the Gralhund Crypt (S01E13)

While the city watch investigates the villa bloodbath, [[Yalah Gralhund]] secretly dispatches a **[[Nimblewright]]** -- a mechanical construct -- carrying the [[Stone of Golorr|Stone of Galore]] and a handwritten note. The note turns out to be a **map of the [[City of the Dead]]** pointing to the **[[Gralhund Crypt]]**.

SWOJ pursues the nimblewright to the crypt during the Feast of the Moon festival. In the lower level, they find:

- The nimblewright **destroyed**
- **[[Zylka|Zilka]]** impaled through the heart with a sword, dying

Before death, Zilka draws the **symbol of Asmodeus** on the ground and points upward -- warning of the cultists above. On the upper level, a **pentagram**, two cultists, and a pair of **spined devils** are found performing a handoff of the Stone. A frantic rooftop chase across the City of the Dead ensues, leading ultimately to [[Willifort Crowell]], the Cassalanter majordomo, who receives the Stone from a devil.

This episode marks the final appearance of Zilka in her original form and seals the Gralhund family's connection to the Cult of Asmodeus.

### The Teleportation Circle and the Kolat Towers (S01E22)

Months later, SWOJ returns to [[Gralhund Villa]] -- now damaged and partially abandoned -- to use the **teleportation circle** in the guest room. The passphrase *"Tuatha Erinji"* activates the circle and transports them to the [[Kolat Towers]], the [[Zhentarim]]'s base of operations and the gateway to the Necromancer's extra-dimensional sanctum. The circle's purpose is now clear: it was a direct link between the Gralhund household and [[Manshoon]].

### The Clone Revelation (S01E22-S01E23)

The most dramatic twist in the Gralhund family's story unfolds inside the [[Kolat Towers]]. Behind an illusory wall in a construct workshop, SWOJ discovers the real **[[Zylka]]** -- the original person -- **shackled and forced to work on [[Nim]]**, the Gralhund family golem.

The Necromancer (later revealed to be [[Manshoon]]) confronts SWOJ and delivers the devastating truth:

**Ylka is a clone of Zylka, created by their grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund]] to protect Zylka's soul from some unnamed threat.**

This revelation redefines Ylka's entire identity. She is not a Gralhund by birth -- she is an arcane duplicate, brought into existence through Mordecai's forbidden magic. The discovery forces Ylka to grapple with questions of autonomy, purpose, and selfhood that drive her character arc for the rest of the campaign.

SWOJ frees Zylka from captivity. The Kolat Towers are destroyed by explosives shortly after.

### The Gralhund Arrests and Ylka's Inheritance (S01E29)

In the aftermath of the Cassalanter trial, the Gralhunds are **arrested for their cult activities** and alliance with the Cassalanters. Ylka receives **keys to [[Gralhund Villa]]** and uses it as a personal retreat. [[Zylka]] also resides at the villa, described as a reanimated being whose body had been given rings to hide her undead aura -- she had been used as an agent for the Gralhunds until SWOJ intervened. By this point, Zylka is learning to play the concertina and developing independence.

Ylka studies the **Book of Shadows** recovered from the villa and learns that Zylka was **reanimated by the Gralhunds without a soul**, used as a puppet agent for the family's schemes.

### Mordecai's Soul-Binding Ritual (S02E38)

The Gralhund legacy reaches its resolution in the Season 2 finale. [[Mordecai Gralhund]]'s greatest invention -- the **soul-binding ritual** -- is performed by [[Antediluvian Jake]] and [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap|Angie Fliplap]] at the God's Night celebration at [[Trollskull Manor]]. Jake binds his soul to his obsidian tablet, ending his undead immortality, using the very ritual Mordecai created.

Ylka performs her grandfather's music at the God's Night fire, completing a chapter of the Gralhund family story. She resolves to **investigate the Gralhund family's dark history** and Mordecai's legacy going forward -- a thread left open for future stories.

## Key Locations

- **[[Gralhund Villa]]** -- The family's two-story estate at the end of Del Zorn Street, near Trollskull Alley. Contains a carriage house, servants' quarters, great hall, library with paintings of the Gralhund lineage, Lord Orond's study (with falcon), master bedroom, and a guest room with a teleportation circle linked to the [[Kolat Towers]]. Scene of the pitched battle between Zhentarim and Gralhund forces (S01E12). The Gralhunds were eventually arrested and Ylka inherited the keys.
- **[[Gralhund Library]]** -- A high-ceilinged, wood-paneled room inside the villa. Contains sliding ladders, reading furniture, paintings of the Gralhund lineage (some faces scratched out), a lectern with a locked book guarded by an imp disguised as a tarantula, and stolen by [[Mr. E]] during the raid.
- **[[Gralhund Crypt]]** -- The family crypt in the [[City of the Dead]]. Lower level: the destroyed nimblewright and dying Zilka. Upper level: a pentagram, two cultists, and spined devils performing a handoff of the Stone of Galore (S01E13).
- **[[Fields of Glory]]** -- Cemetery or memorial grounds connected to the Gralhund crypts where the [[Cult of Asmodeus]] held meetings (S01E10).
- **[[Trollskull Manor]]** -- Mordecai Gralhund's old lab is in a walled-off section of the basement. His Song Sword was found in [[Lif]]'s hidden chest in the cellar.

## Alliances and Rivalries

### The Zhentarim

The Gralhunds bankrolled the [[Zhentarim]] (Black Network) for months, with a teleportation circle in their guest room built explicitly "for the master" -- [[Manshoon]], the Zhentarim's founder. Lord [[Orond Gralhund]] served as a financial patron while the Zhentarim provided muscle and connections. This alliance collapsed when Lady [[Yalah Gralhund]] **double-crossed the Zhentarim** by seizing the [[Stone of Golorr]] for herself, triggering the brutal battle at the villa that left dead servants in the pantry and Zhentarim and Gralhund agents at each other's throats.

### The Cassalanters and the Cult of Asmodeus

The Gralhunds' alliance with the [[Cassalanter Family]] was mediated by their shared devotion to [[Asmodeus]], Lord of Nessus. [[Victoro Cassalanter]]'s letter found in Orond's study -- signed "V" and referencing "the Lord of Nessus" -- confirmed the families were operating together under the [[Cult of Asmodeus]]. The Gralhunds deployed [[Zylka|Zilka]] as an agent at the Cassalanter party, and [[Yalah Gralhund]] funneled the Stone of Galore to cultists at the [[Gralhund Crypt]], where spined devils received it for handoff to the Cassalanter majordomo [[Willifort Crowell]].

### The Necromancer (Manshoon)

[[Manshoon]], operating as "the Necromancer," had deep ties to the Gralhund family through [[Mordecai Gralhund]]. The Necromancer possessed a mechanical hand and knew the secrets of Mordecai's clone magic, holding [[Zylka]] captive in the [[Kolat Towers]] to work on the golem [[Nim]]. The teleportation circle in the Gralhund guest room connected directly to the Kolat Towers, making the villa a waypoint in Manshoon's network.

### Bregan D'aerthe

[[Jarlaxle]]'s drow mercenary organization assisted SWOJ in infiltrating [[Gralhund Villa]] by sending [[Mr. E]], who lockpicked the servants' entrance, stole the silver key from Yalah's neck, and escaped with the locked book from the library.

## The Gralhund Clone Mystery

The Gralhund family's darkest secret is the **clone conspiracy** engineered by patriarch [[Mordecai Gralhund]]:

1. **Mordecai** was a wizard who turned against his moral code and became involved with demons and forbidden magic. He invented a **soul-binding ritual** and experimented with clone magic.
2. **[[Zylka]]** was the original Gralhund heir. For reasons not fully explained, Mordecai created **[[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]** as a clone of Zylka to "protect her soul from some unnamed threat."
3. Mordecai placed Ylka into a **hundred-year death-sleep**. When she was resurrected at the start of the campaign, she had fragmented memories and no understanding of her true nature.
4. Mordecai's **disembodied soul** resided inside Ylka's concertina, whispering guidance and warnings.
5. The real Zylka was later **reanimated without a soul** and used as an agent by the Gralhunds -- sent to the Cassalanter party, captured, and later found dying in the Gralhund Crypt.
6. Zylka was then found **shackled in the Kolat Towers**, forced to work on [[Nim]] (the Gralhund golem) by the Necromancer.
7. After SWOJ freed her, Zylka took up residence at the villa and began learning the concertina, slowly developing her own independent identity.

The full truth of why Mordecai created the clone and what threat he was protecting Zylka from remains an **unresolved mystery** -- one Ylka resolved to investigate in the Season 2 epilogue.

- **Song Sword** -- A blue steel short sword with a gemstone pommel and brass plaque inscribed "M.G." for Mordecai Gralhund. Cold to the touch, deals cold damage, and plays a melody when struck. Found in [[Lif]]'s hidden chest at [[Trollskull Manor]]. Became [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]'s signature weapon.
- **[[Gralhund Crest Heater Shield]]** -- A shield emblazoned with the Gralhund family crest, found in the [[The Bag Dimension|Bag Dimension]]'s armor bag. The pointy bottom can be used as a weapon for 1d4 damage (S02E13).
- **[[Victoro's Letter|Victoro's Letter]]** -- Gilded paper with red ink from [[Victoro Cassalanter]], confirming the Gralhund-Cassalanter-Asmodeus alliance and Zilka's role as a Gralhund agent.
- **[[Gralhund Locked Book]]** -- A huge, beautifully bound book with metal binding and a keyhole lock, guarded by an imp in the [[Gralhund Library]]. Stolen by [[Mr. E]].
- **[[Silver Key]]** -- A silver key on a thin chain worn by [[Yalah Gralhund]]. Stolen by [[Mr. E]]; likely opens the locked book.
- **[[Gralhund Note/Map]]** -- Handwritten note sent by Yalah with the nimblewright, revealed to be a map of the [[City of the Dead]] showing the [[Gralhund Crypt]] location.
- **Ylka's Concertina** -- A battered squeeze-box containing [[Mordecai Gralhund]]'s disembodied soul. Stolen by [[Sixteen String Jack]] of the [[Jolly Gents]] and recovered by Ylka at the [[Neverwinter Chasm]].
- **Rapier (Ouroboros)** -- [[Zylka|Zilka]]'s magical rapier with an infinity symbol on the hilt, wielded during the Cassalanter party attack.
