# Silver Waves of Justice (SWOJ)

The **Silver Waves of Justice** -- commonly known as **SWOJ** -- is the adventuring party at the heart of the Twin Portals campaign. Founded in [[Waterdeep]] in the autumn of 1492 DR, SWOJ began as four strangers brought together by a simple missing-persons job at the [[Yawning Portal]] tavern and grew into one of the most celebrated adventuring companies on the [[Sword Coast]]. Over the course of two seasons and sixty-eight episodes, the Silver Waves dismantled the [[Xanathar Guild]]'s grip on [[Waterdeep]]'s underworld, exposed the [[Cassalanters]]' conspiracy, survived death and resurrection, toppled the [[Zhentarim]] warlord [[Manshoon]], shattered the [[Wall Of The Faithless]], and earned an official guild charter from the [[Sword Coast Alliance]]. Their home base is [[Trollskull Manor]] in [[Waterdeep]]'s [[North Ward]], where they operate **The Short Rest** tavern. The party was named by [[Scoot Sparkles]] in [[S01E04 - Episode Four|S01E04]], combining his love of the sea with the group's commitment to doing the right thing.

## Origin

On the 21st of Marpenoth, 1492 DR, four strangers gathered at the [[Yawning Portal]] tavern in [[Waterdeep]]'s [[Castle Ward]]. [[Scoot Sparkles]] and [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] were childhood friends living at the [[The Splintered Stair]] in the [[Dock Ward]]. [[Ylka Gralhund]] was a recently resurrected human bard performing at the tavern with her concertina. [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] was a lightfoot halfling druid who had arrived in [[Waterdeep]] through a mysterious portal into the [[City Of The Dead]] with no memory of her past.

A bar fight erupted at the Yawning Portal, escalating into a [[Zhentarim]]-versus-[[Xanathar Guild]] skirmish that spilled through the tavern doors. After helping barkeep [[Durnan]] restore order, the four were approached by the famous chronicler [[Volothamp Geddarm|Volo]], who hired them to find his missing friend [[Floon Blagmaar]]. That first job -- tracking Floon through the [[Dock Ward]], infiltrating a [[Zhentarim]] warehouse on [[Candle Lane]], and descending into the [[Xanathar Guild]] sewers -- forged the bonds that would hold the party together for years to come.

As payment for rescuing Floon and [[Renaer Neverember]], Volo gave the group the deed to a rundown property on [[Trollskull Alley]]: [[Trollskull Manor]]. The four adventurers renovated the building, befriended its resident ghost [[Lif Lindgren|Lif]], and opened it as a tavern called **The Short Rest** -- their headquarters for the rest of the campaign.

## Name Origin

The name "Silver Waves of Justice" was coined by [[Scoot Sparkles]] in [[S01E04 - Episode Four|S01E04]], shortly after the party acquired [[Trollskull Manor]]. Scoot -- a sailor by background and the son of a [[Waterdeep Military]] father lost at sea -- combined his connection to the ocean ("Silver Waves") with the group's desire to protect the innocent ("of Justice"). The name stuck immediately. The party would go on to use the abbreviation **SWOJ** throughout both seasons, and it became famous enough across the [[Sword Coast]] that the [[Sword Coast Alliance]] formally chartered it as a guild name in [[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]].

## History

### Season 1: Waterdeep

#### The Search for Floon and the Stone of Golorr

What began as a simple rescue mission quickly drew SWOJ into the tangled underworld politics of [[Waterdeep]]. After rescuing [[Floon Blagmaar]] from the [[Xanathar Guild]] sewers, the party found themselves pursuing the [[The Stone of Golorr]] -- an ancient artifact connected to a vast hoard of gold hidden beneath the city by the deposed Open Lord [[Lord Dagult Neverember|Dagult Neverember]]. The hunt for the Stone brought SWOJ into conflict with the city's most dangerous factions: the [[Zhentarim]], the [[Xanathar Guild]], and the [[Cassalanters]] family. Scoot recovered the Stone itself and experienced a vision of a dolphin swimming alongside a vast tentacle monster beneath the waves. Xae had her own prehistoric vision of pulling a tentacle beast from a river and crushing it into what would become the Stone -- a hint at her deep and ancient connection to [[Waterdeep]]'s history.

#### Trollskull Manor and The Short Rest

SWOJ renovated [[Trollskull Manor]] with the help of local guilds, cleared out a hag named [[Esther]] from the attic, and befriended the ghost [[Lif Lindgren|Lif]]. The tavern opened as **The Short Rest** in [[S01E11 - Episode Eleven|S01E11]], with a grand opening party attended by [[Jarlaxle|Zardoz Zord]], the [[Doom Raiders]], and much of the neighborhood. The celebration was cut tragically short by a devastating fireball explosion on [[Trollskull Alley]] that killed more than ten people.

#### The Xanathar Heist and Lark's Death

SWOJ infiltrated the [[Xanathar's Lair]] to end the beholder crime lord's reign of terror. During the assault, [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] delivered the killing blow to the [[The Xanathar]] with a thrown dagger. But victory came at a terrible cost: [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] was disintegrated by the Xanathar's eye rays, his last words being "For justice." [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] tackled the mind flayer [[Nihiloor]] into a spawning pool in grief-fueled rage. [[Thaelin]] was killed in the lair's collapse. Lark's death was the defining trauma of the party -- and refusing to accept his loss sent SWOJ on a journey that would reshape all of them.

#### The Feywild, Malar's Realm, and Resurrection

SWOJ tracked Lark's soul to [[The Feywild]] and then into the divine realm of [[Malar]], the Beast Lord, where they found his soul trapped inside his hat. They brought him back to [[Waterdeep]], where [[Manshoon|the Necromancer]] in the [[Kolat Towers]] performed a clone spell to resurrect him. Unknown to the party, Manshoon planted a [[Gem of Seeing]] in Lark's temple during the process, using it to spy on SWOJ's movements. Ylka discovered during this same visit that she was a clone of [[Zylka]], created by her grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund]].

#### The Cassalanter Conspiracy and the Vault of Dragons

SWOJ exposed the [[Cassalanters]]' dark dealings, with Ylka delivering a legendary speech at their trial. The party tracked down the [[Vault Of Dragons]] and the hoard of [[Lord Dagult Neverember|Neverember]]'s gold. In a characteristically selfless act, Scoot shattered the party's [[Bag of Holding]] to scatter the gold across [[Waterdeep]] rather than let it fall into the wrong hands. Season 1 ended with [[Manshoon]]'s surveillance of the party revealed, the Gem of Seeing discovered in Lark's temple, and SWOJ established as heroes of [[Waterdeep]].

### Season 2: Beyond Waterdeep

#### The Kryptwood and Amphail

SWOJ ventured north to the [[Sword Mountains]] and the town of [[Amphail]] to investigate the mysterious [[Kryptwood]] -- a forest where [[Arcturia]]'s transmutation magic had been transforming people into beasts. Scoot discovered his former shipmate [[Leah]] had been turned into a six-eyed creature. Ylka smashed Arcturia's enchanted mirror with her [[Song Sword]], breaking the spell and freeing dozens of captives. Xae learned that [[Arcturia]] was her sister, hearing the telepathic message: "Follow me. Break the wall."

#### The Radiant Citadel and the Concord Jewel

Aboard the [[Concord Jewel]], SWOJ traveled to the [[Radiant Citadel]] in the Deep Ethereal Plane -- a floating city of displaced civilizations. There they gathered allies, competed in the Battle Prawns tournament at [[Sid Squid]]'s, and investigated the connections between [[Arcturia]], [[Halaster Blackcloak]], and the wider conspiracy orchestrated by [[Manshoon]].

#### The Jolly Gents Betrayal and Death

The legendary adventuring company the [[Jolly Gents]] turned out to be under the control of [[Rowan Crooks]] -- a [[Manshoon]] clone posing as their leader. Crooks killed SWOJ, but they were resurrected in new bodies in the [[The Bag Dimension]], where [[Fioleth]] provided them with new signature weapons: Scoot received a steampunk anchor pipe maul, Ylka claimed a marching marimba and whip, Lark got a double-ended silver dagger, and Xae received matching red-stained hand crossbows. [[Antediluvian Jake]] secretly passed Lark an obsidian tablet, whispering "we're all prisoners of Crooks" -- reframing the Jolly Gents as victims rather than villains.

#### Zhentil Keep and the Shadowfell

SWOJ infiltrated [[Zhentil Keep]] in the [[Shadowfell]] -- [[Rowan Crooks]]'s fortress -- fighting through trapped hallways, shadow thread snares, and soul armor guardians. Lark won a staff sparring match against [[Ann Jane Flip-Flap]] on the Bridge of Destiny. The party recovered their stolen gear from Crooks's vault and forged alliances with Jolly Gents members who had broken free of his control.

#### The Siege of the Radiant Citadel

The campaign's climactic arc saw SWOJ return to the [[Radiant Citadel]] for a multi-episode battle against [[Manshoon]] and his forces. Ylka used the [[Mask of Dragon Kind]] to summon allied dragons, cast Shatter on a dracolich phylactery, and polymorphed into a Tyrannosaurus Rex. Xae reactivated the [[Concord Jewel]]s to send displaced civilizations home and activated the protective ward over the Citadel. Scoot communed with the [[Raven Queen]], who sent a massive flock of spectral ravens to banish a two-headed dracolich. Lark freed [[Squidly]], [[Urstul Floxxin|Erstel Floxon]], and the [[Doom Raiders]] from magical restraints.

#### The Wall of the Faithless and Manshoon's Defeat

At the [[Wall Of The Faithless]], SWOJ confronted [[Arcturia]] and persuaded her to join their cause. Arcturia led the party to [[Kelemvor]]'s skeleton and raised an army of ancient souls from the Wall. Scoot survived Arcturia's disintegrate spell through sheer toughness. Ylka polymorphed Scoot into a giant ape to fight [[Manshoon]] directly. Xae conjured woodpeckers to shatter soul crystals. Aboard the [[Concord Jewel]], the party defeated Manshoon in a desperate running battle, and Scoot destroyed the navigation console to escape the [[The Bag Dimension]] and an approaching ancient red dragon. Ylka was still playing music with her last three hit points as the ship crash-landed in the [[Spiral Cavern]] to cheering crowds.

## The Short Rest

[[Trollskull Manor]], SWOJ's headquarters, doubles as a tavern called **The Short Rest** on [[Trollskull Alley]] in [[Waterdeep]]'s [[North Ward]]. Originally a dilapidated, haunted townhouse given to the party by [[Volothamp Geddarm|Volo]], the manor was renovated by SWOJ and opened for business in [[S01E11 - Episode Eleven|S01E11]]. The ghost [[Lif Lindgren|Lif]] serves as the spectral attendant, and [[Tagga]] (Scoot's mother) manages the bar alongside staff including [[Nat]], [[Jenks]], and [[Pog Waggletop]]. The building features a taproom, upper-floor living quarters, a tower, an attic, and a deep cellar containing a scrying pool and a teleportation circle completed by [[Ulkoria Stonemarrow]]. The elder gold dragon [[Aurinax]] resides there in dwarven form, and [[Zelifarn]] visits rooftop dragon roosts. During SWOJ's extended absence in the [[The Shadowfell|Shadowfell]], Lif and Tagga kept the tavern running.

## Legacy

By the end of Season 2, SWOJ had transformed from four strangers at a tavern into legends of the [[Sword Coast]]. Their deeds -- slaying the [[The Xanathar|Xanathar]], shattering the [[Wall Of The Faithless]], defeating [[Manshoon]], and saving the [[Radiant Citadel]] -- earned them formal recognition from the governments of [[Waterdeep]], [[Baldur's Gate]], [[Neverwinter]], [[Luskan]], and the wider [[Sword Coast Alliance]].

In [[S02E38 - Episode Sixty Eight - Season Two Finale (S02E38)|S02E38]], [[Ramalia Haventree]] of the [[Harpers]] presented SWOJ with the [[Letters Of Charter (Guild Charter)|Letters of Charter]], officially establishing the **[[Guild Of The Silver Waves Of Justice]]**. The charter grants recruitment rights, petitioning privileges, safe passage, limited judicial immunity, and franchising rights across the allied cities of the [[Sword Coast]]. [[Terry]] was invited as the first official member beyond the four founders.

At the God's Night celebration on the rooftop of [[Trollskull Manor]], each member burned a meaningful item in a fire lit by [[Aurinax]]'s breath: Scoot burned [[Rowan Crooks]] trading cards, Lark burned his [[Jolly Gents]] outfit, Xae threw in a [[The Feywild|Feywild]] mushroom, and Ylka played her grandfather [[Mordecai Gralhund|Mordecai]]'s music. Their individual paths forward reflect the breadth of their shared journey: Scoot chose to stay grounded at Trollskull Manor, Ylka resolved to investigate the [[Gralhund]] family's dark history, Xae set her sights on [[Undermountain]] to free her remaining siblings, and Lark vowed to keep adventuring and recruiting for the guild.

The season ended with twin portals appearing on the Trollskull rooftop -- one green, releasing an aboleth tendril, and one rose-gold, through which [[Laeral Silverhand]] arrived riding the unicorn [[Lyrian]]. The multiverse was calling. The Silver Waves of Justice answered.
