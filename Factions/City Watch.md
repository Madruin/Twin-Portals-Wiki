# City Watch

The City Watch is [[Waterdeep]]'s official law enforcement body, responsible for maintaining order across the city's wards. Throughout Season 1, the Watch served as both ally and obstacle to [[SWOJ]], with individual officers ranging from genuinely sympathetic to deeply corrupt -- reflecting the wider political rot caused by the [[Cassalanters]]' influence over Waterdeep's institutions.

**First appearance:** [[S01E01 - Episode One]]
**Total appearances:** 12+ episodes

---

## Overview

The City Watch patrols Waterdeep's streets, investigates crimes, and enforces the Code Legal. Each ward maintains its own watch post and garrison under a local captain. During the events of Season 1, the Watch was struggling to contain an escalating wave of gang violence between the [[Zhentarim (Zhentarim)|Zhentarim]] and the [[Xanathar Guild]] in the [[Dock Ward]] and [[Trade Ward]]. This institutional strain made the Watch vulnerable to manipulation by powerful noble families -- most critically the Cassalanters, who placed agents and bought off magistrates to bend the city's justice system to their will.

By the end of Season 1, [[Ulkoria Stonemarrow]] led a purge of Cassalanter loyalists from the Watch's ranks, and the organization began rebuilding its integrity. In Season 2, the Watch continued to operate during wartime conditions, though in a diminished capacity.

## Key Members

### [[Captain Hyustus Stagget]]
Captain of the [[Dock Ward]] garrison. Stagget was the Watch officer SWOJ interacted with most frequently during Season 1. He first appeared responding to violence at the [[Zhentarim Warehouse]] on [[Candle Lane]] with approximately thirteen men in tactical formation ([[S01E02 - Episode Two|S01E02]]). Recognizing [[Renaer Neverember]] and [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]]'s Wavesilver signet, he stood down and let the party leave. He later advised the party to register their magic users with the [[Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors]] ([[S01E05 - Episode Five|S01E05]]).

Stagget arrested SWOJ after the High Road cart chase on the night of the Feast of the Moon ([[S01E13 - Episode Thirteen|S01E13]]), but privately warned them at the [[Docks Ward Guardhouse]] that the magistrate assigned to their morning trial had been bought off by the Cassalanters. This act of conscience cost him his life -- he was murdered (bludgeoned to death) in the prison basement, and the doppelganger [[Willifort Crowell (Doppelganger)|Willaford Crowell]] assumed his identity ([[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]).

**Status:** Deceased (murdered by Cassalanter agents).

### [[Sergeant Crowley]]
A Watch sergeant who assisted Magistrate [[Ulkoria Stonemarrow]] in investigating the fireball explosion at [[Trollskull Alley]] ([[S01E11 - Episode Eleven|S01E11]]).

### [[Bartolo]]
A Watch guard who read the formal charges against SWOJ at their trial before [[Laeral Silverhand]] in the [[Docks Ward Guardhouse]] courtroom ([[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]).

### [[Ulkoria Stonemarrow]]
A dwarf magister from the [[Watchful Order of Magists and Protectors]] who also served in a law enforcement capacity. She initially appeared at [[Trollskull Manor]] with her shield guardian Winston, carving a glyph of warding on the front door and registering [[Xaerine Rumblestride|Xae]] and [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] as magic users ([[S01E05 - Episode Five|S01E05]]). In the Season 1 finale, she coordinated the Watch's cleanup operation at the [[Cassalanter Villa]] with a contingent she personally vouched for as trustworthy. She promised to sweep the entire City Watch for remaining Cassalanter agents ([[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]).

### [[Gorg Fallowfoot]]
A Watch member mentioned in Season 2 in the context of Waterdeep's wartime state ([[S02E05 - Episode Thirty Five (S02E05)|S02E05]]).

### [[Bricks And Bracks]]
Guardsmen who served as [[Remalia Haventree]]'s personal guards and were affiliated with both the [[Harpers]] and the City Watch. They were ensorcelled by the [[Stone of Golorr]] ([[S01E26 - Episode Twenty Six|S01E26]]) and later ensorcelled again by the Cassalanters, betraying Remalia by turning her over to the Watch after the theater battle ([[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]]). After the Cassalanter downfall, they were exposed as traitors on the Cassalanter payroll and went on the run ([[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]).

### [[Victoro Cassalanter]]
The Cassalanter matriarch who wielded political influence over the Watch. After the battle against the [[The Xanathar (Beholder)|Xanathar]] at the [[Zadio City Music Hall]], she personally led a city watch army to arrest SWOJ ([[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]]).

## Cassalanter Corruption

The Cassalanter family's corruption of the City Watch was one of the central political threads of Season 1. Their influence operated on multiple levels:

- **Judicial corruption:** The Cassalanters bought off the magistrate originally assigned to preside over SWOJ's trial. Captain Stagget discovered this and warned SWOJ, which led to his murder ([[S01E13 - Episode Thirteen|S01E13]], [[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]).
- **Doppelganger infiltration:** [[Willifort Crowell (Doppelganger)|Willaford Crowell]], the Cassalanter majordomo and a doppelganger, impersonated Captain Stagget inside the [[Docks Ward Guardhouse]] after killing him. He was exposed at the trial when he could not read the original Gralhund note that the real Stagget had already read aloud. His skin turned gray and his features stretched as his disguise failed under stress ([[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]).
- **Political control:** [[Victoro Cassalanter]] marshaled an entire Watch army to arrest SWOJ after they defeated the Xanathar, using institutional authority to suppress the heroes who had just saved the city ([[S01E27 - Episode Twenty Seven|S01E27]]).
- **Turned agents:** [[Bricks And Bracks]] were ensorcelled by the Cassalanters and betrayed [[Remalia Haventree]] to the Watch. Ledgers later recovered from the Cassalanter Villa revealed that many Watch members had been on the Cassalanter payroll ([[S01E29 - Episode Twenty Nine|S01E29]]).

The corruption was finally purged after SWOJ exposed the Cassalanters' deal with [[Asmodeus]] and their devil-summoning at the Founders Day feast. [[Ulkoria Stonemarrow]] led the cleanup with a personally vetted contingent and promised a full internal sweep of the Watch.

## The Arrest and Trial of SWOJ

The City Watch's most dramatic involvement with SWOJ came in the arc spanning [[S01E13 - Episode Thirteen|S01E13]] through [[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]:

1. **The High Road Chase** ([[S01E13 - Episode Thirteen|S01E13]]): After SWOJ pursued [[Willifort Crowell]] and a spined devil carrying the [[Stone of Golorr]] in a cart chase down the [[High Road]] during the Feast of the Moon parade, [[Captain Hyustus Stagget]] arrested the entire party. He took them to the [[Docks Ward Guardhouse]] and privately warned them the presiding magistrate was a Cassalanter puppet.

2. **The Prison Break** ([[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]): Overnight, [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] picked the cell lock using [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]]'s concertina repair kit. In the prison basement, they discovered two dead city watchmen -- bludgeoned to death -- and the doppelganger Willaford Crowell posing as Captain Stagget. [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] used Shatter to blow through a locked door, but the guards overwhelmed and re-arrested the party.

3. **The Trial** ([[S01E15 - Episode Fifteen|S01E15]]): [[Laeral Silverhand]], the Open Lord of Waterdeep, replaced the corrupt judge as presiding magistrate. The charges were extensive: crimes against nobles (the Gralhunds), crimes against the city (property destruction), crimes against the gods (tomb robbing in the [[City Of The Dead]]), and crimes against citizens. [[Bartolo]] read the charges. Witnesses including [[Zardoz Zord]], [[Flo]], and [[Tagga]] testified in SWOJ's defense. The doppelganger was exposed, courtroom chaos erupted with mind-controlled spectators harboring [[Intellect Devourer|intellect devourers]], and Laeral demonstrated her immense power before freeing SWOJ and taking personal command of the investigation.
