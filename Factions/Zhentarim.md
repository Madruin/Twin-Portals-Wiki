# Zhentarim

The Zhentarim, also known as the **Black Network**, is a powerful criminal organization and one of the central antagonist factions throughout the Twin Portals campaign. Founded by the ancient wizard [[Manshoon]], the Zhentarim's agents are identified by their black leather armor and the sigil of a **winged snake**. Over the course of the story, the organization evolves from a street-level criminal syndicate warring over Waterdeep's underworld to a continent-spanning military threat under Manshoon's direct command, before ultimately being reformed under new leadership after his defeat.

**First appearance:** [[S01E01 - Episode One|S01E01]]
**Total appearances:** 20+ episodes across both seasons
**Also known as:** The Black Network, the Zentarim, the Zents

---

## Overview

The Zhentarim operate as a shadowy mercantile and criminal network with fingers in smuggling, extortion, assassination, and political manipulation. In Waterdeep, their operations initially center on the **Candle Lane warehouse** in the Dock Ward, and later the **[[Kolat Towers]]**, a pair of wizard towers that serve as the base for the Necromancer's operations and house a portal to his extra-dimensional sanctum.

The organization is not monolithic. Two competing factions operate within the Zhentarim during the events of the campaign:

- **Manshoon's Division** -- The dominant, aggressive wing led by the founder himself (operating through clones and simulacra). This faction pursues power at any cost and commands assassins, soldiers, barbarian allies, dragon cultists, and undead forces.
- **The [[Doom Raiders]]** -- A moderate subfaction led by [[Davil Starsong]] that opposes Manshoon's extremism and eventually becomes an ally of [[Scoot Sparkles|SWOJ]].

## History in the Campaign

### Season 1: The Gang War and the Stone of Golorr

#### The Yawning Portal Ambush (S01E01)

The Zhentarim first appear in the campaign's opening episode. On the 21st of Marpenoth, seven agents in black leather armor arrive on a dray (a two-story cart) outside the [[Yawning Portal]] tavern and launch a drive-by crossbow ambush against a group of tattooed thugs, part of the escalating gang violence between the Zhentarim and the [[Xanathar Guild]] that has been plaguing Waterdeep's Dock Ward and Trade Ward. The party -- Scoot, Lark, Ylka, and Xae -- help defend the tavern alongside the barkeep [[Durnan]].

Following the attack, the Harper agent [[Jalaster]] directs the party to **[[Candle Lane]]**, where the Zhentarim have been operating out of a warehouse marked with their winged snake sigil.

#### The Candle Lane Warehouse (S01E01-S01E02)

Inside the Zhentarim warehouse, the party discovers three fresh corpses in black leather armor, followed by twelve additional bodies from both factions -- Zhentarim agents and Xanathar Guild thugs who had fought each other to the death. The warehouse is a two-story space with a balcony overlooking a ground-level storage area filled with crates and barrels.

[[Renaer Neverember]] is found hiding in a closet beneath the staircase. He explains that the **Zhentarim kidnapped him and [[Floon Blagmaar]] from [[Fillet Lane]]** after they left the Skewered Dragon tavern. The Xanathar Guild then attacked the warehouse and took Floon, mistaking him for Renaer. Both factions were searching for the **[[The Stone of Golorr]]**, an artifact needed to locate the massive hoard of gold that [[Dagult Neverember]] had embezzled and hidden within the city.

#### The Gralhund Connection (S01E11-S01E13)

The Zhentarim's entanglement with the noble [[Gralhund Family]] is a critical thread. The Gralhunds had been **bankrolling the Zhentarim for months**, but Lady [[Yalah Gralhund]] ultimately double-crossed them.

After the devastating **fireball explosion** at Trollskull Alley in S01E11, which killed the gnome spy [[Dalekar|Dalcar]] (hired by Dagult Neverember to track the Stone), a dark figure in black leather armor -- a Zhentarim agent -- was seen kneeling over Dalcar's body and taking something before fleeing south. The Trollskull Alley Irregulars tracked this burned Zhentarim agent to [[Gralhund Villa]], where [[Hrabbaz]], the Gralhunds' half-orc bodyguard, took him inside through the back door, wounding [[Squidly]] in the process.

In S01E12, SWOJ infiltrates the Gralhund Villa and discovers a scene of carnage:

- **Dead servants in the pantry** -- the Zhentarim had been murdering Gralhund household staff
- A pitched battle between Gralhund guards in purple and silver livery and Zhentarim agents in black leather armor
- SWOJ fights and kills two Zhentarim thugs in the great hall, armed with maces and crossbows, and four more upstairs
- The burned Zhentarim leader corners [[Orond Gralhund]] in a guest room and **slashes his throat** (Ylka stabilizes him)
- A **teleportation circle** is found carved into the floor of the guest room, "for the master" (Manshoon) to use
- A **flying snake** in a cage -- the Zhentarim agent uses it to send the message: *"G's have the stone, send reinforcements"*

Charmed by Ylka, the burned Zhentarim leader reveals the **chain of possession** of the Stone of Golorr:

> **Xanathar Guild** -> stolen by **Dalcar** -> taken by the **Zhentarim** -> double-crossed by **Yalah Gralhund**

Yalah Gralhund ultimately dispatches a [[Nimblewright|nimblewright]] with the Stone and a map leading to the Gralhund Crypt in the City of the Dead, where it passes into the hands of the Cult of Asmodeus.

#### The Doom Raiders and SWOJ's Alliance (S01E11, S01E27, S01E29)

At the [[Yawning Portal]], SWOJ first encounters [[Davil Starsong]], the leader of the **Doom Raiders**, a moderate faction within the Zhentarim. [[Bonnie]] telepathically reads Davil's mind and confirms he is searching for the Stone of Golorr. Davil flees the Short Rest's opening party when a griffin cavalry rider appears overhead.

The Doom Raiders represent the Zhentarim's less extreme wing. Davil is described as a half-elf bandleader and "grandstander" who refuses to cover his face. His crew includes [[Yagra]] (a half-orc fighter and business associate of Lark), [[Ziraj]], and [[Skeemo]].

In S01E27, the Doom Raiders agree to help SWOJ infiltrate the Cassalanter Founders Day feast by letting the party pose as members of their band. In S01E29, Davil Starsong explains the Zhentarim's internal power struggle to SWOJ and requests a **5,000 gold investment** to finance Doom Raiders operations against the Necromancer's dominant Zhentarim division. SWOJ agrees, cementing an alliance with the moderate wing.

#### The Kolat Towers (S01E22-S01E23)

The Kolat Towers serve as the Zhentarim's urban base of operations and the gateway to the Necromancer's lair. [[Remalia Haventree]] informs SWOJ that the Zentarim possess the second Eye of the Stone of Golorr and that the towers are their base.

SWOJ infiltrates the towers via the teleportation circle at Gralhund Villa (passphrase: *"Tuatha Erinji"*). Inside, they encounter **[[Urstul Floxxin]]**, a Zentarim operative, and his associate **[[Mookie]]**. The towers contain seven floors of traps, a flesh golem, summoning circles, and a portal to the Necromancer's extra-dimensional sanctum at the top, accessible via rings emblazoned with the letter "M."

[[Squidly]], the tiefling orphan from the Trollskull Irregulars, is found to have infiltrated Urstul's operation and successfully **pickpocketed the second Eye of the Stone of Golorr** from him.

The towers are destroyed at the end of S01E23 after the city watch raids the location and explosives planted in the adjoining tower detonate.

#### The Theater Battle and Manshoon Revealed (S01E26, S01E29)

When SWOJ enters the Vault of Dragons beneath the Zadio City Music Hall to recover Dagult Neverember's stolen hoard, the **Xanathar** attacks with bugbears, triggering a massive three-way battle. **Manshoon's simulacrum** appears on the theater balcony with Zhentarim lieutenants including **[[Mookie Plush]]**, armed with a crossbow. Manshoon casts **Bigby's Hand** against Scoot, but retreats after [[Remalia Haventree]]'s fireball blasts the balcony. The chandelier falls and Manshoon escapes.

In the Season 1 finale (S01E29), [[Rowan Crooks]] arrives through a Feywild portal and reveals the Necromancer's true identity: **Manshoon, the founder of the Zhentarim.** Rowan extracts a **Gem of Seeing** from Lark's temple, planted by Manshoon to spy on SWOJ ever since Lark's resurrection. This revelation sends SWOJ through the twin portals into the Feywild to begin their hunt for Manshoon.

### Season 2: Manshoon's War and the Clones

#### The Masks and Neverwinter (S02E14-S02E19)

In Season 2, the Zhentarim's influence extends far beyond Waterdeep. Operating as **"The Masks"** in Neverwinter, Manshoon's forces have infiltrated the city's politics and power structure. A Manshoon clone is believed to be operating behind a mask in Neverwinter's government. Renaer Neverember leads an underground resistance against the Masks' influence.

[[Squidly]], now a teenage Harper agent, works from the House of a Thousand Faces in Neverwinter, providing SWOJ with Masks uniforms and disguises for infiltration missions.

#### The Spiral Cavern at Amphail (S02E19)

The Zhentarim's military buildup reaches its zenith at [[Amphail]], where Manshoon has mustered a massive army inside the **[[Spiral Cavern]]** beneath the mountain. The Zhentarim assassin **Erstel Fluxen** (also known as [[Urstul Floxxin]] -- a scarred-face agent in a red hat who previously fought SWOJ at Gralhund Villa and the Kolat Towers) is captured by SWOJ after attempting to steal the Crown of Never from Castle Neverwinter. Under interrogation, Erstel reveals:

- Manshoon ("the boss with the mask" / "Nancy") operates from Amphail
- He commands the forces mustered in the Spiral Cavern
- His army includes Zhentarim soldiers, [[Reghed Barbarians|Reghed barbarians]], periton riders, and dragon cultists

SWOJ flies to Amphail on dragonback and, with help from the [[Circle Of Dusk|Circle of Dusk]] druids (Leah and Shala), infiltrates the Spiral Cavern **disguised as Zhentarim agents** during a staged distraction. Inside, they witness:

- A massive military staging operation with tents, summoning circles, and soldier encampments
- The **[[Concord Jewel]]**, a rose quartz crystal transport vessel, at the cavern's center
- **Manshoon** (the "Nancy" clone) conferring with a regal elf who then transforms into a **colossal ancient black dragon** that burns an exit hole through the mountain's ceiling
- Kobold sentries, dragon landing areas, and Zhentarim patrols throughout the surrounding forest

The party harvests crystals from the cavern outcroppings (needed to seal Neverwinter's chasm) and bluffs their way past a friendly Zhentarim guard named **[[Gorp]]**, who enjoys patchouli and is easily deceived by their cover story about carrying replacement weapons.

#### The Manshoon Clones

The mystery of Manshoon deepens across Season 2 as SWOJ encounters multiple clones:

- **Rowan Crooks** -- Revealed as the **14th clone of Manshoon**, who murdered the real Rowan Crooks long ago and assumed his identity. He leads the Jolly Gents, kills SWOJ, and steals their possessions before SWOJ is resurrected.
- **Shuni** -- A friendly Manshoon clone living inside the corpse of the Breathless Elder. Cooperates with SWOJ before being murdered by Rowan Crooks.
- **Man-Two** -- A clone made of ice and water who accompanies SWOJ into the Neverwinter chasm. Uses hypnotic pattern to neutralize guards. Destroyed by an anti-magic rune trap.
- **The Nancy Clone** -- The clone operating from Amphail, commanding the Spiral Cavern army.

Friar Truck's notes reveal the full scope of Manshoon's soul fragmentation and his plan to reunify his soul via the Wall of the Faithless, ascending to godhood.

#### The Final Battle and Manshoon's Defeat (S02E36)

The climactic confrontation with Manshoon occurs at the **[[Radiant Citadel]]** in the ethereal plane. The last surviving Manshoon clone is revealed as an **undead being wearing a half-mask and possessing a metal arm**. He has been channeling Rowan Crooks' soul pylon infrastructure to attempt godhood.

In the battle:
- Manshoon teleports around the battlefield, casting **synaptic static** and inflicting exhaustion
- Ylka polymorphs Scoot into a **giant ape** to fight him
- Manshoon desperately searches Arcturia's portable hole for Rowan Crooks' soul stone
- Lark frees **Squidly, Urstul Floxxin, and the Doom Raiders** from magical restraints at the bottom of the Citadel
- The hammerhead ship's detachable hammer section is launched into the massive soul pylon, toppling it
- **[[Kelemvor]]** himself manifests and SWOJ votes unanimously to break the Wall of the Faithless
- **Manshoon's soul is sent to the [[Raven Queen]]**, ensuring he can never return

#### The Aftermath (S02E38)

With Manshoon destroyed, the Zhentarim enters a **power vacuum** in Waterdeep. The **Doom Raiders** fill the void, becoming the new local Zhentarim leadership. By the Season 2 finale, they are performing music at the massive celebration at Trollskull Manor on God's Night during Marpenoth.

[[Simon]], Scoot's father, returns home after years lost at sea, telling of being helped by **Zhentarim-affiliated pirates** ("people sailing without official charters") and witnessing a massive divine tidal wave near Luskan. [[Tagga|Tega]], Scoot's mother, **winks knowingly** when asked about the Zhentarim, suggesting she has connections or knowledge of the organization that she has kept hidden.

## Key Locations

- **[[Zhentarim Warehouse]]** (Candle Lane, Dock Ward) -- Two-story warehouse marked with the winged snake sigil. Site of the opening Zhentarim-Xanathar battle. Fifteen total corpses found inside.
- **[[Kolat Towers]]** -- Pair of wizard towers serving as the Zentarim's urban base. Seven floors of traps and defenses, with a portal to the Necromancer's extra-dimensional sanctum at the top. Destroyed by explosives in S01E23.
- **[[Necromancer's Extra-Dimensional Sanctum]]** -- Accessed via the Kolat Towers portal. Contains an audience chamber with masks, a reading room with enchanted green flame brazier, a laboratory, a theater, and a private study with a vault containing a ledger of blackmail targets.
- **[[Gralhund Villa]]** -- Noble estate whose owners bankrolled the Zhentarim. Contains a teleportation circle in the guest room ("for the master"). Scene of a brutal battle between Zhentarim agents and Gralhund guards.
- **[[Spiral Cavern]]** (Amphail) -- A massive bucket-shaped cavern beneath the mountain where Manshoon musters his army. Contains the Concord Jewel crystal transport, summoning circles, tents, dragon landing areas, and crystal outcroppings.

## Conflicts and Rivalries

### vs. The Xanathar Guild

The Zhentarim and the [[Xanathar Guild]] are locked in a brutal gang war throughout Season 1. Their conflict drives the early plot:

- The Candle Lane warehouse massacre (S01E01-02) leaves fifteen dead from both sides
- Both factions are searching for the [[The Stone of Golorr]] to locate Neverember's hidden gold
- The Xanathar Guild employs kenku agents and operates from the sewers
- The conflict culminates in the three-way theater battle (S01E26), where the Xanathar attacks with bugbears while Manshoon's simulacrum appears on the balcony
- SWOJ ultimately kills the Xanathar itself, with Ylka's thrown dagger delivering the final blow

### Internal Power Struggle

The Zhentarim is divided between Manshoon's authoritarian main division and the moderate Doom Raiders:

- Davil Starsong openly opposes Manshoon's methods and seeks SWOJ's financial backing (5,000 gold)
- The Doom Raiders serve as SWOJ's infiltration cover for the Cassalanter feast
- By Season 2, the Doom Raiders are captured by Manshoon's forces and imprisoned at the Radiant Citadel
- After Manshoon's destruction, the Doom Raiders fill the power vacuum and become the new Zhentarim leadership in Waterdeep

---

## The Stone of Golorr

The Zhentarim's pursuit of the [[The Stone of Golorr]] is central to the Season 1 plot. The Stone is a sentient artifact that reveals the location of Dagult Neverember's embezzled gold hoard hidden in the Vault of Dragons. The Stone possesses three removable "Eyes" that were scattered among the factions:

- **First Eye:** Held by the Xanathar Guild, stolen by Dalcar, taken by the Zhentarim, then double-crossed away by Yalah Gralhund
- **Second Eye:** Held by the Zentarim at the Kolat Towers; pickpocketed by Squidly from Urstul Floxxin
- **Third Eye:** Held by the Cassalanters

## Symbols and Identification

- **Winged Snake** -- The primary Zhentarim sigil, displayed above the Candle Lane warehouse door and worn by agents
- **Black Leather Armor** -- Standard uniform for Zhentarim agents and thugs
- **"M" Rings** -- Rings emblazoned with the letter "M" (for Manshoon), required to pass through the portal at the top of the Kolat Towers
- **Flying Snake Messengers** -- A camouflaged-wing snake kept in a cage, used by agents to send coded messages (e.g., *"G's have the stone, send reinforcements"*)
