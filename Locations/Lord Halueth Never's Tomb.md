# Lord Halueth Never's Tomb

The ornate and beautifully preserved crypt of [[Lord Halueth Never]], first king of [[Neverwinter]] and disciple of [[Tyr]], located in the crypts beneath [[Castle Never]].

**Type:** tomb
**Location:** Crypts beneath [[Castle Never]]
**First appearance:** Episode S02E18
**Total appearances:** 1 episodes

## Description

The tomb lies behind a gold-scaled door in the lower crypts of [[Castle Never]], opposite [[Lady Lissandra Never's Tomb]] behind the silver-scaled door. It is richly decorated with a beautiful ornate coffin at its center, ringed by nine silver swords arranged in a sun-like circle on the ground. Golden filaments adorn the walls, and the room contains small statues, podiums, and tapestries all in immaculate condition despite their age. An inscription on a placard at the foot of the coffin reads: "Lord Halueth Never, first king of Neverwinter. Swear the oath of his knights and join him in rest."

Additional motifs throughout the room repeat the theme of reciting an oath with the knights, written in various places on the walls and fixtures. [[Lord Halueth Never]] was a disciple of [[Tyr]], the god of justice and heroism -- the gold scales of his door matching [[Tyr]]'s association with golden justice, while his wife's silver door matched [[Kelemvor]]'s neutral judgment.

## Notable Events

- During Episode S02E18, [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] opened the gold-scaled door and was the first to enter, finding the ornate crypt with the nine silver swords and inscription. He reported that he had found Lord Halueth's crypt.
- [[Scoot Sparkles|Scoot]] initially believed the [[Crown of Neverwinter]] would be in this grander tomb, trying to convince [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] to swear the oath of the knights and open the coffin. [[Malark Wavesilver|Lark]] refused, noting that "join him in rest" sounded like an invitation to die.
- The party noted that the room would require significant effort to explore properly, with multiple inscriptions to translate and the possibility of traps among the nine swords. [[Ylka Gralhund|Ylka]] suggested they check the simpler tomb first.
- Ultimately the party chose to explore [[Lady Lissandra Never's Tomb]] instead, where the [[Crown of Neverwinter]] was actually found -- the ornate tomb serving as a misdirection, or at least not the resting place of the crown.
- The tomb was never opened or further investigated by [[SWOJ]], leaving the oath of the knights and whatever secrets lie within Lord Halueth's coffin a mystery.
